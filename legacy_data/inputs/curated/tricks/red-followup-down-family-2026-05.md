## The question

**Do we canonicalize the down-family bases, or keep them deferred?** Three options:

**A.** Add `down-double-down` (ADD=4) and `double-over-down` (ADD=4) as
canonical compound bases. Existing modifiers (`spinning`, `diving`,

variant; ADD math validates against every external row.

**B.** Add `down` as a registered body modifier (+1). Existing
canonicals compose. Doesn't fully cover the "double-down" suffix family,
so most rows still need a base canonical anyway.

**C.** Keep the family deferred. All 10 rows stay external residue.
Future down-family scrapes get the same status. Per the
fewer-rows-bias principle, the safe default.

If A: do you endorse the two bases at ADD=4, with the modifier-decomposition
cascade in §C below?
