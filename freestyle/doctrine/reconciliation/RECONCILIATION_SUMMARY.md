# Source Reconciliation — Executive Summary

This is the one-page-up view of the three source-reconciliation studies that sit
beside it: `FOOTBAGMOVES.md`, `PASSBACK.md`, and `STANFORD.md`. It does not re-audit
anything. It answers five questions a maintainer should be able to ask without
reading three long documents: which sources were reconciled, what kinds of
disagreement existed, how they broke down by count, which platform principles
resolved them, and what the exercise permanently taught.

**The headline.** Across three outside lineages and well over a thousand compared
readings, exactly **one genuine contradiction** ever surfaced — Bladerunner — and it
is now resolved. Everything else was one of three benign kinds of disagreement that
the platform's standing principles were built to handle: a different notation, a
different name, or a different scoring convention. The encyclopedia did not have to
choose sides in a war of sources. It had to recognize that most of the sources were
not actually disagreeing with it.

## 1. Which sources were reconciled

| Source | What it is | Character | Overlap size | Independence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **FootbagMoves** | A community trick corpus with its own ADD numbers | A rival **scorer** — competing claims about the same structural quantity | ~130 matched ADD rows; 221-row name review | Independent lineage |
| **PassBack** | A community list recording `dex_count` per trick | A different **instrument** — counts dexterities, not structural difficulty | 68 divergent rows | Independent lineage |
| **Stanford** (Ben Lynn) | A one-character re-notation of Job's notation, over a found move list | A different **notation system** | 994 formulas; 608 matched | **Derivative** of FootbagMoves — not independent |

A fourth lineage, **footbag.org**, is the independent-structural baseline the studies
reference for corroboration weighting; it was not itself a divergence audit because
it rarely competes with the platform on structure.

## 2. What kinds of disagreement existed

Every disagreement across all three sources fell into four kinds. The first three
are not really disputes about a trick; only the fourth is.

- **Notation** — the same trick, the same structure, written in a different symbol
  system or measured on a different scale. Stanford's `Z.+1+Z` for the platform's
  `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]` (both Around the World); PassBack's dex count
  of 1 against the platform's structural 3 for a butterfly. No claim is in conflict;
  two instruments read the same object.
- **Naming** — the same trick under a different name: a folk name, a superseded
  historical name, or a structural description used as a name. FootbagMoves' "Double
  Mirage" for the platform's canonical "Barrage"; Stanford's structural primaries
  glossing the platform's folk canonicals.
- **Scoring** — a genuine competing ADD claim about the same structure. FootbagMoves'
  furious and railing cohorts running high; PassBack's three Q7-gated rows running
  low. These are the real adjudications, and they cluster tightly by operator.
- **Genuine contradiction** — a source asserting something structurally incompatible
  that none of the above explains. There was one, and it is resolved.

## 3. The breakdown by count

The overlaps are different sizes, so these are the shape of the finding, not a single
addable total. The point the numbers make is the same in every column: notation and
naming dominate, scoring is a small clustered minority, and contradiction is
effectively zero.

| Kind | FootbagMoves | PassBack | Stanford | Character |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Notation** | positional-layer (systemic) | 65 | 522 (489 equivalent + 32 collisions + 1 filler) | Not a competing claim |
| **Naming** | ~221 name-review rows | few | 139 (41 alias + 98 gloss) | Same trick, different name |
| **Scoring** | ~31 (six operator cohorts) | 3 (Q7-gated) | 118 (largely re-encoding FootbagMoves' cohorts) | Real ADD adjudications |
| **Genuine contradiction** | 1 (Bladerunner, resolved) | 0 | 0 | — |

Two observations make the scoring column smaller than it looks. First, Stanford's 118
are not 118 independent disputes: Stanford re-notated FootbagMoves' lineage, so its
ADD offsets **reproduce FootbagMoves' cohorts in the same direction** — the same
handful of operator patterns (furious, shooting, same-side, atomic stacks) counted
twice. Second, FootbagMoves' ~31 themselves reduce to roughly six recurring
operator cohorts, not thirty-one unrelated cases. The genuinely distinct scoring
disagreements across all three sources number in the low dozens and rest on a
handful of doctrine points.

## 4. Which platform principles resolved them

No disagreement required a new rule. Each was resolved by a principle already in
force.

- **Record, do not adopt** (the Charter's source-reconciliation policy). The single
  operating principle: where a source diverges, the platform publishes its own
  structural value and records the divergence in provenance or a registry. Nothing is
  silently absorbed, nothing silently overridden. This resolved every scoring
  divergence in all three sources.
- **A single source is non-authoritative for a contested value.** FootbagMoves is the
  sole source of nearly every high number; that alone makes it recorded, not adopted.
- **Weight corroboration by independent lineage, not source count.** FootbagMoves and
  Stanford agreeing is *one* doubly-encoded lineage, not two witnesses — the fact that
  Stanford's ADD offsets mirror FootbagMoves' proved this rather than merely asserting
  it.
- **Separate the instrument from the claim.** PassBack's 65 frame-difference rows and
  Stanford's 522 notation rows dissolved once it was clear they answer a different
  question (how many dexes? which symbols?) than the platform's ADD (how much
  compositional difficulty?).
- **The settled structural doctrines**, each of which resolved a whole cohort at once:
  the X-Dex receiver rule (which number of high-ADD divergences it excludes, including
  the lone 9 signal); same-side = +0 (Hurl, Barfry); direction is structural and the
  twelve core atoms are distinct (which resolved Stanford's Butterfly-versus-Reverse-
  Whirl collision without a ruling); one true name plus descriptive sub-names (which
  turned hundreds of naming divergences into aliases).
- **Bracket count equals ADD**, the mechanical backstop that keeps every published
  number self-checking.

## 5. The one contradiction, and why it mattered

Bladerunner shipped as canonical at ADD 5 with an `[XDEX]` bracket that made its
notation sum to 5 — mechanically coherent, so the bracket-count invariant did not
catch it. But the `[XDEX]` fired on a legover-family base, which the settled X-Dex
rule does not permit, and the row contradicted both its own base (`eggbeater`, 3) and
its sister (`atomic-double-leg-over`, 4). The 5 had been taken from FootbagMoves
during a promotion, against a standing internal audit that had already computed 4.
The reconciliation surfaced it; applying the settled X-Dex receiver rule resolved it
to 4. It is documented in full in `FOOTBAGMOVES.md`, Section F.

Its lesson is disproportionate to its size, and it is Lesson 4 below.

## 6. Permanent lessons

1. **Most disagreement is not disagreement.** Before adjudicating, separate the
   instrument (dex count, symbol system) from the claim (structural ADD). Three of
   the four disagreement kinds evaporate once the question each source is answering is
   named. This is the single most reusable habit the exercise produced.
2. **Source count is not evidence; independent lineage is.** Two sources that
   re-notated one list corroborate once. The reconciliation could only weight its
   evidence correctly because it traced FootbagMoves and Stanford to a shared
   ancestor. Always ask whether two agreeing sources are actually one.
3. **A settled doctrine resolves cohorts, not cases.** The X-Dex receiver rule, the
   same-side rule, and the direction-is-structural rule each disposed of whole
   families of divergence at once. Investment in a clean structural rule pays back
   across every future source.
4. **Mechanical coherence is not doctrinal coherence.** Bladerunner's brackets summed
   correctly and the row was still wrong. A self-checking invariant (bracket count =
   ADD) is necessary but not sufficient; a bracket can be individually invalid even
   when the total is right. Structural rules, not arithmetic, are the final check.
5. **Be honest about the platform's own history.** The one real contradiction was an
   internal drift — an outside number adopted against a standing audit — not an
   outside error. A reconciliation that only examines the sources and never the
   platform's own promotions will miss exactly this class.
6. **Outside sources are most valuable as lenses, not as scorers.** The lasting yield
   was not a single adopted number. It was structural information the platform did not
   encode: entry-surface dominance, multi-name folk clusters that bear on the merger
   doctrine, and enumerated valid-but-unnamed slots for the frontier. Reconcile the
   numbers, but harvest the structure.
