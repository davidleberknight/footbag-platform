# Doctrine Paper 5 — History and Testimony

> **This paper is testimony, not doctrine.** Everything below explains how the
> vocabulary actually grew. Nothing here prescribes how it must grow. A
> description of how operators proliferated is not a rule for how operators are
> admitted; that rule lives in Identity and Frontier. Do not cite this paper as
> authority for a ruling. Its value is memory, and memory is the one thing the
> other papers cannot reconstruct.

## Purpose

Record the mechanisms and eras behind forty years of freestyle innovation: why
the vocabulary expanded, why some branches flourished and others vanished, and
how naming became notation became classification became an encyclopedia. This is
the account future readers will use to understand why the system has the shape it
has — kept as history, marked as history.

## Established account (testimony, not rulings)

- **Vocabulary grew along a few recurring axes**, not by random invention: new
  operators, new sets, new surfaces, new directions, new timing, new
  combinations. The same axes that generate new tricks generated the history.
- **The naming eras are legible.** An older "toe" prefix gave way to the modern
  "quantum" naming; folk names gave way to structurally explicit readings, which
  in turn compressed back into named compounds. Blurry-to-stepping is the clearest
  case: not a correction, but conceptual evolution, with the old and new readings
  coexisting.
- **Older terminology was less precise, and some sources preserve the looser
  usage.** As the vocabulary matured it drew finer timing and role distinctions
  than the early names carried; instructional and community sources recorded at an
  earlier moment may still use the looser terms — the `-ing` operator names, or
  leggy and illusioning language — without the modern breakdown. This is
  historical usage, preserved as record: the canon follows the modern technical
  reading and preserves the older usages in provenance or alias notes where
  appropriate, but the looser usage is a real part of how the vocabulary was
  spoken. Memory, not a rule.
- **The terminals concentrated on clipper and toe.** Both ends of the vocabulary
  — where tricks enter and where they land — are dominated by these two surfaces,
  and families emerged as compositions over that concentrated base.
- **Families became the organizing concept** once the vocabulary was large enough
  that a landing signature (whirl, butterfly, osis) mattered more than any single
  compound's name.
- **Historical divergence is legitimate and permanent.** Older sources were never
  updated; conflicting notations are a real record, not errors to reconcile away.
  This is why the encyclopedia records divergence rather than flattening it.

## Open questions (oral history, not rulings)

1. **Why did some branches flourish and others disappear?** The record shows the
   survivors; the reasons are memory, not derivation.
2. **Why did operators proliferate when they did?** The sequence in which the
   operator ecosystem emerged is testimony from the people who were there.
3. **The undecidable names.** Some names cannot be reconstructed even in
   principle — the Motorfly class, carried by one source with no technical name
   and nothing else anywhere. These are recorded as attested-only and are
   distinguished, on purpose, from what can be explained. A first edition that
   promises to explain everything is dishonest about this class.
4. **Whirl versus swirl as directional variation** survives here only as an
   illustration of how a single directional distinction seeded two lineages; the
   identity ruling that makes them two tricks lives in Identity, not here.

## Worked examples

- **Toe to Quantum.** Toe Blur is Quantum Mirage; the older toe-prefixed names
  are aliases of the quantum-named canonicals. One naming era mapped onto the
  next, with the older names preserved.
- **Whirl and swirl.** One directional distinction, two families, decades of
  compounds on each. The illustration of directional evolution; the ruling is
  elsewhere.
- **The topology finding.** An analysis of the whole corpus found clipper and toe
  dominating both the entry and terminal ends, and families emerging as component
  compositions rather than as a designed taxonomy. History confirming the shape,
  not prescribing it.

## Encyclopedia impact

This is the source material for the public History page and for the "why it
exists" sections of the family and set pages. Because it is testimony, its
durability is the highest of any paper — a ruling can be re-derived, but the
memory of why the sport moved as it did cannot. It should be recorded verbatim
where possible and attributed to its sources.

## Who should review it

A panel across time, not a single authority. Red for the rules-era memory; the
archival record (the recovered footbag.org and Goldberg material) for the
documentary layer; and reachable old-schoolers for the pre-notation era. This is
oral history, so redundancy across witnesses is a feature, not duplication.
