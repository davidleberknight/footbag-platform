# Doctrine Riders — the per-row questions

## Purpose

The five formal papers settle classes of cases. These riders settle the rows the
classes leave over: one trick, one twin pair, one operator at a time. Each is too
narrow to carry a paper of its own, but each is a real unasked question that needs
a home, so the record shows what is still open and why a given row is held. They
ride to the rules expert alongside the papers, not instead of them.

A rider earns its place only when it does not reduce to a paper question. Where a
row-level question is already asked inside the Scoring or Notation paper — same
operator twice, the undefined-operator batch, the terraging arithmetic, the
cross-body rake base — it is asked there and is not restated here. The riders below
are the remainder.

Where a rider carries a registry identifier it is cited (`QUESTION_REGISTRY.csv`);
the operational riders beneath it do not yet carry one.

## Registry riders

Each of these is a row-level movement question the rules expert owns.

1. **The sumo implicit-paradox X-Dex (Q11).** Does sumo's provisional `[XDEX]`
   stand — the hypothesis that sumo carries an implicit paradox that qualifies its
   receiver as far? It decides Nuclear Ducking Mirage and its Sumo Thong twin,
   whose ADD is 5 without the X-Dex and 6 with it.
2. **The pogo-clipper terminal (Q12).** Is the pogo-clipper terminal a kick or a
   stall? Row-specific to Pogo Clipper and its same-side form. Pogo's `+1` operator
   contract is already registry-settled; only the terminal contact is open.
3. **The jani-walker structure (Q13).** What is the jani-walker structure? The same
   answer also decides Sidewinder, which reads as a fairy gyro jani-walker.
4. **Twisting and the spyro prefix (Q14).** Twisting is a degenerate structure with
   no exemplar — Toe Spinning Toe and the "What!?" name hang on it. And what does a
   spyro prefix mean, given that spyro is ruled a trick rather than a general
   modifier?

## Operational riders

These are open asks without a registry identifier. If an Emerging Vocabulary row
comes to cite one as its blocker, it is promoted into `QUESTION_REGISTRY.csv` first.

- **The general osis-suffix rule.** Is there a single rule for what an osis suffix
  contributes across the compounds that carry it? A general answer would replace
  the per-row curator path for the whole osis-suffix band.
- **The Blink path-vocabulary exception.** Blink ships promoted at 2 ADD as a
  documented notation exception: its dexterity is written with a `BACK SWIRL` path
  token that sits outside the ratified IN/OUT vocabulary. The ask is the
  path-vocabulary ruling the Notation paper's rotational-dex register defers to —
  sanction rotational path tokens as a register, or re-derive the movement in
  IN/OUT terms. Until it lands, `BACK SWIRL` stays confined to this one exception.
- **The Motorfly memory check.** One source carries the name Motorfly with no
  technical name, and nothing else appears anywhere. The ask is simply whether
  anyone remembers what it is. The History paper carries this as the
  undecidable-names class recorded as attested-only; this rider is the concrete
  memory check for the one name, and a null answer confirms the class rather than
  failing it.
- **The reported 9-ADD landing.** An account relayed to the project holds that a
  9-ADD trick was landed, but the cleanliness of the landing is disputed. The ask
  is the concrete record — which trick, roughly when, whether footage or witnesses
  exist, and the exact reason the landing is questioned — so the Frontier paper's
  ceiling note can move from anonymized testimony to a corroborated (or retired)
  entry. Until then the documented ceiling stands at 8.

## Records-only riders

These block only a world-record badge; none gates a dictionary row. Their live
tracking home is the unresolved-world-record-videos item in
`IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md`, and the letter is only their delivery vehicle, so they
are not double-tracked as dictionary gates.

- **Solestice.** An undefined "osis flapper" operator; the ask is what the movement
  is.
- **Double Dyno and Double Whip.** The general double-prefix scoring reading and the
  routing of the count-quantifier rows are asked in the Scoring paper; what remains
  here is only the per-badge reading of these two names, and the ambiguity of the
  "(rev)" annotation on them.
- **Stepping Ducking Blurry Whirl.** The folk name reads four operators at 6 ADD
  while the structured name reads two operators at 5; the ask is which reading the
  badge takes.

## Who should review it

The rules expert owns the movement rulings: the sumo X-Dex, the pogo terminal, the
jani-walker and twisting structures, the spyro prefix, the osis-suffix rule, and the
path-vocabulary ruling behind the Blink exception. The 9-ADD landing needs
witnesses and footage before it can be read at all, so it is a memory-and-evidence
ask first and a ruling second. The Motorfly memory check belongs to reachable
old-schoolers, not to any single authority. The records-only riders block only
badges, and their disposition is the maintainer's to sequence against the
world-record-video work.
