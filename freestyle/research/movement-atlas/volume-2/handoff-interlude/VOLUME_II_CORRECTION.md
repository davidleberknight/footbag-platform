# Volume II Correction Statement

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. A concise replacement for Volume II's withdrawn unrestricted-product claim, suitable for linking from the falsification erratum (`../VOLUME_II_FALSIFICATION.md`). It does not rewrite the frozen Volume II; it is the corrected claim that supersedes it, grounded in the handoff-state interlude (`HANDOFF_STATE_PAPER.md`).

## Withdrawn

> Over a fixed surface fiber, the two-dexterity movements are the unrestricted Cartesian product of two one-dexterity cubes, 64 points, with the second cube free and independent of the first.

## Corrected

Over a fixed surface fiber, the two-dexterity movements are, in their side-direction-output token labeling, **bounded above by** the 64-point product of two dexterity cubes. This is an **upper bound, not an exact count**, for four grammar-level reasons, none of which is a claim of physical impossibility:

1. **No handoff-state language.** The grammar is an event-sequence language, not a state-transition language. Composability of one dexterity with the next is a question about state (carriage side, reference-foot identity, support-and-free-leg parity, stance mode), and the notation does not represent that state or supply a transition function over it. So the join is undefined, and most pairings are unresolved: the grammar certifies neither their composability nor their incompatibility.

2. **A moving frame.** The reference foot against which a dexterity's side is same or opposite is not recorded and not asserted invariant. If it turns across the chain, the second factor is coordinatized in the first dexterity's transported frame, so the space is a **cube-bundle over the first cube**, not a Cartesian product. The bundle is flat only if the frame never turns, which the grammar does not establish.

3. **A path-property grade.** The conditional far-receiver grade makes a coordinate's difficulty depend on the handoff, so difficulty does not factor into the two vertices. Grade is a property of the whole path, not of its parts. This is a scoring cross-term, documented but not used to claim structural incompatibility.

4. **An ambiguous diagonal.** The repeated-dexterity coordinates admit two unsettled grammatical readings, an ordered pair of dexterities or a single dexterity under a count operator, so the 64 are not a homogeneous category.

## The one-line replacement

The two-dexterity universe over a fiber is an **upper-bounded, mostly-unresolved cube-bundle with a path-dependent grade and an ambiguous diagonal**, not a free 64-point product. Its exact cardinality cannot be stated until the grammar carries a handoff-state vocabulary and a transition function; until then, 64 is a ceiling and the interior is largely undecided.

## Scope of the correction

This correction touches only the description of the two-dex space. Volume II's coordinate addressing, its base map, and its lifted grammar-status rules (launch foreclosure, midline collapse, peak uncertainty) stand; those are surface-fiber facts independent of the join. What is withdrawn is the word "unrestricted" and the treatment of 64 as an exact, homogeneous, separable, flat product.
