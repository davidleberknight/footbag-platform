# Transport-Rule Proposal

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. It proposes the smallest rule the corpus supports for carrying the reference frame and the leg parity through one dexterity. It is a **proposal**, not a grammar change; nothing here edits Grammar v1, the notation, or any frozen volume. It is stated so a later validation pass or an outside-evidence pass can test it.

## What a transport rule must do

If the reference-foot identity and the support-and-free-leg parity are real handoff state (the interlude argued they are, and the scorecard confirms every dexterity needs them), then composing two dexterities requires a rule that says how the first dexterity changes them. The rule takes the frame and parity before the dexterity and returns them after.

## The honest constraint on proposing one

The validation established that the reference foot and the leg parity are **absent from 100% of the notation**. So a transport rule for them cannot be fit to recorded values, because there are no recorded values, and it cannot be falsified by the notation, because the notation never contradicts a claim it does not record. Any rule proposed here is therefore consistent-but-unfalsifiable against notation. This is not a reason to invent a convenient rule; it is a reason to propose the smallest defensible one and to mark it explicitly as untestable in this lane.

## The smallest proposal

A single deterministic rule, stated as a pair of toggles keyed to structure the notation does record:

1. **Frame transport by carriage crossing.** A dexterity toggles the reference foot exactly when its output carriage crosses the body (the bag changes side); it preserves the reference foot when the carriage stays. Rationale: the reference foot is the foot the side is measured against, and a crossing is precisely the event that moves the bag to the other foot's frame.

2. **Parity transport by leg engagement.** A dexterity swaps the free and support legs when the circling leg plants to receive, and preserves them when the same leg remains free (the same-foot-dexing case the grammar already permits). Rationale: a plant is what frees the other leg; no plant leaves the circling leg available.

3. **Reset by an intervening body event.** When the join carries a body event that replants (a no-plant body action that lands), the parity is recomputed from the replant, not carried through the dexterity. Rationale: the body-or-orientation join event (the corpus's forced fifth component) is exactly where the stance is re-established.

Nothing smaller works, because dropping any of the three leaves a class of corpus rows whose second dexterity cannot be framed: rule 1 is needed for the crossing rows, rule 2 for the same-leg-versus-alternating distinction, and rule 3 for the plurality of rows with a body event in the join.

## Where a single rule is not enough

The corpus already shows one place the rule cannot be uniform. Rows with an intervening body event (Group 1 of the counterexample catalog) do not transport the parity through a clean dexterity at all; they route it through the body event (rule 3). So the transport is not one rule over dexterities; it is one rule for a bare dexterity join and a different reset for a body-mediated join. A single deterministic dexterity-only rule is therefore insufficient for the corpus as a whole, and the honest proposal is the two-regime form above (bare-dexterity transport by rules 1 and 2; body-mediated reset by rule 3), not a single map.

## Status

This proposal is offered as the target for the next validation pass and, for the two notation-invisible components, for an eventual outside-evidence pass. It is not adopted, not encoded, and not testable from notation as it stands. Its value now is that it states the smallest transport hypothesis precisely enough to be attacked later, and it records that even the smallest hypothesis already needs two regimes because of the body-mediated join.
