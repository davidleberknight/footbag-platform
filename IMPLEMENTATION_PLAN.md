# IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md

This is the single plan of record for the footbag.org modernization: every open work item and every open question we still need to resolve, grouped by who owns it. It is the clean baseline we will load into the go-live kanban board. Long-term design and rationale live under `docs/`; this file tracks what is left to do.

How to read it. Sections are ordered by owner, from the people we are coordinating with (Steve, then Julie) to the maintainers doing the build (Dave, then James, whose pipeline work carries the most detail). Each item is written to stand on its own: you should not need to open another document to understand what the task is. Every item must be pedantic for a human reader: state the task in full and unambiguously, name exact identifiers (file paths, route strings, schema objects, env vars) rather than vague gestures, give an explicit done-condition, and use no internal shorthand, finding ids, or labels a reader has not been given. An item a human cannot act on without opening another document or guessing what it means is not done. A reference to `docs/MIGRATION_PLAN.md` (the detailed migration design) or a file path appears only where it points at the actual work or saves copying a large table here. Finished items are deleted from this file rather than marked done.

Owners. Steve is the current footbag.org webmaster (he holds the legacy-site, DNS, email, and data-export facts). Julie is the IFPA secretary (she decides IFPA governance, groups, and votes). Dave is the project maintainer and manager (platform code, and he drives every open question to a decision). James is the historical-data and freestyle maintainer (the data pipeline and all freestyle content).

Tags. Each Dave and James item carries a category so this plan can seed the go-live kanban board directly: **[BUG]** a verified defect in running code; **[DEVIATION]** running code intentionally differs from the intended design, guarded, with a removal or unblock condition; **[KANBAN]** a forward feature, test, or gate that becomes a board card; **[PRE-KANBAN]** must be resolved this sprint to make the baseline trustworthy (the data-loss defect, plus the pipeline doc-sync); **[BLOCKED]** waiting on external input (Steve, Julie, the IFPA, or rules expert Red). Steve's and Julie's items, and the final MVP scope, are open questions, marked **[Q]**. The Blockers, Release gates, and Deferred sections are context, not board cards.

## Julie — IFPA governance

Julie is the IFPA secretary. These are governance decisions only she can make. Dave chases each one; Julie decides. The sensitive specifics (actual ballots and tallies) stay out of this public file. `docs/MIGRATION_PLAN.md` §20a (with §19 items 35-37) is the authoritative master for these; this section mirrors them in actionable form with status, kept in sync so the two cannot drift.

- [Q] **Which groups are still alive.** The legacy `ifpa_committees` table historically holds on the order of ~170 committee and group records (IDs run to 172, many likely inactive); a `CommitteeType` field separates a committee from a group, with `CommitteeValid` and `CommitteeIsOfficial` flags, and `ifpa_committee_members` lists who belonged to each. Tell us which of these old groups, committees, and email discussion lists people still actually use, who runs or moderates each, and who would be upset if one changed or went away.
- [Q] **What happens to the old IFPA vote records.** The old site holds past elections (`ifpa_elections`), the issues voted on (`ifpa_issues`), and every individually cast ballot (`ifpa_issue_votes` records each vote as a voter member id plus the choice and a timestamp, so the ballots are individually attributable, not anonymous). Decide whether to keep them, archive them, archive them encrypted and sealed, keep only the final vote tallies, or (only if Julie confirms it is acceptable) delete them. This is a records-retention decision; because each ballot names its voter, the recommended path is encrypted-and-sealed archive, never published, and the underlying ballot data stays private.
- [Q] **How sanctioning actually works.** Tell us whether IFPA event sanctioning is really a committee that discusses and decides together, or just one or two people approving it, and whether anyone still reads the `sanctioning@footbag.org` address. Until we know, the platform's design is simple admin approval: the `sanction_status` field and the admin decision columns exist in the schema, but the approval action is not yet implemented in any controller or service.
- [Q] **Where each group's conversation should live.** Each legacy committee already carried its own email list (the `CommitteeEmail` setting, with optional moderation, restriction, and archiving), so every group has a legacy mailing list behind this choice. For each group or committee, say whether its discussion belongs on the new platform, in Google Groups, in an external chat tool, or nowhere (retired). No in-app group feature gets built until this is decided.
- [Q] **Whether kept groups need moderators.** For any group we keep or move, does it need a real owner or moderator with power over who is a member and what gets posted, or is informal order from admins and the community enough.
- [Q] **Who to notify when something changes.** When a group or address is retired or archived, who needs to hear about it, and how.
- [Q] **Running the email side.** Help administer the IFPA Google Workspace (groups, aliases, and spam settings) once we know which groups and addresses we are keeping.
- [Q] **Establish IFPA governance for platform-secret custody and administrative authority.** The bylaws (Article IV bank-account and contract authority; Articles VI-VII officer roles) predate the digital platform and say nothing about who holds custody of operational secrets or who may grant administrative authority on the new system; `GOVERNANCE.md` gives the maintainer Category A (code) and the Board Category B (policy) but is silent on platform admin authority, so it defaults to the maintainer by omission. We need Julie and the Board to ratify new governance covering: a designated platform-operator (System Administrator) role and the Secretary's oversight of it; custody, separation of duties, and rotation cadence for the operational secrets (the session secret, the KMS JWT-signing and ballot-encryption keys, the CloudFront origin-verify secret, AWS host credentials, the Stripe API key and webhook secret, and the one-time bootstrap-admin claim token); who authorizes granting and revoking platform admin (IFPA officers by the Board or Secretary, curators and volunteers by the maintainer with notification to the Secretary), bootstrap-token provisioning, and recovery if all admins are lost; and audit and reporting to the Secretary, with escalation to the Board for officer changes or credential compromise. The technical mechanisms already exist (the admin lifecycle and secrets management in DESIGN_DECISIONS); this adds the IFPA authority layer on top. Per bylaws Article IV the Board determines the bank account, so this also governs authorizing the Stripe-to-IFPA-bank link that Dave's Stripe activation depends on.
