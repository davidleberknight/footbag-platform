# Footbag Freestyle Glossary

Editorial reference for freestyle-footbag terminology that does not belong
in the trick dictionary. Entries here describe shred-level run quality,
play-quality adjectives, community abbreviations, and competitive-format
vocabulary — none of which have a single trick referent.

**Status:** v0 draft, 2026-04-24. Content sourced from
[footbag.org/reference2/freestyle/](http://www.footbag.org/reference2/freestyle/)
glossary + Additional Degrees of Difficulty pages, annotated with expert-
review status. A schema-level glossary table may follow once Red's 2026-04
follow-up confirms which entries are active.

## How this file relates to the trick dictionary

The `freestyle_tricks` table holds tricks and their ADD values. The
`freestyle_trick_modifiers` table holds reusable prefixes (paradox, spinning,
atomic, etc.). Neither is the right home for:

- Run-quality floor labels (guiltless, fearless, tiltless)
- Play-quality adjectives (slurry, froggy, hippy, leggy)
- Community abbreviations (B2B, BSOS, PWF)
- Competitive format names (Shred:30, Circle Contest)

Those live here, in editorial form.

## A. ADD floor labels (run-quality thresholds)

meets or exceeds a difficulty floor. Source:
`/reference2/freestyle/additional-degrees-of-difficulty/`.

## B. ADD system core terms

| Term | Meaning | Status |
|---|---|---|
| ADD | "Additional Degree of Difficulty" — an indivisible unit of difficulty a move contributes to its total value | Confirmed |
| Delay [del] | Stall/catch on any foot or leg surface | Confirmed |
| Dexterity [dex] | Circling or crossing the bag's path with one leg | Confirmed |
| Unusual Surface [uns] | Surface other than toes, insides, outsides, knees | Confirmed |
| Body [bod] | Spin, jump, duck, or double-hip-pivot (paradox) | Confirmed |
| Cross Body [xbd] | Kick or delay with crossed legs | Confirmed |
| X-Dex | Dexterity-type add for complete circling motions (e.g. second dex in a double around-the-world). Red pt1 2026-04: pushes Atom Smasher to 4 ADD | Pending Red on universality as a modifier |
| BOP | Butterfly / Osis / Paradox-Mirage — the fundamental 3-ADD trio | Confirmed |

## C. Move-components framework

Parabolic motion of the bag splits every trick into three phases. Uptime and
midtime components together form a "set" in modern usage.

| Term | Meaning | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Uptime | Components performed while the bag is travelling up from the initial set | Confirmed |
| Midtime / Hangtime | Components at the apex of the bag's motion | Confirmed |
| Downtime | Components while the bag is falling toward the catch or kick | Confirmed |
| Set | (modern) Entire collection of uptime + midtime components preceding the downtime core | Confirmed |
| Set | (old) Clipper set or toe set — the initial projection only | Confirmed historical |

## D. Play-quality adjectives

Qualitative terms describing execution, not technical content.

| Term | Meaning | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Slurry | Uptime dex where the dex leg clears the bag before it leaves the setting foot — not clean, does not count for ADDs | Pending Red |
| Froggy | Spin/gyro where the player begins the body motion before the bag leaves the setting foot — not clean | Pending Red |
| Thin | Dex where it's unclear whether the leg fully circled the bag | Pending Red |

## F. Play-form concepts

| Term | Meaning | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Symposium | Dex from a one-footed jump where the dex foot was planted and the other foot was not | Confirmed |
| Symple | One-footed jumping dex with no opposite-foot support until the next contact | Pending Red |
| Backside | Downtime component of a multi-dex trick performed symposium | Pending Red |
| Frontside | Uptime component of a multi-dex trick performed symposium | Pending Red |
| Alpine | A move whose uptime component incorporates a duck, dive, or weave (e.g. alpine legbeater = atomic ducking butterfly) | Pending Red |
| Fetish | When a player slaps or kicks themselves during a run | Pending Red |

## G. Competitive formats (not trick-dictionary content)

Included here for glossary completeness; belongs in a competition-rules
domain, not the trick layer.

- **Freestyle Routine** — timed performance choreographed to music, judged
  on artistic and technical merit
- **Shred Contest (Shred:30)** — technical-merit only; formula rewards
  difficulty, variety, quantity within a fixed time
- **Big Trick / Sick, Big, and Best** — one-trick and three-trick combo contests
- **Circle Contest** — pool format, multiple rounds per level, rank-based elimination
- **Most Rippin' Run** — last-player-standing at a designated difficulty floor
