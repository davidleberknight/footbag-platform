# Positional-Collapse Report

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. Companion to `RESEARCH_NOTE.md`, from `triple_dex_matrix.csv` and `distinct_formula_catalog.csv`. It lists every case where a positional label collapses, where a far reading opens a distinct coordinate, where near/far cannot be represented, and where multiple names occupy one formula. It proposes nothing and names nothing new.

## 1. SAME / NEAR wording collapses onto the ordinary near form

A submitted "same-side" or "near" reading on a base whose terminal is already the near (SAME) foot adds no representable distinction: it names the terminal the movement already has. In the matrix, all 128 NEAR and FAR readings are marked as wording-collapse rows (class C), because near and far are the receiver-distance words for the same and opposite feet the notation already writes. The two motivating examples are the headline cases:

- **Pixie Eggbeater.** Ordinary form `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]` already lands near. A "same-side" reading names that same near terminal, so it collapses onto the ordinary form. No distinct formula.
- **Pixie Double Leg Over.** Ordinary form `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > OP IN [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]` already lands near. Same collapse.

The collapse is a property of the terminal-receiver axis. A "same-side" that instead flipped a dexterity's leg side would define a different base (enumerated separately) and would not collapse; the observed collapse is therefore diagnostic that the submission was a redundant terminal label.

## 2. FAR creates a distinct coordinate

For a base whose near (SAME) terminal is named, the far (OP) terminal is a different notation and a different final foot, so it is a genuinely distinct, representable formula. Thirteen bases show exactly this shape, near named and far an empty distinct coordinate (class D). The two motivating bases are among them:

- Far counterpart of Pixie Eggbeater: `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > OP TOE [DEL]` (distinct, unnamed).
- Far counterpart of Pixie Double Leg Over: `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > OP IN [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > OP TOE [DEL]` (distinct, unnamed).

The full list of the thirteen near-named / far-unnamed bases is in `OCCUPANCY_SUMMARY.md`. Distinct here means representable and unoccupied; it is not a claim of realizability and not a proposal to name.

## 3. NEAR / FAR cannot be represented beyond the same/op terminal

Near and far name a receiver foot, which the notation writes as the same/op terminal token. Any finer receiver-distance the words might carry (how far, along which path) is not written. So the near/far distance reading, as distinct from the plain same/op terminal, is representation-ambiguous: the movement may differ physically, but the current representation does not preserve the distinction. This is why every NEAR and FAR reading in the matrix (128 rows) is a wording-collapse onto its same/op twin (class C) carrying an explicit representation-ambiguity note, rather than a separate formula. The Atlas exposes the same/op terminal reliably and the near/far distance not at all.

## 4. Multiple names occupy one formula

Formula-identity comparison surfaced seven distinct formulas each carried by two or more active names (class B). These are the clearest existing evidence that names and formulas are not one-to-one, and that co-articulation and positional wording differences do not create distinct movements:

| One formula | Active names sharing it |
|---|---|
| `TOE > SAME IN > SAME IN > SAME IN > SAME TOE` | triple_around_the_world, pixie_double_pickup_same_side |
| `TOE > SAME IN > OP IN > OP OUT > SAME TOE` | smog, pixie_quantum_legover |
| `TOE > SAME IN > OP OUT > OP OUT > SAME TOE` | pigbeater, pixie_eggbeater, sailing_legover |
| `TOE > SAME OUT > OP IN > OP OUT > SAME TOE` | fairy_double_leg_over, flog |
| `TOE > OP IN > OP OUT > OP OUT > SAME TOE` | bedwetter, quantum_eggbeater |
| `TOE > OP OUT > OP IN > OP OUT > SAME TOE` | predator, tapping_double_leg_over |
| `TOE > OP OUT > OP OUT > OP OUT > SAME TOE` | bladerunner, tapping_eggbeater, atomic_eggbeater |

Two things collapse here, both by formula identity. First, co-articulation: pigbeater writes its first dex with a `>>` layered marker while pixie_eggbeater and sailing_legover write `>`; the movement formula is the same. Second, entry naming: the same three-dex-into-toe chain is named for a pixie entry, a sailing entry, a quantum entry, an atomic entry, or a tapping entry depending on lineage, while the dex tokens and terminal are identical. The matrix records these as one formula with several names, not several movements.

## Summary

The four positional labels reduce to two representable terminals. SAME and OP are real, distinct, and both attested. NEAR and FAR are the receiver-distance wordings for those two feet: they add no representable distinction and their finer distance sense is not preserved. The false Pixie same-side distinctions are the near-wording collapse; their genuine structural counterparts are the far (OP) coordinates, which are distinct and empty; and the corpus already contains seven formulas that one movement wears under several names.
