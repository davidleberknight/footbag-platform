# Zulu / Weaving — first promotion batch, mirrored JOB notation draft

Status: PROMOTED. The 12 first-batch compounds (six bases x Zulu/Weaving) are
canonical: red_additions rows + red_corrections operational_notation + a zulu
trick_modifiers row, mirroring the live ducking_<base> exemplars. ADD math and
bracket-count==ADD verified for all 12; the encyclopedia example sections derive
from the modifier links; the bare compounds were removed from the Emerging
Vocabulary (TRACKED_UNPUBLISHED_NAMES).

Follow-up (not a blocker): the distinguishing launch detail is carried as a
non-scoring annotation — Zulu `(across body)` before the duck, Weaving `(same
foot)` on the terminal — rather than a formal JOB token. A future notation pass
may represent it more explicitly (for Weaving, the candidate is flipping the
terminal side from the ducking exemplar's `OP` to `SAME`); until then the
annotation keeps ADD unchanged and invents no new syntax.

## The mirror rule (the whole proposal in one line)

A Zulu or Weaving compound = the matching **Ducking** compound (identical base
dex chain, identical `DUCK [BOD]`, identical ADD), plus a non-scoring annotation
for the one distinguishing launch detail. Zulu and Weaving are platform-tracked
ducking sets whose launch incorporates the ducking movement; the base mechanics
after the duck are the base trick, unchanged.

Evidence the ADD is inherited, not new: Weaving is already registered so that
`weaving clipper = 3` and `weaving butterfly = 4`, which equal the live
`ducking_clipper` (3) and `ducking_butterfly` (4). So Weaving compound ADD already
equals Ducking compound ADD. Zulu is the sibling ducking set and takes the same
equality by ruling.

## Inherited from Ducking (zero new assumptions)

For every candidate below, the following are copied verbatim from the live
`ducking_<base>` exemplar:

- the launch token (`TOE` or `SET`),
- the `DUCK [BOD]` body token,
- the entire base dex chain after the duck (`OP IN [DEX]`, `SAME CLIP [XBD]`, the
  osis `[BOD] [XBD]`, etc.),
- the terminal `[DEL]`,
- the ADD value.

## Genuinely new (the only things not inherited)

1. A marker for the distinguishing launch detail: Zulu's across-body path (before
   the duck), Weaving's same-foot catch (at the terminal).
2. The assumption that this detail is ADD-neutral (non-scoring). Weaving's
   registered ADD = Ducking ADD already demonstrates this for Weaving; Zulu takes
   it by analogy.
3. Zulu's ADD is not yet registered in `trick_modifiers` (Weaving's is).

## Smallest possible list of remaining curator decisions

1. **Accept the mirror rule.** A Zulu/Weaving compound inherits the matching
   Ducking compound's base chain, `DUCK [BOD]`, and ADD. (This is the whole thing;
   everything else is mechanical.)
2. **Zulu across-body marker.** Confirm it is a non-scoring `(across body)`
   annotation placed before the duck (no added bracket, no ADD change). The
   alternative would be a scored cross-body component, which Weaving's
   ADD-equals-Ducking evidence argues against.
3. **Weaving same-foot marker.** Choose one: (a) a non-scoring `(same foot)`
   annotation on the terminal, leaving the terminal side as the Ducking exemplar's;
   or (b) the same-foot catch IS the terminal side, flipping the terminal from
   `OP` to `SAME` (for example `OP TOE [DEL]` becomes `SAME TOE [DEL]`). Both keep
   ADD unchanged; (b) is more structurally explicit. This is the only genuinely
   open notation question.
4. **Register Zulu's ADD.** Add `zulu,1,1,...` to `trick_modifiers` mirroring the
   existing `weaving` row, so the ADD-math derives Zulu compounds as base + 1 (the
   duck), equal to the Ducking compound.

Decisions 1, 2, and 4 are confirmations of the inherited reading. Decision 3 is
the single real choice. After it, the 12 promote mechanically and populate the
Zulu and Weaving encyclopedia example sections (two 3-ADD plus wing / whirl / osis
at 4 ADD each).
