## Theme 5 -- Atomic-set polysemy (the +1 gap pattern) (HIGH leverage, depends on Q4.A)

If Q4.A returns `far = +0 universal`, the systemic +1 gap in 11 PB rows traces to an implicit operator in IFPA's reading.

| Trick | IFPA stated | PB decomposition | Flat formula |
|---|---:|---|---:|
| Atomsmasher | 4 | Atomic far Mirage | atomic(+1)+mirage(2) = 3 |
| Blur | 4 | Stepping far Mirage | stepping(+1)+mirage(2) = 3 |
| Sumo | 5 | Nuclear far Mirage | nuclear(+2)+mirage(2) = 4 |

Options:
- **A.** PassBack notation is incomplete; IFPA reads an implicit Paradox on the set-modifier (per pt10's nuclear=paradox+atomic-set framing, atomic-set is functionally paradox+atomic-modifier = +2 in any composition). This generalizes pt10 from nuclear to atomic uniformly.

Option A is most consistent with pt10. Confirming it would require ADD_FORMULA_ASSUMPTIONS.md to treat atomic-as-set-modifier as `paradox + atomic-modifier = +2` whenever it appears in a compound name (Atomsmasher, Atomic Illusion, Atomic Torque etc.), not just on rotational bases.
