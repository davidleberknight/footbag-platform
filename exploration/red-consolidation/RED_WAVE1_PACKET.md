## Q4 -- Atomic-set polysemy (the systemic +1 gap)

Auditing IFPA canonical ADDs against PassBack's literal decompositions, 11 named compounds show a systematic +1 gap:

| Trick | IFPA stated | PB literal decomposition | Flat formula |
|---|---:|---|---:|
| Atom Smasher | 4 | Atomic far Mirage | 3 |
| Blur | 4 | Stepping far Mirage | 3 |
| Drifter | 3 | Miraging far Clipper | 2 |
| Sumo | 5 | Nuclear far Mirage | 4 |
| Fury | 5 | Barraging far Mirage | 3 (-2) |

(Eleven rows total; the table above is a representative sample.)

Two readings could explain the systematic +1:

- **A.** Far carries +1, not +0 (closing Q3.a in the other direction). One ruling closes 11 rows.
- **B.** IFPA reads `Atomic X` as `Paradox + Atomic + X` whenever it appears in a named compound (parallel to your pt10 ruling that Nuclear = Paradox + Atomic set). So atomic-set-as-modifier is functionally +2 (paradox + atomic-modifier) in compounds, not just on rotational bases. PassBack's `Atomic far Mirage` is missing the implicit Paradox.

Option B is consistent with pt10 and would explain why Atom Smasher = 4 even though the literal `Atomic Mirage` gives 3 by flat math.

Which reading is canonical? If A, the formula table widens to recognize far = +1. If B, the formula table widens to read atomic-set-as-modifier with implicit paradox in any compound context.

This question depends on Q3.a. If Q3.a is "far = +0 universal", Q4 reduces to B.
