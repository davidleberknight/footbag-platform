# Down-family adversarial audit: falsification pass on the 2x2 topology grid

Date: 2026-07-02. Companion to `RED_PACKET_A1_REVISED.md` (rewritten same day around this
result) and `PASSBACK_SOURCING_REPORT.md`.

## Bottom line

The adversarial audit **failed to falsify the proposed 2x2 topology. Instead, it
strengthened it.** The apparent PassBack/FB.org contradictions were explained by differing
coordinate systems (entry-relative vs bag-relative), leaving only a small number of isolated
residual questions (Torch-R-Rack, embedded-base assignment, and one PassBack wording
inconsistency). This substantially reduces the scope of the original Red doctrine question.

## Method

Search ONLY for counterexamples, across every Down / DDD / DOD / Paradon / Barfly compound
in the corpus, in three evidence strata: FB.org JOBs (can genuinely falsify), platform-
authored notations (mismatches flag authoring bugs), name-only rows (naming strain).
Counterexample classes hunted: a down-named JOB whose tail is not a double-OUT-dex run into
a clipper catch; a trace contradicting BOTH folk name and PassBack tech name; a non-clipper
or non-[XBD] catch; IN-direction base dexes; one name on structurally different JOBs.

All 24 traceable family JOBs land in exactly one cell. No bad-tail and no non-clipper-catch

## 3. The "clipper-column crossing" dissolves

`PASSBACK_SOURCING_REPORT.md` section 5 reported that PassBack and FB.org cross-assign DDD
and Barfly. That was an artifact of assuming near/far is contact-relative. The correct
decoding, forced by Barfly 78's prose:

**PassBack's near/far is bag-relative: near = the leg beside the bag's current position.**
For a toe delay the bag is over the setting foot (near = setting leg). For a **clipper delay
the bag sits beside the SUPPORT leg** (the clipping foot crosses behind it), so near = the
support-side leg. After any dex or plant, near = that contact leg.

Re-checking every PassBack/FB.org pair under bag-relative semantics: DOD, Paradon, DDD,
Barfly, Superfly, Fusion, Plasma, Blurrier, Blurriest, Nemesis, and Venom ALL agree
(11 clean), Scorpion's Tail and Bullwhip agree given the spin-inversion refinement
(section 4), Torch-R-Rack remains crossed (section 6). Venom's own prose tension ("a gyro
blurriest" over a notation that traces differently) also dissolves: bag-relative, Venom's
dex pattern IS the blurriest pattern. **There is no cross-source structural disagreement
anywhere in the family.**

Two naming conventions coexist and are both decodable:
- **Folk base names are entry-relative** (which absolute leg dexes, given the set): this is
  the platform's parity-trace reading and matches shipped rows (fusion = DOD, etc.).
- **PassBack technical names are bag-relative**: same structures, different coordinate frame.

## 4. Refinement forced by the pass: rotation inversion

Scorpion's Tail (PB "Spinning far", dexes on the support-side leg) and Bullwhip (PB
"Spinning near", dexes on the clipping-foot leg) are labeled opposite to the bare clipper
rows for identical dex legs. Both differ from the bare rows only by the leading back-spin.
Consistent resolution: **a body spin inverts the near/far frame** (physically: the unwind
carries the bag to the other side of the body). Two independent exhibits, zero
counter-exhibits. Venom is compatible (its surge dex re-anchors the frame after the spin).
Recorded as an evidenced convention note, not yet doctrine.
