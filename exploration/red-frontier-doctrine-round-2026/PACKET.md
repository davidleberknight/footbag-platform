# Section A — Core Structural Doctrine

## A1. Down Family / DOD Doctrine

### The question

The "down" family uses two near-identical names with the same ADD:

* **DOD = Double-Over-Down** (4 ADD), the base for ~15 canonical compounds.
* **DDD = Down-Double-Down** (4 ADD).

Are DOD and DDD the **same structure under two names**, or **two distinct moves**? And does
the down-family naming follow a consistent arithmetic, or has it drifted?

### Existing evidence

* `double-over-down` (DOD) anchors a large, settled compound family at 5–7 ADD:
  `fusion` (atomic DOD = 5), `plasma` (quantum DOD = 5), `down-diver` (5),
  `oh-wheely` (6), `pixie-/gyro-/fairy-/stepping-/symposium-/tapping-double-over-down` (5),
  `spinning-ducking-symposium-double-over-down` (7), `miraging-symposium-double-over-down` (6).
* `down-double-down` (DDD) is sparsely populated: `scorpions-tail` (spinning DDD = 5),
  `spinning-symposium-down-double-down` (6).
* Both DOD and DDD assert **4 ADD** as bare bases.
* The down-naming chain extends into compression names: `superfly` (5) →
  `superduperfly` = spinning-superfly (6) → **`superdeeduperfly` = spinning-ducking-superfly (7)**.
  The "dee/duper/down" naming encodes the same spinning/ducking stack the structural names
  already carry — i.e. the down-family naming is a folk layer riding on top of structural
  operators.

### Current contradictory decompositions

* DOD and DDD both read 4 ADD, but the dictionary keeps them as separate base rows. If they
  are the same move, one should collapse to an alias of the other; if distinct, the
  difference (over-down vs double-down ordering) needs a stated structural rule.
* The tracked frontier carries ~46 "down/DOD/DDD" entries that cannot be promoted until the
  DOD↔DDD relationship and the down-arithmetic are fixed.

### Ruling options

* **Option A — DOD ≡ DDD (single structure).** Pick one canonical base; the other becomes a
  naming alias. The down-naming variants (including superdeeduperfly) resolve to the chosen
  structural form.
* **Option B — DOD ≠ DDD (distinct).** State the structural distinction (over-down vs
  double-down) explicitly so the ~46 frontier entries decompose against the correct base.
* **Option C — Down is a naming layer, not a base.** Treat "down" forms as folk names over
  the underlying operator stack (the superdeeduperfly pattern), and decompose each compound
  to its structural operators rather than to a DOD/DDD base.

### Affected tricks

~46 tracked entries plus the ~15 already-canonical DOD compounds (whose base identity would
be confirmed, not changed).

## A2. Weaving Doctrine

### The question

"Weaving" appears as a prefix across a wide spread of bases but has no defined ADD
contribution or structural identity. Is weaving **an operator** (a fixed +N), **a movement
family** (a topology grouping), or **a naming layer** (a folk synonym for an existing
operator stack)?

### Estimated impact

~32 weaving compounds across the observational corpus and competition records (17 distinct
in the tracked universe; the remainder appear as record-attested names and source variants).
Examples: Weaving Mirage, Weaving Butterfly, Weaving Clipper, Weaving Drifter, Weaving
Illusion, Weaving Legover, Weaving Osis, Weaving Pickup, Weaving Magellan, Weaving Pigbeater,
Weaving Symposium Mirage, Weaving Guay.

### Existing evidence

* Weaving spans both stall bases (clipper, osis) and dexterity bases (mirage, illusion,
  legover, pickup), which is the spread of an operator rather than a single trick.
* No notation has been authored for any weaving compound, and no source corpus defines its
  ADD contribution; it is currently treated as an undefined-operator folk cluster.

### Candidate decompositions

* **Operator (+N):** weaving contributes a fixed ADD on any base (analogous to spinning /
  ducking / stepping at +1, or shooting at +3). The spread across bases supports this.
* **Movement family:** weaving names a body-path topology (a weave of the legs) that does not
  add ADD but groups tricks observationally.
* **Naming layer:** weaving is a folk synonym for an existing operator combination, in which
  case each "weaving X" resolves to a known stack.

### Ruling options

* **Option A — Operator with a stated +N.** Then the whole weaving family promotes by the
  standard compositional rule.
* **Option B — Movement family, +0.** Weaving compounds derive purely from their base; the
  weaving name carries topology, not ADD.
* **Option C — Naming layer.** Define weaving's structural equivalent so each compound
  resolves to existing canon.

## A3. Blurry Transitivity

*(Reused from the standalone blurry packet; evidence preserved.)*

### The contradiction, in data

"Blurry" is registered as a flat +1 set modifier, and its accepted decomposition is
stepping + paradox (= +2). Both cannot hold uniformly. Every blurry compound is stored
linking the +1 modifier, but the asserted ADDs split cleanly by base type:

| Compound | Base | Base type | Base ADD | Asserted | blurry = +1 | blurry = +2 (stepping+paradox) | Matches |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ripwalk | butterfly | non-rotational | 3 | 4 | 4 | 5 | **+1** |
| Blurriest | barfly | non-rotational | 4 | 5 | 5 | 6 | **+1** |
| Blur | mirage | rotational | 2 | 4 | 3 | 4 | **+2** |
| Blurry Whirl | whirl | rotational | 3 | 5 | 4 | 5 | **+2** |
| Blurry Torque | torque | rotational | 4 | 6 | 5 | 6 | **+2** |
| Food Processor (Blurry Blender) | blender | rotational | 4 | 6 | 5 | 6 | **+2** |

The split is exact: **blurry = +1 on non-rotational bases, +2 on rotational bases**. This is
structurally the same rotational-bonus tension that atomic carried (now retired for atomic).

### How it arose

* Blurry registered as +1; Red pt11 migrated its rotational bonus from 2 to 1 ("+1 model").
* pt11 recorded the fallout: "Blur rescued via the existing Stepping Paradox Mirage
  decomposition; Blurriest improves; Ripwalk unchanged; **Blurry Whirl breaks under +1 — pt12
  follow-up; Blurry Torque breaks under +1 — pt12 follow-up**."
* Ripwalk was re-decomposed to "Stepping Butterfly" (+1, paradox rejected), confirming its
  "blurry" is really just stepping.
* The rotational compounds keep their asserted ADDs only via the explicit stepping + paradox
  (+2) reading, NOT via the +1 modifier they link to.

### Ruling options

* **Option A — blurry is +2 (stepping + paradox), uniformly.** Re-examine the non-rotational
  cases: Ripwalk and Blurriest then are stepping-only compounds wearing a folk "blurry" name.
* **Option B — blurry stays +1; rotational compounds decompose explicitly.** Keep +1 (matches
  non-rotational) and store rotational blurry compounds via explicit stepping + paradox (+2),
  treating their "blurry" name as a folk compression.
* **Option C — blurry is base-type-dependent (+1 / +2 rotational).** Formalize the split
  (paradox is meaningful only on a rotational dex). Mirrors the pre-migration atomic model.

### Recommendation

Treat "blurry" as a folk naming layer rather than a single arithmetic modifier: decompose
each compound explicitly — stepping + paradox (+2) where the paradox is real (the rotational
set), stepping-only (+1) where it is not (Ripwalk, Blurriest). The one item Red must rule is
the pt12 follow-up: do Blurry Whirl (5) and Blurry Torque (6) keep +2 via stepping+paradox
(Options A/B), or does blurry carry a formal rotational +2 (Option C)?

# Section B — Modifier Arithmetic

## B1. Pogo ADD

*(Reused from the completed pogo packet; evidence preserved.)*

### The question

**Does pogo contribute ADD?** Red's current position: "Pogo is a set and does not add ADD"
(pogo = +0). Footbag.org consistently lists pogo compounds one tier above their pogo-less
base, which is only consistent with pogo = +1.

### Decisive evidence — base-vs-pogo delta table

Base ADDs are independent canonical-dictionary values, so the delta is not circular.

| Trick | Base | Base ADD | fb.org ADD | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pogo Pickup | Pickup | 2 | 3 | +1 |
| Pogo Butterfly | Butterfly | 3 | 4 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Mirage | Paradox Mirage | 3 | 4 | +1 |
| Pogo Barfly | Barfly | 4 | 5 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Barrage | Paradox Barrage | 4 | 5 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Drifter | Paradox Drifter | 4 | 5 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Whirl | Paradox Whirl | 4 | 5 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Eggbeater | Paradox Eggbeater | 4 | 5 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Blender | Paradox Blender | 5 | 6 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Da Da Curve | Paradox Da Da Curve | 5 | 6 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Torque | Paradox Torque | 5 | 6 | +1 |
| Pogo Paradox Whirling Swirl | Paradox Whirling Swirl | 5 | 6 | +1 |
| Pogo Voodoo | Voodoo (Paradox Symposium Blur) † | 5 | 6 | +1 |

**Delta = +1 on all 13 rows, no counter-example.** († Voodoo's base ADD is footbag.org's own
5-ADD count; all other base ADDs are dictionary-anchored.) Most of the family is
pogo + paradox + base, so under the +1 reading the pogo +1 lands on top of paradox's settled

### Ruling options

* **Option A — Pogo = +0.** Red's current doctrine stands; footbag.org's counts become
  legacy and the whole family normalizes one tier down.
* **Option B — Pogo = +1.** Published counts are canonical; the family promotes by one
  compositional rule; pogo becomes a first-class +1 modifier. (This reverses the current
  "+0" guidance.)

Either outcome is consistent and promotable; what blocks promotion is only the choice.

## C2. Spyro Governance / Operator Definition

*(Reused from the standalone spyro packet; evidence preserved.)*

### The question

What does "spyro" denote? `spyro` is a 1-ADD canonical trick (a body primitive), **not** a
registered modifier. Yet "Spyro X" compounds appear across the corpus.

### Evidence — Spyro family inventory vs gyro

Every simple "Spyro X" notation is byte-identical to the canonical `gyro-X` compound:

| footbag.org "Spyro X" | Canonical `gyro-X` | ADD | Match |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spyro Illusion | gyro-illusion | 3 | identical |
| Spyro Mirage | gyro-mirage | 3 | identical |
| **Pandora** (Spyro Pickup) | gyro-pickup | 3 | identical |
| Paralax (Spyro Legover) | parallax (= Gyro Legover, canonical) | 3 | identical |
| Spyro Whirl | gyro-whirl | 4 | identical |

So structurally **spyro = gyro**. Pandora is simply the folk name for Spyro Pickup, which is
gyro-pickup. The one complication: Red noted the *name* "spyro" shifted toward **inspin**
(front-spin, a structurally different move), and Fairy Spyro Mirage diverges from
`fairy-gyro-mirage` by one dex-side (the "plant-before-dex" nuance).

### Naming vs structural interpretations

* **Structural:** the notations are gyro; spyro is an older folk name for gyro.
* **Naming (Red):** "spyro" now denotes inspin; a modern "spyro X" would mis-resolve to gyro.

### Ruling options

* **Option A — spyro = gyro (folk alias).** Wire simple Spyro forms (incl. Pandora) as
  aliases to the canonical `gyro-X` tricks. Most data-supported.
* **Option B — spyro = inspin.** "Spyro X" means inspin-X; the back-spin corpus notations are
  a historical mismatch.
* **Option C — distinct plant-before-dex variant.** Promote `spyro-X` as distinct rows
  (justified by the Fairy Spyro Mirage divergence).

### Recommendation

Option A, gated on confirming Red's "spyro → inspin" note means *historical renaming* (not
that a modern "spyro X" is front-spin), and resolving the Fairy Spyro Mirage dex-side
divergence per-compound.

# Section D — Naming and Normalization Questions

## D1. Atomic / Illusioning Normalization

**This section is governance-only. Atomic arithmetic is settled and is not reopened here.**

### Current state (settled)

* **Atomic = +1, finalized.** The +2-rotational reading is retired; the last data holdout
  (atomic-torque) is corrected to its +1 value.
* **Illusioning audit completed.** Illusioning is registered as a +1 form equivalent to
  rev(0) miraging (the outward-dex reverse of a miraging dex).

### Audit findings

* Atomic, illusion, mirage, and illusioning all share the **same outward-dex chassis** (an
  outward-then-inward, or reversed, dexterity). Illusioning is the rev(0) reading of that
  chassis — descriptively a presentation of an existing movement, not a new mechanic.
* The dictionary already records (for eggbeater) that a *historical* decomposition such as
  "illusion + legover" does not, by itself, make "illusioning" a productive modifier family
  across the dictionary.

### The governance question

Is **illusioning an independent operator**, or a **descriptive presentation** of an existing
outward-dex movement (rev(0) miraging) that should not spawn a parallel "illusioning-X"
family?

### Recommended normalization

Treat illusioning as a **descriptive presentation layer**, not an independent operator: keep
it as the rev(0)-miraging reading for the handful of historically-named cases, and do not
generate a productive "illusioning-X" family parallel to miraging-X. This keeps the outward-
dex chassis single-sourced and avoids two names for one mechanic. (Governance/presentation
only; no ADD value changes.)

# Section E — Residual Atomic Follow-Up

## E1. X-Dex Eligibility

### Current state (mostly settled)

* Atomic = +1 (settled). The **X-Dex is a separate +1** conditional event on an eligible
  following dex, distinct from the atomic operator itself.
* **Atom Smasher** (atomic mirage) is the canonical **explicit** X-Dex case: the X-Dex is
  named in the move, giving 4.
* **Atomic Torque is corrected to 5** (atomic +1 on torque; torque earns no X-Dex), now
  consistent with the published `atomic-blender` (5) and `atomic-drifter` (4) siblings.
* Read off the data, per-base X-Dex eligibility is: **mirage = eligible** (atom-smasher);
  **blender / drifter / torque = not**; **whirl / swirl = undetermined** (no active atomic
  compound yet).

### The follow-up question

**Beyond the established cases (mirage = explicit X-Dex; the rotational set otherwise not),
are there any additional structures that legitimately earn an X-Dex?** In particular, are
`whirl` and `swirl` X-Dex-eligible when an atomic compound on them eventually appears?

This is a small follow-up, not a major doctrine family. The +1 atomic arithmetic and the
existing X-Dex cases are settled; only the eligibility of the two un-exercised rotational
bases is open.

# Appendix B — Frontier Context (not doctrine questions)

Beyond the doctrine clusters above, the tracked frontier holds a large inventory that is
**explicitly excluded** from this packet because it raises no doctrine question:

* **Folk-name frontier (~262):** moves documented only by a folk name whose structure is
  known or recoverable. These are alias/naming work, not rulings.
* **Unresolved-structure frontier (~368):** moves whose decomposition is not yet worked out
  but which involve no open doctrine — they need notation authoring and structural analysis,
  not a Red decision.

These are routine (if substantial) downstream work, not consultation items. The important
point for the call: **the doctrine rulings in this packet are expected to unlock substantial
portions of both inventories.** Many folk-name and unresolved-structure entries are blocked
indirectly — a weaving ruling clears every weaving compound, a DOD ruling clears the down
family, a pogo ruling clears the pogo family, and so on. Resolving the eight items here
converts a large share of the frontier from "blocked" to "mechanical."
