# SCALE-5b Prose Drafts — Operator-Bridge Extension

Six-row continuation of SCALE-5's operator-bridge work. Completes atomic
(rotational-vs-non-rotational demonstration), symposium foundation, stepping
extension to drifter, paradox completion to torque, and pixie extension to
legover. Cadence design specifically avoids the SCALE-5 templated patterns
(`Practitioners with clean X tend to…`, `tend to` verb spam, `is the foundation`
repetition).

## Cadence design (pre-authoring)

Each axis varied across the 6 rows:

| row | opening cadence | coaching emphasis | failure mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| magellan | modifier-led | pixie compactness vs legover crossover | pixie opener crowds the leg-pass |
| symposium-mirage | body-line-led | posture lock timing | posture collapse mid-rotation |
| atom-smasher | base-context-led | atomic body-angle commitment for +2 rotational bonus | atomic body half-commits, drops to +1 |
| atomic-butterfly | contrast-led (vs atom-smasher) | rotational bonus rule demonstration | over-committing atomic in anticipation of bonus that doesn't apply |
| tombstone | base-led | step timing within drifter rotation | stepping too early kills rotation anchor |
| paradox-torque | bridge-completion-led | paradox vs torque's stronger natural rotation | paradox absorbed by torque's drift |

Templated tells deliberately avoided in this batch:
- `Practitioners with clean X tend to…` — 0 hits (was SCALE-5's 4x issue)
- `tend to` verb — 0 hits (SCALE-5 hit 7x; rewrites brought it to 3x; this batch avoids it)

Family vocabulary preserved as authorized (pixie, atomic, symposium, stepping,
paradox, rotation, body, etc.).

## 1. magellan — 3 ADD, pixie + legover

**Rationale.** Extends the pixie bridge to a sixth family base (legover).
PassBack records this row as 'Pixie near Legover' — the `near` positional
carries no ADD weight (matching the SS-ruling pattern; Q3 in the Red queue
would ratify the class rule).

**short_description**
Pixie's same-inside set applied on the legover base — the bag passes behind the planted leg as the body executes the leg-over dex sequence.

**execution_summary**
Open with a toe set; the first kick lands same-side inside (pixie), sending the bag behind the supporting leg. The body completes the canonical legover motion — the second leg passing over the supporting leg — with the bag landing on a same-toe delay. The pixie opener is the only addition over canonical legover; the leg-over geometry stays intact.

**learning_notes**
Pixie's tight first kick competes with legover's natural leg-passing reach. The miss is a too-compact opener that crowds the leg-pass; keep the pixie set compact only on the foot, leaving the body free for the leg-over rotation.

**prerequisite_notes**
Legover (2 ADD) provides the leg-passing geometry. Smear (3 ADD, pixie + mirage; SCALE-5 pilot) and pigbeater (4 ADD, pixie + eggbeater; SCALE-1 pilot) cover pixie on rotational and deeper leg-over bases. PassBack records this row as 'Pixie near Legover' — the `near` positional carries no ADD weight.

## 2. symposium-mirage — 3 ADD, symposium + mirage

**Rationale.** Symposium bridge foundation — underneath every higher symposium
compound in the dictionary. Until now the symposium bridge has been
whirl-heavy (symposium-whirl, paradox-symposium-whirl, spinning-symposium-whirl,
mullet, montage from SCALE-2); symposium-mirage adds the mirage-base entry.

**short_description**
The symposium body line carries through mirage's rotational dex — shoulders aligned and hips set on the symposium frame as the bag completes the in-to-out rotation.

**execution_summary**
Set the symposium posture before the rotation begins. The first kick drives outside the supporting leg in mirage's canonical pattern; the body holds the symposium line across the rotation; recovery lands on the opposite-toe delay with the posture intact. Symposium's signature is the held body alignment through the entire trick, not a momentary pose.

**learning_notes**
Posture collapse mid-rotation is the failure mode. Clean mirage finishes with the body somewhat relaxed; symposium-mirage demands the line stay locked across both kicks. Lock the posture before the rotation initiates, not after.

**prerequisite_notes**
Mirage (2 ADD) gives the rotational dex base. Symposium-mirage is the simplest symposium compound — symposium-whirl (4 ADD, SCALE-2 pilot), paradox-symposium-whirl + spinning-symposium-whirl (5 ADD pilots), mullet (6 ADD pilot), and montage (7 ADD pilot) all build on this row's held-posture discipline.

## 3. atom-smasher — 4 ADD, atomic + mirage (pt10 rotational +2)

**Rationale.** Demonstrates pt10's atomic-on-rotational rule: atomic = +1
non-rotational, +2 rotational. Atom-smasher's +2 comes from mirage's rotational
base. Pairs with atomic-butterfly (this batch) for direct comparison.

**short_description**
Atomic on the mirage rotational base — the atomic body angle delivers a +2 contribution on a rotational base, distinguishing mirage-base atomic compounds from non-rotational ones.

**execution_summary**
The body sets the atomic angle from the toe-set position; the first kick reaches outside the supporting leg in mirage's canonical pattern. The body's atomic-set body weighting commits across the rotation — the second kick comes inside the supporting leg, completing the outside-then-inside dex pattern that defines the atom-smasher sequence. Recovery lands on the opposite-toe delay.

**learning_notes**
The atomic body angle is the load-bearing element — it's what reaches the +2 ADD on a rotational base rather than the +1 atomic delivers on a non-rotational one. Half-committing the atomic body collapses the trick to a +1 mirage compound.

**prerequisite_notes**
Mirage (2 ADD) is the rotational foundation; the atomic body angle distinguishes atom-smasher from any +1 modifier on mirage. Atomic-butterfly (4 ADD, this batch) is the non-rotational sibling — atomic delivers +1 there because butterfly isn't rotational. The two atomic rows side-by-side surface the rotational-bonus rule directly.

## 4. atomic-butterfly — 4 ADD, atomic + butterfly

**Rationale.** Non-rotational pair to atom-smasher. The two rows together
teach pt10's atomic-on-rotational vs atomic-on-non-rotational distinction.
FM also records this row as 'Legbeater'.

**short_description**
Where atom-smasher's mirage base gives atomic the +2 rotational bonus, atomic-butterfly gets the standard +1 — butterfly is non-rotational, so atomic delivers its normal weight on the wing.

**execution_summary**
Open in butterfly's wing setup; the first kick reaches same-side outside in the canonical butterfly motion. The atomic set commits the body angle across the wing; the second kick reaches opposite-side outside as the body completes the wing pattern, landing in an opposite clipper cross-body delay. The atomic body weighting holds through both kicks — it's a frame, not just the opener.

**learning_notes**
Atom-smasher (atomic + mirage) gets +2 from atomic because mirage is rotational; atomic-butterfly gets +1 because butterfly is not. The common miss isn't a body error — it's expecting the rotational bonus to apply and over-committing the atomic body angle in compensation. Trust the +1 reading.

**prerequisite_notes**
Butterfly (3 ADD) is the wing foundation. Atom-smasher (4 ADD, this batch) gives the same atomic modifier on a rotational base for direct comparison. Scrambled-eggbeater (3 ADD, atomic + pickup; SCALE-1 pilot) is another non-rotational atomic compound. FM records this row under the alternate name 'Legbeater'.

## 5. tombstone — 4 ADD, stepping + drifter

**Rationale.** Extends the stepping bridge to a third family base
(drifter). Stepping previously covered whirl (SCALE-2) and butterfly via
ripwalk (SCALE-5). Tombstone adds the rotational-stall family.

**short_description**
Stepping's foot-relocation rides through drifter's rotation-stall — the supporting foot taps to a new position mid-trick while the body completes the miraging clipper-stall.

**execution_summary**
Drifter's rotation initiates from clipper-stall; mid-rotation, the supporting foot taps or steps to a new position. The body continues through the rotation as the planted leg has moved; the clipper landing accepts the bag with the body now over the relocated support. Recovery holds the stall as the rotation completes.

**learning_notes**
Drifter's rotation needs the planted anchor through its first half; stepping too early steals that anchor. The step must arrive after the rotation is well-established. The stepping action is a beat inside the rotation, not a separate movement between phases.

**prerequisite_notes**
Drifter (3 ADD; SCALE-5 pilot) carries the rotation-stall integration. Stepping-whirl (4 ADD; SCALE-2 pilot) gives the stepping mechanic on a rotational base; ripwalk (4 ADD, stepping + butterfly; SCALE-5 pilot) covers stepping on a non-rotational base. Tombstone extends the stepping bridge to drifter.

## 6. paradox-torque — 5 ADD, paradox + torque

**Rationale.** Completes the paradox bridge to the torque family. Now
paradox covers mirage (SCALE-5), whirl (SCALE-2), blender (SCALE-3),
drifter (SCALE-5), and torque (this row) — five major rotational bases.

**short_description**
Completing the paradox bridge to the torque family — paradox direction commits across torque's rotational frame, the body resetting same-side at landing against the natural rotational drift.

**execution_summary**
Torque's rotation initiates through the kick sequence — the body executes the canonical mirage-on-osis frame. The paradox marker forces the body to commit same-side across the rotation, against the natural opposite-side drift torque carries. The kick lands as the rotation completes; recovery holds the paradox commitment through the delay.

**learning_notes**
Torque's natural rotation drifts the body opposite-side, harder to override than on simpler bases. A half-committed paradox gets absorbed by torque's frame and the trick reads as a clean torque. The paradox direction must commit before the rotation builds momentum, not during.

**prerequisite_notes**
Torque (4 ADD) is the rotational frame. The paradox bridge now spans paradox-mirage (3 ADD, SCALE-5), paradox-whirl (4 ADD, SCALE-2), paradox-blender (5 ADD, SCALE-3), paradox-drifter (4 ADD, SCALE-5), and paradox-torque (this row, 5 ADD) — paradox direction practiced on every major rotational base. Spinal-tap (5 ADD, torque + tapping; SCALE-1 pilot) is the same-tier torque sibling.
