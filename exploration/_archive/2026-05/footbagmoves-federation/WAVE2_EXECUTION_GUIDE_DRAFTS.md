## 1. Matador (Nuclear Butterfly, 5 ADD)

**shortDescription (UX2 hero):**
> A 5-ADD butterfly compound where nuclear's paradox-atomic +2 weighting layers onto the butterfly frame.

**executionSummary:**
> From a clipper set, body opens with a same-side out kick carrying both dex and paradox direction. Body completes with an opposite-side out kick and lands cross-body in a clipper delay. The nuclear modifier is what makes this a 5-ADD trick rather than a 4-ADD same-out-then-op-out butterfly: nuclear's +2 ADD comes from the paradox-atomic body weighting that the same-out kick carries explicitly.
>
> Operationally the trick is four steps: clipper start, same-out (dex)(pdx), op-out (dex), op-clip cross-body delay.

**learningNotes:**
> Nuclear's +2 ADD weighting reflects the paradox-atomic body commitment (per the pt10 ruling). The most common miss is the same-out paradox: practitioners reach for the kick without committing the body direction first. The paradox marker in the operational notation is where the +2 anchors -- ensure body weight is committed before the dex.

**prerequisiteNotes:**
> Comfortable execution of any 4-ADD butterfly compound is the natural entry point. Atomic-butterfly (4 ADD) shares the most body mechanics with Matador. Pt10's ruling that nuclear=+2 grouped with the Paradox Atomic set provides the math context for understanding why this trick lands at 5 ADD rather than 4.

## 2. Phoenix (Pixie Ducking Butterfly, 5 ADD)

**shortDescription (UX2 hero):**
> A 5-ADD 3-modifier compound: pixie, ducking, and butterfly stacked in a single sequence.

**executionSummary:**
> From a toe set, body executes a same-side in kick (the pixie reset to the inside), drops into a ducking body compression, then opens to an opposite-side out kick and lands cross-body in a clipper delay. Five operational steps, three editorial modifiers (pixie + ducking + butterfly).
>
> Each modifier surfaces as a distinct step in the operational notation. Pixie shows as the Same In dex; ducking surfaces as a Duck (BOD) body-component marker mid-sequence; butterfly is the base frame the rest is hung on.

**learningNotes:**
> The challenge is holding the ducking compression through the body opening. Pixie alone is +1; pairing it with ducking on butterfly is a 3-modifier compound where each modifier's signal must stay distinct. If the ducking is shallow, the trick reads as a pixie-out-butterfly (4 ADD) rather than a Phoenix. Most miss is in the Duck-to-OpOut transition where the body decompresses too early.

**prerequisiteNotes:**
> Solid execution of pixie-butterfly (4 ADD, if exists in the family ladder) and ducking-butterfly (4 ADD) in isolation is the natural prereq. Phoenix is structurally the combination of both -- if either reads unclean in isolation, Phoenix will dilute.

## 3. Mind Bender (Ducking Paradox Blender, 6 ADD)

**shortDescription (UX2 hero):**
> A 6-ADD blender compound -- ducking and paradox layered onto the pt11 Whirling-Osis rotational base.

**executionSummary:**
> From a clipper set, drop into a ducking body compression, then execute a same-side front whirl with both dex and paradox direction. Body reverses to a back-facing spin, then lands cross-body in a same-side clipper delay. Five operational steps: clip, duck, same-front-whirl (dex)(pdx), (back)-spin (bod), same-clip cross-body.
>
> The blender base (pt11 = Whirling Osis) carries 4 ADD; ducking + paradox add 2 more. The (front) and (back) annotations in the operational notation track the body orientation across the spin transition -- this is the load-bearing rotational signature of the blender family.

**learningNotes:**
> Pairs naturally with Spender (Spinning + Paradox + Blender; also 6 ADD; Wave-1). Both are 6-ADD blender compounds; both carry paradox; the difference is body-modifier: Spender carries spinning (body rotation), Mind Bender carries ducking (body compression). Compare the two side-by-side for cross-modifier feel.

**prerequisiteNotes:**
> Comfortable execution of blender (4 ADD) and paradox-blender (5 ADD) is the natural entry point. The ducking modifier should be solid on a separate base (ducking-whirl, ducking-butterfly) before attempting Mind Bender. Spender is the natural same-tier sibling to study alongside.

## 4. Tripwalk (Quantum Butterfly, 4 ADD)

**executionSummary:**
> From a toe set, body opens with an opposite-side in kick (dex), transitions to an opposite-side out kick (dex), then lands cross-body in a clipper delay. Four operational steps; minimum-vocabulary butterfly compound. Quantum's +1 ADD (pt10 ruling) reflects the body's directional shift across the dex transition.

**learningNotes:**
> Quantum is the cleanest single-modifier example of the +1 weighting on a non-rotational base. The body-shift signal in the dex transition is the entire pedagogical point. Pairs well with the seven other 4-ADD butterfly compounds (atomic, dimwalk, ducking, parkwalk, ripwalk, sidewalk, spinning) for cross-modifier study at the same difficulty.

**prerequisiteNotes:**
> Butterfly (3 ADD) is the only direct prereq. Tripwalk is structurally the simplest possible quantum compound -- no other modifiers stack onto it. Best used as a quantum-modifier introduction page.

## 5. Legeater (Quantum Pickup, 3 ADD)

**executionSummary:**
> From a toe set, body executes an opposite-side in kick (dex), then a second opposite-side in kick (dex), then lands in a same-side toe delay. Four operational steps; ends in toe delay rather than clipper -- diagnostic of the pickup-base family.

**learningNotes:**
> Quantum's body-shift signal is the pedagogical point. The pickup base (2 ADD) means quantum's +1 lands cleanly at 3 ADD with no other modifiers. Pairs with paste (Pixie Pickup) and scrambled-eggbeater (Atomic Pickup) at the same 3-ADD pickup tier for modifier-comparison study.

**prerequisiteNotes:**
> Pickup (2 ADD) is the base. Legeater is structurally the simplest quantum compound at the lowest possible ADD value -- ideal entry point for the modifier.

## 6. Spinal Tap (Tapping Torque, 5 ADD)

**executionSummary:**
> From a toe set, body opens with an opposite-side out kick (dex), then a same-side in kick (dex), then a front-body spin, and lands cross-body in a clipper delay. Five operational steps. Tapping's +1 ADD (pt3 ruling: tapping distinct from stepping) layers onto the torque frame. Pt11 ruled torque = Miraging Osis transitively, so the full structural decomposition is tapping + miraging + osis = 1 + 1 + 3 = 5.

**learningNotes:**
> Tapping is the body-touch modifier per pt3 (distinct from stepping). At 5 ADD, Spinal Tap sits in the densest torque tier (alongside spinning-torque, paradox-torque, mobius, grave-digger). Each tier-mate uses a different body modifier on the torque frame -- useful for cross-modifier study. Note: mobius and spinning-torque share a structural decomposition (per pt11); they are pedagogically distinct as labels but identical mathematically.

**prerequisiteNotes:**
> Torque (4 ADD) is the natural base prereq. Lower-tier tapping introduction on a non-rotational base would be helpful (Tapdown / Tapping Butterfly is a Wave-2 candidate not yet shipped; if it lands later, it becomes the prereq tapping-modifier introduction).
