The operators of freestyle

Freestyle footbag is a compositional movement language. These fourteen operators are its primitives — combine them and you get every named trick in the dictionary.

I · Sets

Set operators

What sends the bag into the air.

PIX

Pixie

Compressed uptime set, leg drives the bag through a tight orbit.

PIX + BUTTERFLY → DIMWALK

AT

Atomic

Set wrapped with full-body rotation in the air.

AT + OSIS → FLUX

Q

Quantum

Set with added rotation through uptime.

Q + MIRAGE → TOE BLUR

BL

Blender

Set with low-orbit pre-set sweep into the dex.

BL + BUTTERFLY → BLENDER BUTTERFLY

FAIRY

Fairy

Set carrying an extra revolution through uptime.

FAIRY + BLUR → DOUBLE FAIRY

STEP

Stepping

Foot relocates between the set and the catch.

STEP + BUTTERFLY → RIPWALK

II · Body

Body operators

What the body does while the bag is up.

SPIN

Spinning

Full-body rotation around the vertical axis during the trick.

SPIN + TORQUE → MOBIUS

GY

Gyro

Body-rotation variant on a partial or inverted plane.

GY + BUTTERFLY → GYRO BUTTERFLY

DUCK

Ducking

The body drops under the bag mid-dex.

PIX + DUCK + BUTTERFLY → PHOENIX

PDX

Paradox

A hip pivot inserted between two dexes.

PDX + LEG-OVER → PARADOX LEG-OVER

SYMP

Symposium

An illusion combined with body rotation.

SYMP + ILLUSION → FLAIL

III · Structure

Structural concepts

Relationships across the trick.

XDEX

Cross-dex

The leg circles the bag on the opposite side of the body.

XDEX + INSIDE → CLIPPER

SAME

Same-foot

The set foot and the catch foot are the same.

SAME + BUTTERFLY → SAME-FOOT BUTTERFLY

OP

Opposite

The set foot and the catch foot are different. The conventional default.

OP + BUTTERFLY → BUTTERFLY
