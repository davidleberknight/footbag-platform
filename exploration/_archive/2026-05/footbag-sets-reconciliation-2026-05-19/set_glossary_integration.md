## 1. THE central pedagogical finding

**Source provides THE foundational doctrinal framing for sets** (verbatim, lines 12-18):

> "The 'new school' of freestyle involves a tremendous number of moves which are essentially variants of older moves, where the player performs additional difficulty (e.g., dexterities and spins) while the footbag is on its way up (at the beginning of a move, just after the set) before performing the remainder of the move. In the 'old days', most tricks (but not all) were done with the bag on its way down.
>
> There's a name for almost every trick you can possibly do with the bag while it's on its way down. Since the explosion of the concept of 'prefix' tricks — e.g., tricks on the way up — freestylers have begun to refer to new school moves using one of quite a few prefixes to denote almost entirely separable motions done with the bag on the way up, before doing an otherwise standard trick on the way down.
>
> Below is a list of most of the currently used terms for tricks on the way up — these act as prefixes that can be put in front of trick names to indicate the more complicated versions as described below. Because these are done on the way up, in many cases not affecting the fundamental execution of the trick on the way down, most freestylers think of these as 'sets'."

This is THE 2003-era foundational explanation of:
- Sets as **uptime work**
- Sets as **prefixes** (operator composition)
- Sets as **separable** from downtime trick execution
- Sets as **+ADD-contributing** operators (the "more complicated versions")

The modern Movement System Set/Uptime axis is a direct descendant of this 2003 doctrine.

## 2. Cross-cutting Wave 2 RESOLUTION CANDIDATE: Spinning vs Gyro structural distinction

The source's Components section provides NEW EVIDENCE for the 5-audit lead Wave 2 question:

> Spinning: SET > (BACK) SPIN [BOD] >
> Gyro: CLIP > (BACK) SPIN [BOD] > (same dex component)

**Source distinguishes spinning vs gyro by ANCHOR + CONTINUATION, NOT direction or magnitude.**

- **Spinning** = anchor-agnostic back-spin (any SET position)
- **Gyro** = CLIP-anchored back-spin with same-dex continuation

This is a STRUCTURAL distinction the source preserves. Modern doctrine's "spinning = full turn (360°); gyro = half turn (180°)" framing has NO source operational precedent across 5 audits.

**Implication:** The Wave 2 LEAD question now has THREE possible resolutions:
1. **Modern is correct** — full-vs-half-turn doctrine post-dates source; source is incomplete
2. **Source is correct** — spinning/gyro are anchor/continuation variants; modern doctrine has misframed the axis
3. **Both are valid lenses** — source structural distinction + modern pedagogical magnitude framing co-exist

The audit recommends surfacing as Red Wave 2 LEAD question (SW2-1).
