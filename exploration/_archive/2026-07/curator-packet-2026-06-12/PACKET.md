Two rulings that together unblock the largest doctrine-gated share of the freestyle promotion frontier. Each is framed against the **current** resolved canon (`exploration/red-consolidation/RED_RESOLVED_CANON.md`), so the parts already settled are carved out and only the genuinely-open question is put to you.

## Ruling 1 — Does `blurry` carry +2 on rotational bases, or stay flat +1?

### Already settled (not in question)
- **Blurry = +1, "stepping only."** Red 2026-05-20: *"Blurry just implies stepping; it does not imply paradox anymore."* The old `stepping paradox X` expansion is retired.
- **Rotational escalation is retired for everything except atomic.** pt10: spinning / whirling / swirling do **not** escalate to +2 on rotational bases; only `atomic` kept rotational class (+1 non-rotational / +2 rotational, rotational bases = whirl / swirl / torque / drifter / blender / dyno).

### The one open question
`atomic` is the sole precedent for an intermediate set-operator that gains +1 on rotational bases. Does `blurry` behave the same way, or does it stay flat +1 like every other operator post-pt10?

This is the only thing that separates two readings of the same rows:

| Trick | Flat +1 reading | +2-rotational reading |
|---|---:|---:|
| Blurry Whirl | blurry(+1) + whirl(3) = **4** | blurry(+2 rot) + whirl(3) = **5** |
| Blurry Torque | blurry(+1) + torque(4) = **5** | blurry(+2 rot) + torque(4) = **6** |
| Blurry Drifter | blurry(+1) + drifter(3) = **4** | blurry(+2 rot) + drifter(3) = **5** |

### Recommended default: **flat +1** (blurry does NOT escalate)
Two structural reasons:
1. pt10 retired rotational escalation for all operators but atomic. Granting blurry rotational class would re-open that, against the established direction.
2. Blurry's settled semantic is *stepping*, and stepping is flat +1 including on rotational bases (pt11). So "Blurry Whirl = stepping whirl = 4" is the reading most consistent with the 2026-05-20 ruling. The +2 reading would require blurry to mean *more* than stepping on rotational bases specifically, which the semantic ruling argues against.

If you instead rule **+2 rotational**, that is a clean answer too — it just means blurry joins atomic as a second rotational-class operator, and the 24 rows below take the higher ADD.

## Ruling 2 — How do the Down-family terminals score, and are they canonical bases?

### Already settled (not in question)
- **Down, Double-Down, Double-Over-Down, Down-Double-Down are DISTINCT tricks** (pt7). This is not "are they real" — they are. The open part is their ADD and their dictionary status.
- **Two resolved compounds already imply a base value.** Fusion = `atomic double-over-down` = 5, and Plasma = `quantum double-over-down` = 5. Atomic and quantum are both +1 (non-rotational), so **both rulings imply Double-Over-Down ≈ 4 ADD** as a terminal. The down-family scoring is therefore already half-anchored; the question is whether to make that explicit and extend it.

### The open question (three parts)
For each Down terminal (`down`, `double-down`, `double-over-down`):
- **(a) Base ADD** — what is the terminal's own ADD? (Double-Over-Down ≈ 4 is implied by Fusion/Plasma; Down and Double-Down are unanchored.)
- **(b) Dictionary status** — A: a canonical base entry (its own `freestyle_tricks` row), B: modifier-derived (`down + N`), or C: deferred until notation backfill makes the decomposition explicit?
- **(c)** Given (a)+(b), the 47 compounds built on these terminals score and promote.

### Recommended default
- **Double-Over-Down = 4** (lock the value Fusion + Plasma already use).
- **Option A — canonical base entries** for the three Down terminals, since pt7 already ruled them distinct tricks; modifier-derived (B) would re-litigate that. A base ADD for Down and Double-Down is the only genuinely new number you'd need to set.

### What your answer moves: the 47 dod-ddd rows
Distribution: **31 Double-Down**, **12 Double-Over-Down**, **2 Symposium-Down** (+ DDD variants). **Honest scope:** **34 are blocked by the Down-terminal score alone** and promote directly on this ruling; the other **13 also carry a second blocker** and need that too:
- 5 atomic-rotational (Atomic Double Over Down, Atomic far Double Down, …) — also waits on atomic-Q3
- 4 nuclear (Nuclear DDD / DOD / far Double Down, Pogo Nuclear far Double Down)
- 4 shooting (Shooting DDD / Star / far Double Down, Gyro Shooting Double Down) — `shooting` is an undefined folk operator

(`Frantic far DOD Swirl` and `Flailing ss Symp DOD` are NOT second-blocked — frantic = pixie-quantum and flailing = symposium-illusioning are both already-resolved operator stacks.)
