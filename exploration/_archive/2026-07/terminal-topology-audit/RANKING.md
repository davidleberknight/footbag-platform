# Terminal-Topology Audit — Complete Terminal-Identity Ranking

**The shape of the documented trick universe: every terminal identity in one list, ranked by recursive descendant count.** Clipper-stall (328) and toe-stall (252) dominate everything: they are the grandparent surface roots every movement family lands into, and they dwarf the largest movement family (osis, 84). They are themselves family parents and are NOT omitted because they are surfaces; they simply do not appear as ordinary glossary family cards because they are too broad to be educationally useful. The movement families (Osis, Whirl, Mirage, Legover, ...) emerge beneath those two dominant roots, which is why the glossary surfaces *them* as cards. Inside-stall is both a promoted family parent and a terminal surface root, so it appears naturally in the ranking at its descendant count (11).

Clipper Stall and Toe Stall dominate the topology as terminal surface roots. Once those two broad roots are set aside, the largest movement families emerge naturally from the data: **Osis, Whirl, Legover, Mirage, Butterfly, Illusion, Swirl, and Pickup** occupy the top of the ranking and together account for roughly two-thirds of the documented vocabulary (436 of 651 active tricks; 76% of those with a terminal family). This matters because the ranking was not engineered to produce that roster: the major family parents fall directly out of the topology on their own, so the family system is empirical, not arbitrary.

Read-only. Generated from `freestyle_tricks` by the method in `METHODOLOGY.md`. `rec` = recursive descendants (for a surface root, all tricks landing on it; for a family, its subtree; no-notation tricks inherit their family's surface). Every row is a terminal identity; the badge is its role.
