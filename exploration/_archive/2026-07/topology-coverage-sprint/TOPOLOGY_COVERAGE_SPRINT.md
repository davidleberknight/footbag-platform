# Topology Coverage Sprint

Read-only analysis. No notation authored, no tricks promoted, no doctrine or data changed.

## Purpose
The four topology audits (terminal, entry, body, component) are formula-grounded: they read `operational_notation`. **178 of 651 active tricks (27.3%) have no notation**, so they are invisible to the formula-grounded layers. This is not a dictionary-completeness nicety; it is a topology-coverage problem, because every histogram and every empirical conclusion currently rests on a 73% sample of the documented universe.

## A necessary refinement of the premise
"Invisible to all four audits" is only half true, and the half that is false reshapes the sprint:

- The notation-less tricks **are** invisible to the formula layers: terminal surface counts, entry surface counts, and the entire component ecology.
- They are **not** invisible to the membership/family layers: **167 of the 178 still carry a `trick_family`** (so they appear in the family recursive ranking), and many carry entry/body ecosystem memberships. Only **11 are fully invisible** (no family, no membership, no notation).

So the audit **rankings are robust today**. What the missing 27% actually costs is the **component ecology** (under-counted across the board) and the **absolute surface / dex / catch magnitudes**. The sprint goal is therefore precision of the formula-grounded layers, not rescuing the rankings.

## Goal
Raise the share of active tricks that participate in the formula-grounded audits (component + surfaces), prioritizing the lineages that carry the most documented weight.

## Questions answered
1. **How many participate in all four audits fully?** 473 (72.7%) carry notation and so appear in the component + surface layers; all but the 11 fully-invisible appear in at least the membership/family layers.
2. **Which remain topology-invisible?** The 178 notation-less tricks (`TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_GAPS.md`); the hard core is the 11 with neither family nor membership.
3. **Which missing formulas have the greatest impact?** By family: whirl (23 missing), mirage (20), butterfly (15), swirl (11), legover (10). By entry: symposium (20), stepping (19), fairy (15), pixie (15), paradox (10). By body: spinning (19), diving (18). (`TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_TARGETS.md`.)
4. **What coverage is realistically achievable next sprint?** 72.7% to **92.8%** by completing the 131 HIGH-impact base-derivable tricks; 94.6% if all 143 base-derivable are done. 100% requires authoring 35 formulas from scratch (out of next-sprint scope).

## Method
Each notation-less trick was inventoried with slug, ADD, family, entry/timing ecosystem, body ecosystem, canonical status, base trick, estimated difficulty (LOW = base already has notation, so the compound formula is derived; MED/HIGH = authored), and topology impact (HIGH = major family or major ecosystem; MEDIUM = minor lineage; LOW = isolated). Full table in `TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_GAPS.md`.

## Deliverables
- `TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_BASELINE.md` - current statistics and per-audit participation.
- `TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_GAPS.md` - the 178 notation-less tricks, sorted by impact.
- `TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_TARGETS.md` - recommended target groups (family / entry / body).
- `TOPOLOGY_COVERAGE_PROJECTION.md` - projected histogram shifts; robust vs sensitive conclusions.

## Robust vs sensitive (the conclusion that should drive the sprint)
- **Robust today:** the family ranking, the entry-ecosystem ranking, the body-ecosystem ranking. All membership/family based.
- **Sensitive today:** the whole component ecology (cross-body dominance magnitude, dex and catch frequencies), and the absolute surface counts (clipper-stall 328 / toe-stall 252, toe-set 207 / clip-set 197). All formula based.

The missing set even skews slightly higher-ADD than the documented set (4.4 vs 4.2 average), so it carries proportionally more components than its headcount; the component ecology is the single most under-reported audit.

## Recommended Sprint Order
The smallest set of additions for the largest empirical gain is the **131 HIGH-impact, base-derivable** tricks, run family-by-family from the top, because those families also dominate the terminal and component audits:

1. **whirl (23) -> mirage (20) -> butterfly (15) -> swirl (11) -> legover (10).** 79 tricks, all clipper/toe dominant, all derivable from a based formula. This alone lifts notation coverage from 72.7% to ~84.8% and fills the surface + component detail for the five largest terminal lineages.
2. **illusion (9), osis (9), torque (8).** +26 tricks, ~88.8%.
3. **The remaining base-derivable tail** (clipper, inside-stall, pickup, blender, eggbeater, and the rest), reaching the 131 / ~92.8% target.
4. **Out of next-sprint scope:** the 35 tricks with no based formula (authored from scratch) and the 11 fully-invisible tricks (which need a family assignment before notation even helps).

Because the family/ecosystem rankings are already robust, this order is chosen to maximize **component and surface** coverage per unit of effort, not to change any ranking. The objective is empirical coverage of the topology model, not raw trick count.
