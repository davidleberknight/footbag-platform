## The atomic / spinning / paradox caveats
- **atomic (7):** the tokenizer marked 5 as A, but atomic's X-Dex notation is a **held doctrine** (atomic/quantum placement). Treat all atomic as gated on that one ruling, not grammar-derivable - it overlaps the 18 atomic structures already in the notation tail.
- **spinning (7) / gyro (4):** mostly opaque (E) - these are `Spinning <opaque>` / `Gyro <opaque>` folk names where the spin is ratified but the base is not recoverable. The spin operator is not the blocker; the unknown base is.
- **paradox (6):** 1 A, 5 E - same pattern; paradox is ratified, the bases are opaque.

## Reading
The sprint's biggest ecosystem wins are **the set/compound operators** (sailing 15, railing 7, inward 7, double/double-dex 21, symposium 9) - all now fully recoverable - and the **entry operators** (pixie 12, stepping 9, fairy 6). The biggest remaining single-ruling levers are **zulu (29), symple (14), alpine (14), frantic (11), slapping (11)**, of which **frantic and neutron may already be covered** by the Frantic set and the nuclear-family pt14 ruling - check those two first. Everything else (spinning/gyro/paradox with opaque bases, dragon/merlin/blacula) is genuine source authoring.
