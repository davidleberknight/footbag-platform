## Why this placement (educational flow)

1. **Each histogram lands at its point of introduction.** The entry chart appears exactly where sets are first explained; the family chart appears the instant the family roster is introduced, so each answers "why does this structure exist?" before the reader can ask.
2. **Entry sets up a symmetry that terminal pays off.** Because Timing & Sets precedes Core Trick Families, the reader first learns that two set surfaces dominate how tricks begin, then meets the same two surfaces dominating how they end. The family chart's first line explicitly calls back ("just as two surfaces dominate the entry..."). One idea, seen twice, locks in.
3. **The explanatory text does the doctrinal work, not the bars.** The bars show dominance; the sentence converts the framing from "ratified/curated" to "measured," which is the entire point of surfacing the audits.
4. **Nothing else moves.** Reading the Dictionary, Contact Surfaces, Dexterities, the five-kinds list, the roster, and the cards are untouched; the two charts plus two short paragraphs are purely additive, preserving the existing simplicity.

The body and component histograms would follow the same pattern much deeper in the page (Modifiers & Operators / advanced reference), well past this 25%; per the synthesis, the component chart stays in the advanced tier to avoid noise.
