# Component-Ecology Audit — Movement-Component Frequency

**The fourth audit counts components, not tricks.** A trick contributes one occurrence per component it contains (two op-in dexes count twice). Formula atoms (catches, dexes, dex-features, body tokens, entry sets) are occurrence-counted from `operational_notation` (473 tricks carry a formula); set ecosystems and operator overlays have no atomic token and are counted per-trick from curated membership (651 active tricks). `trick_family` is not used.

Named family-dexes (mirage-dex, legover-dex) and family components (osis, whirl, butterfly) are not atomic: they are *compositions* of the atoms below (e.g. whirl = a leggy-in dex landing in a clipper catch). They are out of scope for atomic counting and discussed in the comparison section.

## Comparison with the Three Topology Audits

The component view **confirms all three prior audits** and adds a layer they could not see.

- **Terminal:** the catch components reproduce the terminal audit exactly. clipper-catch (243) and toe-catch (196) dwarf every other catch, mirroring clipper-stall / toe-stall as the dominant terminal surfaces.
- **Entry:** the set components reproduce the entry audit. toe-set (207) and clipper-set (197) are the dominant entry surfaces.
- **Body:** the body tokens confirm the body audit's leaders (spin, duck), but the component view exposes that the spin *occurrence* (206) far exceeds the spinning *ecosystem* (78). Rotational bases (osis, torque) embed a spin in their own structure, so rotation is structurally far more pervasive than the "spinning modifier" count implies. This is the body audit's conflation, made visible.
- **New: the dex layer.** The topology audits never surfaced the dexes. The most common atomic moves are op-in (220), op-out (194), same-out (144), and same-in (135) dexes, plus **cross-body (246), the single most common feature, present in over half of all tricks.**

**Do the major family parents emerge from component frequency?** Yes, as compositions of the top atoms, not as labels:
- whirl = a leggy/op-in dex + clipper catch (both top-3 components)
- osis = a spin + clipper catch (both top-5)
- mirage = an op-in dex + toe catch (both top-7)
- butterfly = an op-out dex + clipper catch

The major families are precisely the recurring high-frequency dex+catch combinations, which is independent corroboration of the terminal audit's finding that those families emerge from the data rather than from doctrine.
