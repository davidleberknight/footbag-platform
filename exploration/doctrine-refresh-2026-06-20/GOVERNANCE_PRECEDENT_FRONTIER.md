# Governance / Precedent Frontier (2026-06-20)

**Status: standing first-class work queue.** The four precedent buckets below are the active backlog,
maintained **separately from doctrine** (`DOCTRINE_AUDIT.md` §3) and from identification. New members
are cross-listed here as later audits surface them (e.g. `atomic-gyro-torque` from
`ATOMIC_QUANTUM_NUCLEAR_READINESS.md` §2 joins the set-then-gyro bucket).

These are **topology-precedent questions** — not Red doctrine — that still block promotions: how an
operator places/inserts on a base, and whether a structure with no prior exemplar is authorable. Each
is a **single curator precedent ruling** that unlocks its whole band; none needs Red.

Sized by scanning the 1,375-slug tracked-names frontier (`src/content/freestyleTrackedNames.ts`).

## Reading

- The governance/precedent frontier is a **modest tail (~25–40 slugs), not a large hidden blocker.**
  It resolves to roughly **4 curator precedent rulings** (whirling-on-BOD generalization, gyro
  insertion-into-set, paradox-miraging placement, same-operator-twice policy), each unlocking its band.
- Against the 1,375-slug tracked frontier, the **fully-blocked subset is ~6–7%**: Weaving (~30, Red) +
  this governance/precedent tail (~25–40, curator) + identification (dragon/refraction/clipper-symposium-whirl
  band, ~12). The remaining **~90%+ is mechanically authorable** via the mirror/compose pipeline.
- So "the frontier is execution-bound, not doctrine-bound" **holds** — with the precise qualifier that
  the residual blockers are: **Red = Weaving only**; **curator = ~4 precedent rulings**; **identification
  = the dragon/refraction band**.
