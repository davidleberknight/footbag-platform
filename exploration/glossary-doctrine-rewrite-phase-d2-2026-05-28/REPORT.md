## 3. Doctrine codifications this report adds (beyond the family-doctrine drafts)

The family-doctrine report already authored: the family definition + 5 criteria, the three-tier model, the Implicit-contacts explainer, and the Lineages-vs-ecosystems explainer. This report authors the three areas it did not:

### 3a. Interpretation Doctrine (§composition rewrite) — core statement
> **Interpretation Doctrine.** An **interpretation** (the `≡` reading on line 1) describes *how a trick can be read* — it is **descriptive**, never a recipe you apply to other tricks. Readings arrive by different routes, and the route matters:
> - **Editorial equivalence** (curator-authored chain): *smear ≡ pixie mirage*.
> - **Historical derivation** (a naming tradition): *eggbeater ≡ atomic legover* — two vocabularies describing one movement.
> - **Compositional descent** (a named lineage built from canonicals): *drifter ≡ miraging clipper*.
> - **Structural parse** (the parser reading the canonical name) and **policy-dependent** readings (a reading that holds only under a curator ruling).
>
> **The boundary that older vocabularies lost:** *historical derivation ≠ productive modifier.* "eggbeater ≡ atomic legover" does **not** make *eggbeater* an operator you can apply — there is no "eggbeater-ing" another base. A reading tells you what a trick *is equivalent to*; a modifier ecosystem (pixie, ducking) tells you what you can *do to* a base. Conflating the two is the single most common vocabulary error the platform exists to prevent.
>
> And: **an interpretation is not always doctrine.** Some readings are ratified canonical structure; others are observational or policy-dependent and may change. Line 1's `≡` shows the reading; it does not promise the reading is settled.

- **Flagship (historical-derivation):** eggbeater.
- **Flagship (compositional-descendant):** drifter.

### 3b. Foundational vs Alternative Surfaces (§surfaces) — core statement
> **Foundational surfaces** — toe and clipper — are where *most* tricks terminate. They are base primitives, central to notation and neighborhoods, but **not browse families**: grouping by "everything that ends on clipper" buries the whole dictionary together and teaches nothing.
> **Alternative surfaces** — sole, heel, cloud, cross-body sole, flying variants, head/neck/shoulder — are *unusual catch surfaces*, not movement lineages. They are their own browse axis (Movement system → Alternative surfaces), separate from families, because a contact surface is not a terminal *mechanic*.

### 3c. Governance states (advanced-reference) — core statement
> **Documentation ≠ canonization.** The platform tracks more names than it endorses, on purpose. A name can be:
> - **Canonical** — a ratified trick with a structural identity and a detail page;
> - **Observationally represented** — seen in the corpus and recorded, but not endorsed as canonical structure;
> - **Unresolved** — a name whose decomposition or status is still open (carries a pending marker);
> - **Tracked** — recorded for completeness with no canonical claim;
> - **Promoted** — moved from observational/tracked into canonical after a ruling.
> Tracking a name is evidence it exists in the culture, not a claim that it is canonical.

## 8. Risks if implemented too aggressively (deliverable 8)

- **Over-hardening unresolved doctrine.** Publishing a closed descendant-lineage roster or a final symposium/paradox class before the ruling makes the glossary wrong the day a ruling lands. → use transitional language; the fuzzy-boundary clause is the safety valve.
- **Big structural reorder.** Renumbering/moving sections breaks inbound `#anchor` deep-links (the glossary preserves several legacy anchors deliberately, e.g. `#derivation-atlas`, `#compression-vs-alternate-derivation`). → keep the spine; nest doctrine into existing sections; preserve anchors.
- **Blurring under simplification pressure.** A "cleaner" glossary that merges ecosystem/family or surface/family to read simpler would violate the core constraint. → every rewrite must *sharpen*; the five-kinds + lineage-vs-ecosystem tables are the guardrails.
- **One-shot rewrite.** Rewriting §families and §composition simultaneously in one pass risks contradiction and an unreviewable diff. → sequence them; land framing first.
- **Glossary↔trick-detail drift.** If glossary adopts "parent family / descendant lineage" but the trick-detail prose still says "branch family," readers see two vocabularies. → the terminology sweep (§7) must cover both surfaces in the same slice.
- **Doc-governance.** Any canonical-doc reflection (VIEW_CATALOG/DESIGN_DECISIONS) needs explicit approval and must avoid implementation-status language. → doc-sync is a gated final step, not part of the prose edits.
