## Strategy

Each row classifies into one of three buckets:

- **(a) derivable safely now** — base has a published canonical-bracket JOB and the modifier composes mechanically (single prefix token like spinning/stepping/ducking/symposium/atomic/pixie/paradox into the entry slot of an atom base). Derived JOB takes the form `<modifier-prefix> + <base JOB-tail>` or, more often, follows the canonical-bracket convention `<modifier-flag> > <base operational chain>` documented per-modifier in the existing curator-authored entries.
- **(b) needs curator review** — composite modifiers (blurry, surging, furious, gyro), unresolved-compound flag (`UNRESOLVED_COMPOUNDS`), nested compound base (paradox-blender, paradox-torque, paradox-whirl with whirl-base ambiguity), or any row where the timing/topology (`(no plant while)`, `(midtime)`, `(back)`) introduces sequencing ambiguity the curator hasn't pinned.
- **(c) should remain pending** — atomic-bracket re-bracketing requires curator-doctrine work (see `PUBLICATION_STANDARDS.md` dual-convention rule). Composite-modifier rows that depend on Wave-2 doctrine answers.

## Important constraints

- Do NOT auto-overwrite curator-authored JOB strings (`red_corrections` rows with `operational_notation` correction).
- Do NOT derive when the base trick lacks an authored JOB itself (chained dependency).
- Mark each derived row's `operational_notation_source` as `derived_2026_05_24` so future curator passes can find them quickly.
- Confidence ≠ correctness: even "derivable safely" rows should be flagged for curator drive-by approval before locking.
