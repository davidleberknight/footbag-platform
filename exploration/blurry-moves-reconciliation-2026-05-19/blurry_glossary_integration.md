## 1. Major findings (must surface in any glossary integration)

1. **2-source confirmation of Red pt11 doctrine.** blurryMoves source has 11 explicit "Blurry X (Stepping Paradox X)" folk-name/structural-reading pairs — combined with paradoxMoves's 11 pairs, the 2-source corpus provides ~22 historical-precedent pairs for Red pt11 doctrine. Cross-validation complete.

2. **CRITICAL: First "rake" element appearance in any reconciliation source.** The source's 7-ADD compound "Stepping P.S. Whirling x-body Rake" uses `OP X-BODY RAKE [XBD] [DEL] [DEX]` as a single compound token. This is the FIRST 2003-era "rake" precedent in any reconciliation source we have reviewed. **Direct historical-precedent for the user's Sprint 6 rake doctrine (2026-05-19) — 23-year pedigree.**

3. **Food Processor multi-reading confirmation.** Source's "Blurry Blender" parenthetical reading matches modern chain registry's primary reading (alongside "stepping paradox blender" Red pt15-locked). Strong source precedent.

4. **4 NEW compounds unique to blurryMoves** (not in paradoxMoves): Symposium Blizzard (5), Symposium Blur (5), Food Processor (Blurry Blender) (6), Stepping P.S. Whirling x-body Rake (7).

5. **New operational tokens NOT in modern ATAM grammar §13:**
   - `OP IN/OUT [DEX]` — alternation marker (Symposium Blur; first appearance in reconciliation corpus)
   - `OP X-BODY RAKE [XBD] [DEL] [DEX]` — compound rake token (Stepping P.S. Whirling x-body Rake)
   - Multiple [BOD] flags in same dex segment (Symposium Blizzard / Symposium Blur)

6. **Paradox-without-PDX-flag pattern persists.** 4 of 18 blurry compounds use "paradox" in name but operational notation lacks [PDX] flag. Same inconsistency rate as paradoxMoves (11 of 57 = 19% vs blurryMoves 4 of 18 = 22%).
