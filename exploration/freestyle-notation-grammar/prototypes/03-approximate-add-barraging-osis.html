Prototype notes — approximate ADD

What this prototype is testing

Whether ADD disagreement renders as actionable editorial signal rather than as "the parser is wrong" or "the dictionary is wrong." A learner reading this page should understand: math says 4, asserted is 5, the difference is interesting and unresolved.

What feels successful

  • The Δ is foregrounded in the header panel — not buried — so the disagreement is the first thing a reviewer sees.
  • The "three resolution paths" section turns the Δ into a decision tree rather than a vague flag. Editorial users can scan and pattern-match.
  • The lineage view's comparative-sibling observation surfaces a real ontology hypothesis (other modifiers compute exactly; barraging is the outlier; suggests weight is wrong) without making the call.
  • Asserted ADD remains visually authoritative (large, dark) and computed appears in a warning color — the right hierarchy.

What feels overloaded

  • The "Δ +1 disagreement" cell in the header panel is signal-rich but adds a fourth ADD value next to asserted/computed — three numbers visible. Probably one too many for at-a-glance reading; could collapse "Δ" into a tooltip on the computed value.
  • The modifier-layering box has TWO opinions inline ("+1 ADD" and "→ but +2 if asserted is correct"). Adventurous editorially but cluttered. Phase-3 might let users toggle "show structural" / "show asserted-back-derived".

Ontology concerns exposed

  • The Δ has no provenance. Without commit/audit history, a reviewer can't tell whether the asserted-5 was historical default, Red-endorsed, or a typo. Phase-3 production data: every approximate row should link to its review_status and ideally the source CSV row that asserts it.
  • Modifier-weight changes ripple. If James decides barraging is +2 on osis, what happens to other barraging compounds? The modifier table is shared. The QC report needs to surface "if you change this weight, here are the N other rows affected."
  • "Approximate" implies a numerical tolerance. The status name is slightly misleading — it's not "the math is approximately right," it's "the math disagrees." A rename to computed_disagrees or add_conflict might be more honest. Defer to Phase-3 vocabulary review.
