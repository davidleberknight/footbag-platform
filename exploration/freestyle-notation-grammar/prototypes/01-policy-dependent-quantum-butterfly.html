Prototype notes — policy-dependent

What this prototype is testing

Whether policy_tokens[] can be communicated UI-side without collapsing the structural-parse value. Specifically: a row with a contested-weight token should still parse fully, and the visualization should distinguish "the math agrees but the answer is policy-pending" from both "exact" and "approximate".

What feels successful

  • quantum renders in its primary semantic role (set), not as a separate "policy" role. Orthogonal flagging works.
  • The ? suffix on the policy-role span + amber tone of the badge convey "approach with caution" without screaming error.
  • "Asserted ADD: — (deferred)" is honest about the missing editorial value. Many UIs would show "0" or "Unknown" — this version explains.
  • The lineage tier-pill ← here (deferred) places the row in its structural neighborhood while signaling its non-active status.

What feels overloaded

  • Three concurrent signals on the modifier-layering box: "+1 ADD (policy-pending)", a separate "policy" badge in the corner, AND the role-color underline. Phase-3 reduction: keep the role color and ONE of the textual signals.
  • The "(pending policy ratification)" tail-text on the total feels redundant given the badge above.

Ontology concerns exposed

  • Hypothetical-row tension. This prototype is a synthetic compound; quantum-butterfly doesn't sit in the active dictionary. The visualization works for the speculative case but the UI needs an explicit "synthetic / for-illustration" affordance to prevent confusing real users. Production version: only render real rows; if a row is policy-deferred at upstream curation, show a "candidate" placeholder or 404.
  • Policy weight presented as fact. The "+1 ADD (policy-pending)" wording assumes the registered weight is approximately right. If Red rules quantum is +2, the formula display is misleading. Phase-3 mitigation: display weights from policy-pending tokens with an explicit "current registered" qualifier.
  • "Quantum" in lineage as gray sibling. Should deferred rows appear in family lineage at all? Pedagogically yes (shows the shape of the family); editorially uncertain (might confuse "what is and isn't a real trick").
