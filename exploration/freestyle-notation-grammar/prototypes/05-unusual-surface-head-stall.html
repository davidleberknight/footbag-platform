Prototype notes — unusual-surface

What this prototype is testing

Whether surface-role tokens (delay_surface, unusual_surface) get visualized appropriately in foundational stall rows AND whether the parser's D1-self-atom-clears-roles behavior loses important information.

What feels successful

  • The "stalls form a cross-family lens" framing introduces a useful pedagogical category that doesn't exist in trick_family. Phase-3 could expose this as a ?view=surface projection alongside the existing ?view=family and ?view=sets.
  • The lineage flat-list at 1 ADD makes the foundational-tier coverage gap visceral — many stalls, mostly without media. Aligns with the foundational-tier media-curation backlog already documented.
  • The "compounds that use a head-stall surface: none" observation is a real editorial finding the visualization surfaces — head-stall is a stall but no compound builds on it. Suggests either head-stall is genuinely terminal-only or a curation gap.

What feels overloaded

  • The "(stall surface, distinct row)" inline annotation on clipper in the lineage is a footnote inside a list. Same for cloud-stall's "(unusual-surface)" tag. Phase-3 should pick a uniform treatment — maybe a tiny pill suffix per lineage row indicating its surface category — rather than ad-hoc parenthetical notes.
  • The decomposition table's unusual_surface: — row with the long parser-limitation explanation is the most educational content on the page but visually disrupts the otherwise-clean empty-roles list. A "ⓘ parser note" callout box might be cleaner than mixing the limitation explanation into the table.

Ontology concerns exposed

  • D1 clears the role buckets. When self-atom recognition fires, the per-token classifications get wiped. This is correct for ADD math (avoids double-counting) but loses the descriptive information about which tokens carried which roles. For visualization, knowing "head was classified as unusual_surface" is useful even though the atom owns the ADD. Phase-3 parser refinement: preserve the pre-D1 classifications in a separate descriptive_roles bucket, leave the ADD-contributing buckets cleared. Then the decomposition table can show the descriptive roles without affecting compute.
  • Stalls don't have a unified family. Each stall is its own trick_family ("head-stall", "toe-stall"...). The pedagogical "stalls" category is implicit in category = 'surface' only. Either: (a) introduce a meta-family "stalls" in the ontology, (b) live with the cross-family-projection-via-category pattern. The proposal didn't anticipate this; worth resolving in Phase-3 family-lineage design.
  • "clipper" is double-listed. It appears as a delay-surface token (in compound notation) AND as a 1-ADD canonical row in its own right (the body / kick variant). The lineage view conflates these. Phase-3 needs to decide: are they the same entity or two? CANONICALIZATION_POLICY treats clipper as a body canonical and clipper-stall as a stall surface; the parser registries put "clipper" in delay_surface. Inconsistent.
