# SCALE-1a Prose Drafts

Movement-first prose for the 10 SCALE-1 batch tricks. Each row carries four columns
(`short_description`, `execution_summary`, `learning_notes`, `prerequisite_notes`)
authored against the universal UX3 shell.

**Style discipline applied:**
- Movement clarity > ontology explanation
- "X compound" / "carries / opens with" instead of "X-modified Y"
- ADD math kept implicit (already visible in hero ribbon + formula); referenced only
  when naming a prereq tier
- Coaching tone in execution + learning; pedagogical pairings called out where strong
- Per-section paragraph budgets: short ~1 sentence; execution ≤ 2 short paragraphs;
  learning + prereqs each ≤ 1 short paragraph

Drafts ordered by ADD ascending, then family.

## 1. tap — 3 ADD, mirage family

**Rationale.** Mirage family's entry-level tap-rhythm compound. Pedagogical anchor: tap rhythm reappears in spinal-tap (5 ADD, torque) — both share the two-dex-with-tap signature on different rotational frames.

**short_description**
A 3-ADD mirage compound where the tapping body action adds an extra accent between the two dex kicks of the mirage frame.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, drive an opposite-side outside dex, then bring the body across for a same-side inside dex; finish on an opposite-side toe delay. The tapping action lives between the two dex strikes — a brief foot-tap on the supporting leg that resets body rhythm before the second kick.

**learning_notes**
The tap itself is rhythmic, not athletic — it should feel like a hesitation, not an extra step. Practitioners who reach tap after a heavier-feeling 3-ADD mirage compound (smear, paradox-mirage) tend to over-commit the tap; treat it as a beat, not a body movement.

**prerequisite_notes**
Comfortable mirage (2 ADD) is the entry. Same-tier siblings include smear (pixie-mirage), paradox-mirage, and symposium-mirage — all 3-ADD mirage compounds layering a single modifier onto the same rotational base.

## 2. legeater — 3 ADD, pickup family

**Rationale.** First of the pickup-family triad in this batch. Pickup-family compounds at 3 ADD differ only in the first-kick variant; legeater's quantum set is the opposite-inside opener. Treat as one of three siblings (with paste + scrambled-eggbeater) to be enriched together.

**short_description**
A 3-ADD pickup compound where the first dex strikes opposite-side inside before continuing into the standard pickup rhythm.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick comes opposite-inside and crosses through the supporting leg; the second kick continues opposite-inside before the body shifts back for a same-side toe delay. Two consecutive opposite-side dexes is the trick's distinguishing feature — the body holds across the support longer than in paste or scrambled-eggbeater.

**learning_notes**
The trap is the second kick: practitioners reaching across for the first opposite-inside dex tend to return the body to neutral before the second, which kills the rhythm. Hold body weight off the support across both kicks; reset only after the second dex lands.

**prerequisite_notes**
Pickup (2 ADD) establishes the set + dex + stall frame. Same-tier pickup siblings — paste, scrambled-eggbeater, and omelette — share the frame and differ only in the first kick variant; practicing one carries over to the others.

## 3. paste — 3 ADD, pickup family

**Rationale.** Pickup-family same-inside opener (pixie set). Pairs in study with legeater (opposite-inside) and scrambled-eggbeater (opposite-outside) — same trick frame, three different first kicks.

**short_description**
A 3-ADD pickup compound opening with a same-side inside dex — the pixie set carries the bag behind the planted leg before the standard pickup follow-through.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick strikes same-side inside, which sends the bag behind the supporting leg. The body opens for an opposite-side inside dex on the second kick, then closes with a same-side toe delay. The pixie set is the load-bearing element — the same-inside angle differentiates paste from legeater (opposite-inside) and scrambled-eggbeater (opposite-outside).

**learning_notes**
The same-inside first kick is closer to the body than the other pickup variants — most misses come from over-rotating the foot, which sends the bag too far behind. Keep the first kick compact; body rotation on the opener should be minimal.

**prerequisite_notes**
Pickup (2 ADD) is the foundation. Familiarity with any pixie compound — smear (pixie-mirage, 3 ADD) or dimwalk (pixie-butterfly, 4 ADD) — primes the same-inside set feel before the pickup variant lands.

## 4. scrambled-eggbeater — 3 ADD, pickup family

**Rationale.** Pickup-family opposite-outside opener (atomic set). Completes the pickup-family triad in this batch: paste (same-inside), legeater (opposite-inside), scrambled-eggbeater (opposite-outside).

**short_description**
A 3-ADD pickup compound that opens with an opposite-side outside dex — the atomic set creates a wider first kick than its same-tier pickup siblings.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick reaches opposite-side outside, the widest first kick of the pickup-family compounds. The body settles back across for an opposite-side inside dex, then closes with a same-side toe delay. The atomic set's signature is the outside reach on the first kick — paste goes same-inside, legeater opposite-inside, scrambled-eggbeater opposite-outside.

**learning_notes**
The first kick's reach is the limiter — going too wide pulls the body off-axis before the second kick can settle. Keep the outside reach controlled; the opener should clear the support without overshooting.

**prerequisite_notes**
Pickup (2 ADD) is the entry. Atomic on a wider base — atom-smasher (atomic-mirage, 4 ADD) or atomic-butterfly (4 ADD) — gives the modifier its own feel before the pickup compound layers it in.

## 5. hatchet — 4 ADD, whirl family

**Rationale.** Whirl-family compound with the distinctive diving body modifier. Cross-family modifier comparison: hatchet's dive (forward extension) vs ducking-whirl's duck (compression) — the same family's rotational frame holding two different body-modifier feels.

**short_description**
A 4-ADD whirl compound where the body dives head-low through the rotation before recovering into a cross-body clipper.

**execution_summary**
From a clipper set, the body drops into a forward dive as the bag rises — head and shoulders descend while the supporting leg holds the rotation axis. The same-side front whirl carries the bag around the planted leg, landing in an opposite-side clipper cross-body. The dive is the trick's signature: a body extension across the rotation, not a compression like ducking.

**learning_notes**
The dive timing is the limiter — too early and the body's already low when the whirl starts, killing the rotation; too late and the bag escapes the whirl window. Treat the dive as a forward lean that begins on the set's rise and bottoms out as the whirl peaks.

**prerequisite_notes**
Whirl (3 ADD) is the foundation. Diving on a separate base — diving-butterfly (4 ADD) — gives the modifier's feel before the whirl rotation layers it in. Ducking-whirl (4 ADD) is the closest same-tier sibling for body-modifier comparison.

## 6. tripwalk — 4 ADD, butterfly family

**Rationale.** Butterfly-family quantum compound. Sits next to matador (pilot) and phoenix (this batch) on the butterfly base. Pedagogical clean: quantum is the same set modifier present in legeater (pickup family) — cross-family set-modifier study.

**short_description**
A 4-ADD butterfly compound where the quantum set opens with an opposite-side inside dex before the standard butterfly outside-wing closes the trick.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick strikes opposite-side inside — a tight kick that crosses the body line before the wing opens. The second kick reaches opposite-side outside in the canonical butterfly wing motion, landing in an opposite-side clipper cross-body delay. Quantum's first-kick angle is the differentiator: it pulls the body across before the wing rather than after.

**learning_notes**
The cross-body opening on the first kick is unfamiliar to practitioners coming from canonical butterflies — the wing instinct kicks in too early, which overshoots the first dex. Resist the wing impulse on the opener; hold the body tight, then release on the second kick.

**prerequisite_notes**
Butterfly (3 ADD) is the foundation. Same-tier siblings — atomic-butterfly, dimwalk (pixie-butterfly), ducking-butterfly, and ripwalk — each layer a different set or body modifier onto butterfly; studying any of the 4-ADD butterfly cohort carries over.

## 7. pigbeater — 4 ADD, legover family

**Rationale.** The Wave-1 loader-19 incident row (well-understood data state). Direct parent is eggbeater (3 ADD); pigbeater = pixie set on eggbeater. Pedagogical clean: eggbeater is the immediate prereq; the trick is a single-modifier add over its parent.

**short_description**
A 4-ADD legover compound — eggbeater's three-kick pattern fronted by a pixie set that lifts the bag behind the planted leg.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick strikes same-side inside, sending the bag behind the supporting leg — the pixie signature. The next two kicks both reach opposite-outside in eggbeater's characteristic double-out pattern, then the body resets for a same-side toe delay. The pixie set is the only addition over eggbeater — the rest of the trick is the eggbeater motion intact.

**learning_notes**
The transition from pixie's same-inside opener to eggbeater's opposite-outside second kick is the body's biggest shift in the trick. Practitioners who learn pigbeater before eggbeater feels solid tend to lose the second kick — settle eggbeater first, then add the pixie set on top.

**prerequisite_notes**
Eggbeater (3 ADD) is the direct parent — pigbeater is the same trick with one extra set modifier. Smear (pixie-mirage, 3 ADD) and dimwalk (pixie-butterfly, 4 ADD) offer pixie practice on simpler bases.

## 8. spinal-tap — 5 ADD, torque family

**Rationale.** Only torque candidate in batch — fills the family gap. Cross-family pairing with tap (mirage family, 3 ADD): same tap-rhythm at the dex-pair level, executed on torque's body-spin frame.

**short_description**
A 5-ADD torque compound — the tap rhythm from the mirage family extended through a body-spin and cross-body clipper landing.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick drives opposite-outside while the body initiates rotation. The same-inside second kick lands as the body completes a front-facing spin, then the supporting leg crosses for an opposite-clipper delay. Torque's body-spin is the load-bearing structure — the tap-rhythm of the two dexes overlays the rotation rather than sitting between them.

**learning_notes**
The rotation must be committed before the first kick lands — practitioners who learn tap before torque tend to delay the spin until after the dexes, which kills the timing. Treat the body-spin as the trick's core motion; the dexes ride on it.

**prerequisite_notes**
Torque (4 ADD) carries the body-spin signature. Tap (mirage + tapping, 3 ADD) demonstrates the tap rhythm without rotation; practicing both before spinal-tap lets the two layers integrate naturally.

## 9. phoenix — 5 ADD, butterfly family

**Rationale.** Two-modifier butterfly compound (pixie + ducking) — exercises the new ≥ 1 modifier-link decomposition strip threshold and sits as same-tier sibling to matador (already pilot) on the butterfly base.

**short_description**
A 5-ADD butterfly compound — pixie's same-inside opening, a ducking body compression, then the butterfly wing closing into a cross-body clipper.

**execution_summary**
From a toe set, the first kick strikes same-side inside (pixie); the body then drops into a ducking compression, holding low as the second kick reaches opposite-outside in the canonical butterfly wing. Recovery is an opposite-clipper cross-body delay. The ducking sits in the gap between the two dexes — body low through the transition, rising into the wing.

**learning_notes**
Ducking between dexes is what makes phoenix harder than 4-ADD butterflies — the duck must be deep enough to feel committed but quick enough to release before the wing. Most misses come from rushing the wing while still low; let the body rise on the second kick rather than driving it from a compressed position.

**prerequisite_notes**
Butterfly (3 ADD) and either ducking-butterfly or dimwalk (pixie-butterfly), both 4 ADD, cover the modifier feel separately before they combine. Matador (5 ADD, butterfly family) is the same-tier sibling — both layer two modifiers onto butterfly with different body/set splits.

## 10. spender — 6 ADD, blender family

**Rationale.** Closes the Spender↔Mind-Bender pedagogical pair (mind-bender already pilot). Both are 6-ADD blender compounds carrying paradox; the second modifier differs (spinning vs ducking) — the natural side-by-side study.

**short_description**
A 6-ADD blender compound — two body-spins flanking a paradox-direction whirl kick, landing in a cross-body clipper.

**execution_summary**
From a clipper set, the body initiates a back-facing spin. As the spin completes, the opposite-side front-whirl kick lands with paradox direction — a same-side body reset across the kick. A second back-spin carries the body around again before the supporting leg crosses for a same-side clipper delay. The two body-spins are the trick's frame; the whirl kick is the centerpiece they're built around.

**learning_notes**
Pairs naturally with mind-bender — both are 6-ADD blender compounds carrying paradox, framing a paradox whirl kick with a second body modifier. Spender's spinning gives the trick continuous rotation; mind-bender's ducking gives it a compression-and-release rhythm. Study the two side-by-side for body-modifier feel.

**prerequisite_notes**
Blender (4 ADD) and paradox-blender (5 ADD) are the direct progression. Spinning-whirl (4 ADD) gives the spinning-on-rotational-base feel. Mind-bender is the natural same-tier sibling to study alongside.
