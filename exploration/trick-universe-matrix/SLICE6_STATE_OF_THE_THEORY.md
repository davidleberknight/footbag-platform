# State of the Theory: an Honest Snapshot of the Movement Algebra

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. This is a consolidation, not an extension. It does not investigate any new movement family. Its purpose is to say plainly what we know, what we merely suspect, and what we got wrong, and to make the theory smaller rather than larger. The discipline is falsification: where a claim is weak, it is marked weak.

Two adversarial checks were run against the whole deployed corpus (895 tricks with notation) specifically for this snapshot, and their results are used below to calibrate confidence rather than to add anything new.

## The confidence ladder

- **Observation.** Directly read from the data or the code; not in dispute, but also not yet a claim about structure.
- **Working hypothesis.** A plausible reading with thin or indirect evidence; could easily be wrong.
- **Strong hypothesis.** Broad evidence, survived at least one attempt to break it, but not proven and with known soft spots.
- **Supported structural invariant.** Tested against the full corpus and holds with only explained exceptions.
- **Refuted.** An idea we held and the data killed.
- **Unknown.** Named but not yet studied.

## The one thing we ran this session, and what it settled

**ADD equals the count of scored brackets, across the whole corpus.** For 892 of 894 tricks with real notation, the trick's stored difficulty equals the number of scored components in its formula (every bracketed component except the non-scoring kick). The only two exceptions are the two rows whose notation is an unfinished placeholder, the bare toe and clipper stalls, which is a data gap, not a counterexample. This is the strongest result in the theory and the rock the rest leans on.

Two smaller checks: paradox never appears in a trick without at least as many dexes to attach to (zero counterexamples), and no step carries two body components (zero cases of two body brackets in one bundle). Both support the picture below. One check broke a sub-claim: a cross-body component stands alone, with no held landing, in four tricks (the bare clipper, flying clipper, knee clipper, toe clipper), so cross-body is not purely a manner; it can be a contact in its own right.

## Classification of every major idea

Each entry: the problem it solved, the evidence for and against, and the verdict.

**ADD as event count, not step count.** Problem: what does difficulty count. For: 892 of 894 corpus-wide. Against: nothing structural; two data-error exceptions. Verdict: **Supported structural invariant.** This is the firmest thing we have.

**Primitive movement vocabulary (surfaces, sides, directions, scored components).** Problem: what is the alphabet. For: it is the closed token set the notation and code already use, and it accounts for the corpus. Against: it is the existing system's alphabet, so this is description, not discovery. Verdict: **Observation**, secure.

**Movement formulas instead of trick names.** Problem: are names or structure primary. For: 895 tricks carry a formula; the audit showed names mislead (osis is named an atom but has zero dexterities; the eight cube atoms are one shape at eight settings). Against: formulas are curator-authored and could encode a house style; two are unfinished. Verdict: **Strong hypothesis**, close to a working principle. It has repeatedly paid off.

**Event grammar (a trick is built from a few event kinds: dex, body, landing, plus entry).** Problem: what are the parts. For: the ADD invariant treats exactly these as the scored parts and holds corpus-wide. Against: the boundary between a body event and a manner is not crisp (see below). Verdict: **Strong hypothesis**, leaning invariant.

**Bundle-step grammar (a trick is a sequence of steps, each a set of co-occurring events).** Problem: is a trick linear or bundled. For: the ADD invariant forces it (events outnumber steps whenever a step bundles); three temporal relations are used at scale (895 tricks use plain succession, 232 use the overlap marker); the fairy contrast shows bundling changes identity. Against: it was read closely on a curated slice, not parsed across all 895 tricks; the parse has not been shown to be residue-free. Verdict: **Strong hypothesis**, not yet a swept invariant. The first roadmap slice is to sweep it.

**Simultaneity as its own axis.** Problem: how do same-instant events combine. Verdict: **Merged.** Simultaneity is not a separate idea; a bundle-step is simultaneity made concrete. The two names describe one thing and should not both exist.

**Carrier versus manner (each step has one main event; the rest describe how it is done).** Problem: why manners cannot be isolated as clean axes. For: paradox never appears without a dex; there is no bare suspension; the body slot never doubles. Against: cross-body stands alone as a contact in four tricks, so the carrier or manner role is contextual, not intrinsic to a component; and a suspension is scored like an event yet was called a manner, so the line is blurry. Verdict: **Working hypothesis, partially refuted in its strong form.** It is a useful heuristic that correctly predicted axis-hunting would fail, but it is not clean enough to be the ontology. See question 2.

**The one-contact definition of a step (a step is one foot-to-bag contact; a new contact opens a new step).** Problem: what physically is a step. For: consistent with the re-plant marker forcing a new step in the fairy contrast. Against: contact is inferred from the notation alone; nothing independent (video, biomechanics) has confirmed that a succession marker really coincides with a new contact. It could instead mean simply the next scored moment. Verdict: **Working hypothesis.** This is the softest assumption under the whole grammar.

**Kick as a grade-lowering terminal.** Problem: why a kick scores one less than its stall. For: given as a ratified rule, and consistent with the corpus, the ADD invariant holds only when the kick component is treated as non-scoring. Against: it was handed to us as an axiom; we confirmed consistency, not necessity. Verdict: **Supported within the system** (an axiom the corpus corroborates).

**The eight-vertex dex cube.** Problem: are the foundational atoms related. For: all eight combinations of dex-side, direction, and terminal-side at the toe are named, and each trick's notation matches its predicted coordinate exactly. Fully observed, not predicted. Verdict: **Supported structural invariant** for the toe fiber. The strongest positive structure after the ADD invariant.

**Side and direction symmetry (a small reflection group on the dex).** Problem: how the cube vertices relate. For: the cube's edges are exactly side-swap and direction-reversal, and direction is already known to be structural (around-the-world is not orbit). Against: calling the relation a formal group is a mathematical dress on an observed pattern; the pattern is real, the group language is interpretive. Verdict: cube existence is **Supported**; the group framing is a **Strong hypothesis** that adds elegance but not yet leverage.

**Surface fibers (landing surfaces sort into cube-preserving, cube-halving, cube-lifting).** Problem: why the clipper is not a peer of the toe. For: the plain, unusual, and cross-body landing split is read straight from the notation, and the whirl family confirms the lift (a one-dex trick reaching grade three purely by a cross-body landing). Against: the specific claim that non-toe fibers carry the full cube is mostly unnamed, so it is prediction, not observation. Verdict: the three-phase landing classification is a **Strong hypothesis**; the fiber-preservation predictions are a **Working hypothesis** awaiting realizability tests.

**Launch surfaces.** Problem: does where you launch shape the cube. For: launch tokens are observed; the one cross-body-launch trick sits where the rule predicts. Against: essentially one named example carries the claim. Verdict: launch vocabulary is **Observation**; the launch-halves-the-cube effect is a **Working hypothesis** on thin evidence.

**Landing surfaces.** Problem: what can a trick land on. For: the plain, unusual, cross-body, and midline distinctions are all directly in the notation and in the ADD arithmetic. Verdict: **Observation**, secure; its cube consequences ride with the surface-fiber entry.

**Support-leg posture.** Problem: is suspension a coordinate. For: explicit planted and no-plant markers, per step, changing within a trick, and structural (distinguishing otherwise-identical tricks). Against: the claim that posture is grade-neutral rests on a single comparison (symposium versus pogo), and the four-way mode grouping (planted, no-plant, flying, jump) is one we imposed. Verdict: **Strong hypothesis** that posture is real, per-step, and structural; **Working hypothesis** that it is grade-neutral and cleanly four-valued.

**Body modifiers.** Problem: how body actions enter. For: they are registry operators and appear as body components; the body slot never doubles in a step (checked). Verdict: **Observation** that they exist; **Supported** that a step holds at most one.

**Cross-body relationships.** Problem: what is the clipper. For: a cross-body component is grade-two when held and drives the whirl family and the clipper landing. Against: it is dual-role, a manner of a held landing in most tricks but a standalone contact in four. Verdict: **Observation** that it exists and scores; the earlier implicit claim that it is always a manner is **Refuted**.

**Paradox.** Problem: not studied yet. For: it only ever appears bundled onto a dex (zero counterexamples). Verdict: **Unknown**, deliberately deferred. We know it co-occurs with a dex; we do not know if it is a manner or its own structure.

**X-dex.** Problem: not studied yet. For: appears bundled; its conditional scoring is settled elsewhere. Verdict: **Unknown**, deferred.

**Bag path (swing, backside).** Problem: is trajectory a slot. For: a handful of tricks carry swing or backside path tokens. Against: two or three tricks, and in one case the path token itself scores as a dex, muddying whether path is grade-neutral. Verdict: **Working hypothesis** bordering on **Unknown**; too little evidence to place.

**Rotation.** Problem: how do whirl and swirl score. For: they fold into a single dex component and are grade-preserving; the family's grade comes from the cross-body landing, not the rotation. Against: none found. Verdict: the corrected claim (rotation is a grade-neutral parameter of a dex) is a **Strong hypothesis**; the earlier guess that a cross-body rotational dex must be grade two is **Refuted**, and this is the clearest example of the data killing one of our ideas.

## The merges (making the theory smaller)

- **Simultaneity and the bundle-step are one idea.** Drop simultaneity as a separate axis.
- **The cube and the side-direction symmetry are one object.** The symmetry is the cube's edge structure, not an extra fact.
- **The four event kinds, the ADD-equals-events invariant, the one-contact step, and the bundle are one grammar.** They are not four discoveries; they are one model seen from four sides.
- **Surface fibers, launch surfaces, and landing surfaces are one thing:** the surface parameters of the dex and terminal events. Not three topics, one.

After merging, the theory has far fewer independent claims than the topic list suggests. That is the point.

## Question 1: the smallest complete description

A trick is a sequence of contacts. At each contact the foot or body does one scored thing to the bag, either a dexterity around it or a landing on it, and sometimes a second scored thing happens in the same instant, most often a body action. How each scored thing is done, across the body, on an odd surface, rotated, while suspended, is recorded but does not add a new contact. The difficulty is the total number of scored things over all contacts.

That is the whole model, in three concepts:

1. **Contact** (a step): one moment of foot-or-body meeting bag. A trick is an ordered list of them.
2. **Scored event**: one countable thing at a contact, of which there are essentially two kinds that stand on their own, a dexterity and a landing, plus a body action that can either stand alone or share a contact.
3. **Count**: difficulty is the number of scored events.

Everything else in the theory, the cube, the reflections, the fibers, the postures, is what you see when you hold most of the parameters of an event fixed and vary one. The famous eight atoms are the single configuration "one dexterity, then one plain toe landing, planted" with three of its parameters varied. The model is: sequenced contacts, each carrying a small bundle of scored events, counted. If you had to keep only one sentence, it is that.

## Question 2: have we found the right ontology

The live question is whether the split into carriers and manners is a better organizing idea than continuing to hunt for coordinate axes. Both, honestly.

**For carriers and manners.** It earned its keep by prediction. When we tried to add posture as a clean fifth axis, the carrier-manner view said it would fail, because a suspension is a manner and a manner has nothing to attach to on its own, and that is exactly what happened: there is no bare suspension in the corpus. It is also more economical than axis-hunting. Axis-hunting invites a new independent dimension for every family, posture, cross-body, rotation, paradox, and the space explodes. Carriers and manners say most of those are not new dimensions at all; they are ways of doing a dexterity or a landing, so they attach rather than multiply. The data leans this way: paradox never floats free of a dex, the body slot never doubles.

**Against carriers and manners.** The distinction is blurry and contextual, which is fatal for an ontology if not for a heuristic. A suspension is called a manner yet it scores exactly like an event and is counted in the difficulty, so the same thing is on both sides of the line. Cross-body is a manner in most tricks and a standalone contact in four, so the role is not a property of the component but of its surroundings. An ontology whose categories flip with context is not carving nature at the joints.

**The smaller, stronger replacement.** Drop the carrier-manner dichotomy as fundamental and keep only what survives: there are scored events, and there is a co-occurrence relation that groups some of them into one contact. "Manner" then dissolves into "a co-occurring event," which removes the contradiction (a scored suspension is simply an event that shares a contact with a dex) and the context-dependence (cross-body is an event; whether it shares a contact with a landing or stands alone is just its bundling, not a change of kind). At the same time, axis-hunting is not dead, because some parameters genuinely are clean orthogonal axes: side, direction, and surface form a real product space, which is why the cube exists and is fully filled. So the honest ontology is hybrid and minimal: a few genuine coordinate axes inside a dexterity or landing event, plus events grouped by co-occurrence into contacts. Carrier-manner was a useful ladder to this, and should now be kicked away. This is a case where two ideas were really one, and saying so shrinks the theory.

## Question 3: the roadmap, ordered by information gain

Five slices, ordered by how much each could move or break the core, not by ease.

**1. Sweep the bundle-step grammar across all 895 tricks.** Hypothesis tested: every trick parses into a sequence of contacts, each a bundle of the known event kinds, with no leftover. Falsified by: any trick that will not decompose, or any scored component the grammar cannot place. Why first: the grammar is currently read on a curated handful; the ADD invariant already passed corpus-wide, but the step structure has not. This is the highest-information test because everything else assumes the grammar parses, and a single residue would force a rethink. It is analysis of existing data, not new theory.

**2. Decide events-only versus carriers-and-manners empirically.** Hypothesis tested: every scored component behaves the same way, as a countable event grouped by co-occurrence, with no need for a carrier-versus-manner label. Falsified by: finding a component that behaves differently depending on its role, for example one that can be reordered or removed as a manner but not as a carrier. Why second: it decides the ontology of question 2 and could delete a whole distinction, making the theory smaller. Do it before studying any new family, so the new family is described in the right vocabulary.

**3. Test the one-contact definition of a step against an independent signal.** Hypothesis tested: a succession marker really coincides with a new foot-or-body contact, not merely the next scored moment. Falsified by: notation boundaries that do not line up with contacts in video or an expert breakdown of a sample of tricks. Why third: this is the softest assumption holding up the word "contact," and it is the one thing the notation cannot confirm by itself. It likely needs data from outside the dictionary, which is why it is called out rather than assumed. If it fails, "bundle-step" is the right shape with the wrong name.

**4. Study paradox and x-dex as the test of the events-only ontology.** Hypothesis tested: paradox and x-dex are ordinary co-occurring dex events, not new axes. Falsified by: either behaving like an independent dimension rather than an attached event. Why fourth and not sooner: it is the largest unstudied region and tempting to rush, but it should be described in whatever vocabulary slice 2 settles on, or the study will bake in the ontology we are trying to test.

**5. Test surface-fiber preservation by realizability, not by naming.** Hypothesis tested: the non-toe plain paired fibers carry the full eight-vertex cube. Falsified by: a predicted vertex that cannot be performed with control. Why last: it refines the reach of a structure we already trust rather than threatening the core, so its information gain for the foundations is lower, and it needs realizability judgment (expert or video) that the earlier slices do not. It is good science, but it moves the edges, not the center.

## What would break the theory, stated plainly

The core would fall if the full-corpus parse in slice one leaves residue, or if slice three shows succession markers do not track contacts. The ontology would simplify further if slice two shows the carrier-manner line is unnecessary, which current evidence already suggests. The weakest load-bearing claims today are the one-contact definition of a step (a working hypothesis dressed as a definition) and the grade-neutrality of posture (one comparison). The firmest are ADD-equals-events and the eight-vertex cube, both checked against the whole of the relevant data. The theory is currently smaller than the topic list makes it look, and the honest next moves are the ones most likely to shrink it further, not grow it.

No names generated, no production code, no dictionary changes. Deliverable: this note.
