# The Movement Algebra of Freestyle Footbag, and a Symbolic Classification of the 2-ADD Universe

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not canonical doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. This paper proposes an algebra that generates the freestyle movement universe from a small primitive basis, and classifies the complete 2-ADD space by structure rather than by name. Companion files: `one_add_universe.csv` (the grade-1 basis, from the prior slice) and `two_add_classification.csv` (every observed 2-ADD movement mapped to its algebraic coordinates).

Working axioms (given, not re-derived here):

- A trick is a movement formula; names are annotations on formulas.
- The movement grammar is primary; the dictionary is one observed subset of a larger universe.
- Kick rule (settled): a kick is worth exactly one ADD less than its stall counterpart. Equivalently, a stall is a kick plus a terminal delay worth one.

Method: the algebra is proposed from first principles and then checked against the operational notation of every named 2-ADD movement in the live dictionary (`freestyle_tricks`, `adds = 2`, active). Names appear only as annotations that let the reader verify the structure; the structure does not depend on them.

---

## 1. The central move: from a layer list to a graded word algebra

The prior slice suggested five layers (contact, set, dexterity, body, terminal). That is a good first cut, but the layers are not five independent dimensions. Two of them, "set" and "dexterity," turn out to be the same object in different positions. A cleaner abstraction subsumes all five.

**Proposal.** A movement is a finite word

```
M = e · g1 · g2 · ... · gn · t
```

read in temporal order, where `e` is an entry (a starting contact), each `gi` is a grade-1 generator, `t` is a terminal, and well-formed words obey simple positional rules (a regular language). The ADD of a movement is its **grade**: the number of scoring events in the word.

The whole vocabulary is generated by a very small basis.

**The generator basis.**

| Symbol | Kind | Parameters | Grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| `e(s)` | entry / contact | landing surface `s` (TOE, CLIP, INSIDE, ...) | 0 |
| `d(sd, dir, rot)` | dexterity | step side `sd` in {SAME, OP}; direction `dir` in {IN, OUT}; rotation `rot` in {none, WHIRL, SWIRL} | 1 (2 if cross-body rotational) |
| `b(k)` | body step | kind `k` in {SPIN, DUCK, DIVE, FLYING, JUMP, ...}, optional pre-state (front)/(back) | 1 |
| `D(ts, s)` | plain landing | terminal side `ts`, surface `s` | 1 |
| `T(ts, s, h)` | hard landing | plus hardness `h` in {cross-body, unusual} | 2 |
| `K` | kick terminal | none | 0 |

**Grade (ADD)** is additive over the word: each `d`, `b`, and plain `D` contributes 1; a hard landing `T` contributes 2; the entry `e` and the kick `K` contribute 0.

**Two consequences make this the right abstraction.**

1. **Set and dexterity unify.** A "set operator" is not a separate generator kind. It is a dexterity in the first position. Pixie is `d(SAME, IN)` at the front of the word; the identical shape later in the word is a receiver dexterity. What the culture calls "set versus body versus receiver" is *position*, not *kind*. This collapses three of the five layers into one generator with a positional constraint, which is exactly the parsimony we want. The layer list is recovered as the regular-language shape `e (set-dex)? (body)* (receiver-dex)* t`, but the generator alphabet no longer needs a distinct set symbol.

2. **The kick rule becomes an operator identity.** By the settled rule, replacing a plain terminal `D` with the kick `K` lowers the grade by exactly 1. So `K` is the grade-lowering terminal and `D` the grade-raising terminal; the stall form and kick form of any movement are the *same word* with the terminal delay present or absent. Stall and kick are grade-adjacent images of one movement under the terminal operator. This is why the kick family (grade `n`) exposes the primitive that its stall cousin (grade `n+1`) hides.

**Definition of primitivity.** A movement is *primitive* if its grade is 1, that is, if its word contains exactly one scoring generator. Primitivity is a grade-1 property. This single definition answers the whole "which are primitive" question in advance: see section 4.

## 2. The symmetry group acting on the basis

The dexterity generator carries two binary parameters, and each admits a natural involution.

- **Side reflection** `R_s`: swap SAME and OP, on both the dex step side and the terminal side. `R_s^2 = 1`.
- **Direction reversal** `R_d`: swap IN and OUT. `R_d^2 = 1`.

These commute, so together they generate a Klein four-group `V = {1, R_s, R_d, R_s R_d}` acting on dex-bearing words. Direction reversal is the reflection that the platform's own doctrine already treats as structural (around-the-world is not orbit; mirage is not illusion; spinning is not inspinning). The involution therefore *pairs distinct real movements*; it is a symmetry of the space, not a collapse of it.

Orthogonal to `V` is a free parameter:

- **Surface substitution** `S`: relabel the landing (or entry) surface. The movement shape is invariant; only where it lands changes. This is not a group element that pairs movements but an index set the terminal is drawn from.

And a grading operator:

- **Rotation lift** `Rot`: attach a WHIRL or SWIRL to a dex. For a plain rotational dex this is grade-preserving; for a cross-body rotational dex it raises grade (which is why whirl and swirl sit at grade 3, not 2).

The algebra of 2-ADD movements is then: grade-2 words, acted on by `V` (pairing shapes), fibered over the surface set `S` (spreading each shape across landing surfaces), and graded by `Rot` (lifting to higher grades).

## 3. The first hard result: the 2-ADD toe cube is complete and filled

Restrict to the most basic grade-2 schema, a single dexterity followed by a single plain toe landing:

```
M = e · d(sd, dir) · D(ts, toe)
```

Its coordinates are three independent bits: `sd` in {SAME, OP}, `dir` in {IN, OUT}, `ts` in {SAME, OP}. That is a cube with eight vertices. Reading the operational notation of the eight named toe atoms places one at every vertex:

| dex side | direction | terminal side | observed annotation |
|---|---|---|---|
| SAME | IN | SAME | around the world |
| SAME | OUT | SAME | orbit |
| SAME | IN | OP | pixie |
| SAME | OUT | OP | fairy |
| OP | IN | OP | mirage |
| OP | OUT | OP | illusion |
| OP | IN | SAME | pickup |
| OP | OUT | SAME | legover |

All eight vertices are realized and all eight are named. The eight "atoms" that the dictionary treats as unrelated foundational tricks are in fact one movement schema, `d · D(toe)`, evaluated at the eight corners of a `side x direction x terminal-side` cube. Their edges are the group `V` plus terminal reflection:

- around the world and orbit differ only by direction (`R_d`).
- around the world and pixie differ only by terminal side.
- mirage and pickup differ only by terminal side.
- mirage and pixie differ only by dex side (`R_s`).
- legover and orbit differ only by dex side.

This is the symmetry the request asked to expose, and the data confirms it exactly: the culture, working purely by feel and history, discovered and named every vertex of a complete finite group orbit without ever writing the group down.

The four grade-1 set-dexes from the prior slice (pixie, fairy, atomic, opposite-inward, the `side x direction` square) are the *bottom face* of this cube seen without a terminal, that is, the kick forms. The held cube is the grade-1 square lifted by the terminal reflection into grade 2.

## 4. Structural classification of the complete 2-ADD space

Every grade-2 word is one of a small number of composition schemas. There are no others, because grade 2 is just "two scoring events in temporal order," and each event is a dex, a body, or a landing (with a hard landing counting as two). The schemas, with observed annotations for verification (full mapping in `two_add_classification.csv`):

**Class A, dexterity then landing (`d · D`).** One trip out, one held landing. The dominant class. Parameterized by the cube coordinates above times the surface fiber. The toe fiber is fully named (the eight atoms). Other fibers are sparsely named: inside around the world and inspinning reverse guay live on the inside fiber, outside around the world on the outside fiber, guay is the pickup shape on the inside fiber, bubba is the illusion shape entered from the clipper set. The unnamed cells of these fibers are the visible gaps between the named subset and the universe.

**Class B, body then landing (`b · D`).** One body reshape, one held landing. Parameterized by body kind times terminal side times surface: diving, ducking, and (via pre-state) front-spin and back-spin toe stalls; hop over is the jump-bodied member landing inside.

**Class C, a single hard landing (`T`).** One event that is itself grade 2: a cross-body landing (`[XBD][DEL]`) or an unusual-surface landing (`[UNS][DEL]`). The clipper stall, dragon, and probe are cross-body landings; cloud and heel stalls are unusual-surface landings. This class is why the clipper stall scores 2 while the toe stall scores 1: the terminal operator itself is graded by landing hardness. The landing carries both ADD, so the pre-terminal word is empty.

**Class D, kick generation (`... · K`).** Two trips then a kick. Because the kick is the grade-lowering terminal, a grade-2 kick word is the kick form of a grade-3 stall word: butterfly kick is jump-body plus outward dex closed by a kick, dragonfly kick is flying-body plus opposite-outward dex closed by a kick. Their stall cousins live at grade 3. This class is the bridge that makes the whole spectrum commensurable: it is grade-2 movement whose "natural" home is one grade up.

**Class E, iteration (`g · g`).** The same generator twice: two toe landings in sequence (juggling), or two body spins (double spin). Degenerate but real; they occupy grade 2 by repetition rather than by composing two different generators.

**The five schemas answer the request's structural questions directly:**

- *Which are primitive?* None. By the definition in section 1, primitivity is a grade-1 property, so the 2-ADD universe contains no primitives at all. Every named 2-ADD "atom" (mirage, legover, clipper stall) is a composition. The true primitives are the grade-1 generators of the prior slice.
- *Which are compositions?* All of them. Classes A, B, D, E are explicit two-generator compositions; class C is a single grade-2 terminal, which is still a composition of a cross-body-or-unusual event with a delay.
- *Which are parameterizations of the same movement?* The eight cube atoms are one movement (`d · D(toe)`) at eight parameter settings; each surface fiber is that same movement moved to another landing surface.
- *Which differ only by surface?* Mirage and guay (toe versus inside), around the world and its inside and outside fibers. Same word, surface parameter changed.
- *Which differ only by dex direction?* Around the world and orbit; mirage and illusion. Same word, `R_d` applied.
- *Which differ only by body relationship?* The terminal-side reflection within the cube (pixie versus around the world, pickup versus mirage), and any pair that differs only by inserting or removing a body generator.

## 5. The minimal grammar

Collecting the above, the smallest grammar that generates the observed universe is:

```
Move       := Entry (SetDex)? Body* Dex* Terminal
Entry      := contact(surface)                         ; grade 0
SetDex     := Dex                                       ; a dex in first position
Dex        := d(side, direction, rotation)              ; grade 1 (2 if cross-body rotational)
Body       := b(kind, prestate?)                        ; grade 1
Terminal   := D(side, surface)                          ; grade 1  (plain landing)
            | T(side, surface, hardness)                ; grade 2  (cross-body or unusual)
            | K                                          ; grade 0  (kick)
ADD(Move)  := sum of generator grades
```

Two rules capture almost everything else:

1. **Kick identity.** `K` is `D` with grade lowered by one. The kick form and stall form of any move are the same word up to the terminal.
2. **Symmetry.** The pair group `V = <R_s, R_d>` acts on dex-bearing moves, the surface set indexes the terminal, and rotation lifts grade.

The five-layer picture is recovered as the positional shape `Entry (SetDex)? Body* Dex* Terminal`, but the generator alphabet is only four kinds (entry, dex, body, terminal), because set-dex is dex-in-first-position and kick is the grade-0 terminal. That is the minimal basis the research set out to find.

## 6. What maps cleanly, what does not

Cleanly mapped: entry, dexterity (side, direction), body, plain and hard terminals, the kick identity, the surface fiber, and the `V` symmetry. The entire observed 2-ADD set fits the five schemas with no residue (`two_add_classification.csv`).

Open structural questions, stated as research directions, not defects:

- **Grade of rotation.** Whirl and swirl sit at grade 3, so a cross-body rotational dex must count as grade 2, not 1. The generator table encodes this, but the precise rule (which rotations are grade-raising) needs the grade-3 data to pin down. That is the natural next slice.
- **The `>>` layering operator.** Operational notation marks a layered or simultaneous step with a double separator. In this algebra that is temporal order within one grade slot; whether true simultaneity (two events at once) needs its own operator is an open question, likely first visible at grade 3 and above (butterfly and osis).
- **Pogo and non-dex sets.** A set that adds 1 without a scoring dex does not fit the "dex in first position" identity. It may be a genuine second grade-1 generator kind (a grade-1 body-like set), or a special case. Grade-1 already flagged this; grade 2 does not resolve it.
- **Entry as identity.** Entry is treated as grade 0 and mostly free. Whether some entries constrain which dexes can follow (a compatibility relation, not a free product) is worth testing against the surface fibers.

JOB notation is left as historical annotation only, per the working axioms and the platform's own treatment of the opaque Jobs column. A faithful JOB cross-map would need the external Stanford encoding and is out of scope here.

## 7. Summary of the proposed algebra

1. A movement is a graded word over four generator kinds: entry (grade 0), dexterity (grade 1, cross-body rotational grade 2), body (grade 1), and terminal (plain landing grade 1, hard landing grade 2, kick grade 0). ADD is the grade.
2. Set, body, and receiver are positions of the one dexterity generator, not separate kinds. This collapses the five-layer picture to a four-generator basis with a regular-language shape.
3. The kick is the grade-lowering terminal; stall and kick forms are one word up to the terminal. This is the settled kick rule expressed as an operator identity.
4. A Klein four-group of side reflection and direction reversal pairs dex movements; the surface set fibers each shape across landing surfaces; rotation lifts grade.
5. The 2-ADD universe contains no primitives. It is five composition schemas (dex+landing, body+landing, hard landing, kick-generation, iteration), and its densest region, the toe dex-landing schema, is a complete eight-vertex `side x direction x terminal-side` cube whose every vertex the tradition already named.
6. The named dictionary is the toe fiber filled in and the other surface fibers sparsely populated; the unnamed cells are the coordinates of the universe the culture has not yet labeled.

The recommended next research step is the same shape as before: enumerate and visualize one grade at a time. For grade 2 that means rendering the cube and its surface fibers with named cells filled and candidate cells empty, then using the grade-3 data to fix the rotation-grade rule. No names, no production code, no dictionary changes; the deliverables are this paper and the two companion classification files.
