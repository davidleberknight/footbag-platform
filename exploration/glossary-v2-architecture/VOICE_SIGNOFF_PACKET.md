## Top 5 voice rules learned from the prototypes

1. **Open with a movement the player can feel, before any relationship or number.** The
   strongest entries lead with the foot and bag ("the shelf you reach across yourself,"
   "set the bag, circle it, come home"); the test-4 opening is physical, never metric-first.
2. **A Reveal that only redirects is not a Reveal.** The pilot's "see the other entry"
   expanders were a dead layer; the insight-link belongs in Relates, and a real Reveal must
   change how the reader sees freestyle, not point at where the change happens.
3. **One insight per entry, and space them in the reading order.** Stacking the geometry
   reveals (direction, side, surface frame) dulls each; the entries themselves forced the
   spacing rule, and Direction earns its keep precisely by NOT re-revealing what Mirage
   already did.
4. **Ground every structural or ADD claim in the live notation, and carry the caveat where
   the data is unsettled.** The entries that needed correction (blurry's fixed ADD, the
   swirl-family framing) drifted from the notation; the ones that hold (torque = 4, the
   ATW/pixie shared opening, paradox's open-scoring hedge) cite the structure and flag what
   is not yet settled.
5. **Name the neighbor in plain kin language, and introduce every in-house term on first
   use.** "Its mirror is X; they share everything but direction" teaches better than
   family-taxonomy speak, and "dex" or "ADD" appears only after it is glossed.
