# Glossary V2 — Pilot Entries, Compounds tranche (Torque + Blender)

Prototypes, not production. Purpose: author the two compound entries that sit at the
bottom of the deepest prerequisite chain in the dependency graph, now that every term they
depend on has an authored entry — and, in doing so, **prove the dependency graph resolves
end to end.** If Torque and Blender can be written using only already-defined terms, the
graph's topological order works: operators before compounds, no forward reference to an
undefined concept.

These supersede the first-draft Torque and Blender entries in `PILOT_ENTRIES.md`, which
were written *before* their prerequisites existed (as flagship voice exemplars). The
versions here rest on the full authored chain and are grounded in the live ADD math
(checked read-only, no data changed): **osis = 3; torque = miraging osis = 4; blender =
whirling osis = 4.** Torque and blender differ in exactly one thing — the side of the
clipper they land on (torque opposite, blender same) — which is the operator's only visible
effect on the score-neutral geometry.

## Torque
**Compound · insight home: famous-sounding tricks are compressed formulas (the algebra, rung 2)**

**Line.** Torque is a powerful, turning trick: from a set the body spins and the bag is
carried on an inward dex, then caught cross-body on a clipper, behind the leg. It has a
short, hard name and it *feels* like something the sport was built out of — a fundamental.

**Relates.** That feeling is the illusion the entry exists to correct. Torque's structure
is "miraging osis": an osis — the spinning, cross-body core atom, three points — with a
miraging operator laid over it for one more, four points in all, and the parts are exactly
the score (see Osis, Miraging, Composition, ADD). Its near-twin blender makes the point
vividly: the same osis base, the same added inward dex, the same four points — the two
differ only in which operator drives the dex and, with it, which side the trick lands on
(torque to the opposite side, blender to the same). Two famous names, one shared base.

**Reveals.** Torque teaches the lesson blur introduced, one level down. Blur showed that a
short *name* can hide a structural name; torque shows that a name which sounds *primitive*
is really a base plus an operator. Osis is the foundation, miraging is the operator, and
"torque" is the compression — and once you see that, the true foundations of freestyle
shrink to a small handful of atoms and operators, with the hundreds of named tricks
assembled from them. When two tricks that both feel like bedrock turn out to be the same
atom under two different operators, you are no longer memorizing a list of moves; you are
reading a system. That is the encyclopedia's central claim, and torque is one of its
cleanest proofs. *(The staircase: blur is rung 1, the name level; torque is rung 2, the
formula level; butterfly is rung 3, the family level. Essay: Name vs Structure.)*

**Explore.** Blender (its near-twin, whirling osis) · Osis (their shared base) · Miraging
(the operator that drives it) · Blur (the same lesson one level simpler) · essay: Name vs
Structure.

## Blender
**Compound · connective** *(the algebra lesson is homed at Torque; the link lives in Relates, not a Reveal)*

**Line.** Blender is a turning, cross-body trick that lands on a clipper, close enough to
torque that a player who knows one half-knows the other. Its structure is "whirling osis" —
the same osis foundation as torque, driven by a whirling operator instead of a miraging one.

**Relates.** Blender is torque's sibling. Both are an osis (three points) with one operator
laid over it (one more) — four points each; the operator differs, whirling versus miraging,
and with it the finishing side: blender lands on the same side, torque on the opposite side
(see Osis, Whirling, Torque). Set the two beside each other and the shared skeleton is
unmistakable — one base, two operators, two names. Blender is the second witness to torque's
lesson rather than a new one: two solid-sounding names, one shared base, told apart by a
single operator (see Torque, and the essay Name vs Structure, for why so many
fundamental-*sounding* tricks are compressed formulas).

**Explore.** Torque (its near-twin, miraging osis) · Osis (their shared base) · Whirling
(the operator that drives it) · essay: Name vs Structure.

# Review

## Which entries earned Reveals

- **Torque — yes.** It is the registered home of the algebra insight's second rung
  (name-sounds-primitive → base + operator). Its Reveal names its place in the staircase so
  the reader climbs rather than loops.
- **Blender — no.** It is connective: the algebra lesson lives at Torque, and Blender
  carries the one-line link in Relates. This is a deliberate correction of the first-draft
  Blender in `PILOT_ENTRIES.md`, which still had a "reference Reveal" (an expander that only
  said "see Torque"). That dead layer was removed in the architecture revision, so the
  refined Blender has Line + Relates + Explore and no Reveal — a small proof that the
  two-kinds-of-entry rule is being followed.

No new insight was introduced; the registry count stays at eleven. Torque's Reveal is
row 5 (rung 2), already flagship-homed at Torque.

## Does this prove the dependency graph works?

**Yes, for the compound path.** The two deepest compounds are now authorable using only
defined terms, in the graph's order — operators (tranche 2) before compounds (this
tranche), scoring (ADD) available to both. The "composition gap" the original pilot review
found (compounds written with undefined operators) is closed: every operator and concept
torque and blender stand on has an entry, and the entries were authored in prerequisite
order.

## The one gap the walk surfaced

**Cross-body and Spin (body elements) have no standalone entries.** They are graph Core
Concepts and torque/osis prerequisites, currently taught inline within Clipper Stall and
Osis. This does not block torque or blender — both read cleanly — but it is the last pair of
nodes on the compound path without their own homes. A curator call for James, not decided
here:

- **Option A — author two brief Core Concept entries** (Cross-body; Body elements / spin,
  duck, dive, jump), matching the other core-concept prototypes. Cleanest for graph
  completeness; cross-body in particular is a genuine hub (it defines the clipper).
- **Option B — ratify inline teaching** and record that cross-body and spin are taught
  within Clipper Stall and Osis rather than as standalone entries, and add a one-line
  cross-reference from those entries.

Recommendation: **Option A for Cross-body, Option B for spin.** Cross-body is high
out-degree (the whole clipper world routes through it) and earns a home; the body elements
are a looser bag of qualities better introduced where they first bite (spin at Osis). Either
way it is a small, non-doctrinal follow-up, not a blocker.

## Registry / status impact

None required. Torque and Blender were already flagship-homed for row 5; these refined
prototypes replace the first drafts and add nothing to the insight list. If James wants the
walk's finding tracked, the natural place is a one-line note that Cross-body / spin homes
are the remaining compound-path authoring item.
