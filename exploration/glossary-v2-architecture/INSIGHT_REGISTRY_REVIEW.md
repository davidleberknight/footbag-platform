# Insight Registry — Editorial Correction Note

Editorial corrections to the insight canon from James, recorded beside
`INSIGHT_REGISTRY.md`. This is a review note, not an edit: it explains the corrections
and gives a revised recommendation. The registry itself is unchanged until these are
ratified. No code, no production glossary content.

This note has been through two rounds of correction, and both mattered. The first
editorial review (in conversation) wrongly proposed merging the whirl/swirl insight
into "direction is structural." The first correction fixed that but framed the missing
axis as "leg assignment." The **second correction supersedes it**: the axis is not
which leg performs the dex — it is **surface-relative structure**, and the toe→clipper
analogy is not the one a beginner expects. This note records the final understanding.

---

## 1. The load-bearing fix: three levels of "different"

The canon was blurring three genuinely different kinds of "these two things differ,"
and a difference at each level means something different for the ontology.

1. **Structural identity** — the canonical operator or trick, read in its full frame.
   Two things with the same identity *are the same thing*; a naming merge is valid.
2. **Surface context** — a dex must be read relative to the **set surface, support leg,
   dexing leg, and receiving surface.** The *same abstract dex idea is a different trick
   in a different surface context.* This is structural, not decorative notation.
3. **Body-mechanics flavor** — how a trick *feels* to execute (e.g. "leggier"). This is
   **not** structural and **never** a separate canonical operator. Only *some* mechanical
   differences reach level 2; the rest are flavor, preserved as an execution/historical
   note.

The two worked examples that separate the levels:

- **Atomic and illusioning — level 1 (same identity), level-3 flavor only.** Illusioning
  is structurally identical to atomic: **same leg, same operator identity.** The merge
  (illusioning folded into atomic) is correct. Illusioning can *feel* more leggy — but
  that is body-mechanics flavor (level 3), not a surface-context difference (level 2) and
  not a separate operator (level 1). The flavor is preserved as a note; it does not
  reopen the merge. This is the **cautionary case**: a real, felt mechanical difference
  that does *not* create a separate structure.
- **The clipper world — level 2 (surface context).** Whirl and swirl are not an isolated
  exception and not a "different dexing leg" pair. They are the clipper-context
  instantiation of toe-context ideas, and the mapping must be *re-derived*, not
  transferred (see §2).

The failure mode this guards against runs both ways: mistaking a level-3 flavor
(illusioning's legginess) for a structural difference and wrongly splitting a settled
operator; or mistaking a level-2 surface-context difference for "just flavor" and
wrongly flattening it.

## 2. Surface context is structural — the correct analogy

The naive analogy is wrong and must not be taught:

- **Wrong:** *Mirage / Legover :: Whirl / Swirl.* Do not use this grid.
- **Wrong:** *Swirl is a clipper Legover.* It is not.

The correct, re-derived mapping across surface contexts:

| Toe context | Clipper context | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| **Mirage** | **Whirl** | Whirl is best understood as a **clipper-context Mirage** |
| **Around the World** | **Swirl** | Swirl is best understood as a **clipper-context Around the World** |

And direction stays a **separate** axis — every trick has a direction mirror within its
own surface context:

- Mirage / Illusion (toe direction mirrors)
- Around the World / Orbit (toe direction mirrors)
- **Whirl / Whip** (clipper direction mirrors; whip = reverse whirl)
- **Swirl / Reverse Swirl** (clipper direction mirrors)

The insight the grid teaches: **direction and surface context are independent axes, and
surface context gives the same dex logic a different family.** A dex does not mean the
same thing in the abstract; it must be read relative to its set surface, support leg,
dexing leg, and receiving surface. Toe-context analogies help, but they must be
**re-derived in clipper context, not transferred mechanically** — the beginner who
expects whirl:swirl to map like mirage:legover has transferred instead of re-derived,
and is wrong.

**Proposed insight wording:**

> **Surface context is structural.** The same dex idea becomes a different trick
> depending on its surface frame — the set surface, support leg, dexing leg, and
> receiving surface. Whirl is the clipper-context Mirage; Swirl is the clipper-context
> Around the World. Toe-context intuition transfers only after it is re-derived for the
> clipper context, and the honest analogy is rarely the obvious one.

## 3. The five corrections, applied

**Correction 1 — Do not collapse whirl/swirl into "direction is structural."** Withdrawn
the earlier merge call. Whirl/swirl are surface-context tricks (clipper-context Mirage
and clipper-context ATW), not direction mirrors of each other. The clean *direction*
examples are the true reversals: Mirage/Illusion, ATW/Orbit, Whirl/Whip,
Swirl/Reverse-Swirl.

**Correction 2 — Add "Surface context is structural"** (revised from the interim "leg
assignment is structural"). The axis is surface-relative reading, not leg identity per
se — leg roles are components of the surface frame, not the headline. Home example: the
clipper world (Whirl ≈ clipper Mirage, Swirl ≈ clipper ATW).

**Correction 3 — Guard "One operator can wear many names."** A name-merge is valid only
when structural identity *and* surface-context frame match. Atomic = illusioning
qualifies (same frame; the difference is level-3 flavor). The principle must never erase
a real surface-context distinction.

**Correction 4 — Soften "A trick is a journey between stalls."** Not universal — there
are notable non-stalling tricks (kicks, pure body tricks, dexterity-only sequences).
Prefer **"Stalls are anchor states"** (or "Many freestyle tricks are journeys between
stable contact states"), with the exception explicit.

**Correction 5 — Re-evaluate the count** (below), without forcing twelve, and without
demoting whirl/swirl — the overlap with direction was the error; the correct reading is
that whirl/swirl reveal the surface-context axis.

## 5. Revised count recommendation

**Stay at eleven — by swap, not growth.** Retire the standalone whirl/swirl row (−1),
add "Surface context is structural" (+1). Whirl/swirl are not demoted; they are the
clipper-world example of the axis they revealed. Do **not** force twelve: surface
context earns full insight status precisely because it is a distinct axis from
direction, which is why the swap keeps the number flat.

Two structural notes that follow:

- **There are now three independent geometry axes** — direction (1), side (2), surface
  context (new) — with a fourth (timing / execution window) still parked as the open
  frontier question (11). Several near-parallel "geometry is structural" reveals must be
  **spaced in the reading order and assembled by the Mirror Law essay**, never stacked as
  consecutive expanders. The surface-context insight in particular is the one most likely
  to be mis-taught by a mechanical analogy, so its entry must lead with the *re-derive,
  don't transfer* warning, not the grid.
- **Atomic/illusioning is promoted to a teaching centerpiece.** It is the cleanest case
  for separating the three levels — same identity, same frame, flavor-only difference —
  and it anchors insight 9 and is cross-referenced wherever the ladder is taught. It must
  never be reopened as a structural difference; the leggy feel is a preserved note.
