# Foundational Dex Pathways — Around the World / Orbit (Planning Review)

A short planning note on treating Around the World and Orbit as a **Foundational Dex
Pathway** — a teaching cluster, not a promoted family. Read-only; grounded in the live
dictionary notation. No code, no production-data change, no family-page implementation.

## The three-way distinction this establishes

| Category | Members | Surface |
|---|---|---|
| **First-Class Family Parents** | count-qualified major lineages (osis, whirl, mirage, legover, …) | ordinary family pages |
| **Anchor Family Parents** | Toe Stall, Clipper Stall | special anchor pages (curator override) |
| **Foundational Dex Pathways** | Around the World / Orbit and their variants | a **teaching cluster** — glossary cluster, foundational-skill page, or future teaching page; **not** an ordinary family page unless separately ruled |

The Dex Pathway is a fourth kind of surface reason, sitting beside "count-qualified lineage"
and "universal catch surface": **pedagogical primacy.** It is earned by being where a
learner starts, not by descendant count.

## Should ATW / Orbit get special treatment? — Yes, but as a pathway, not a family

Yes. Around the World is typically the first dexterity trick a player learns, and it teaches
the core dex idea in its purest form: set the bag, circle it, return to control. That value
is pedagogical, and the numbers confirm it is **not** count-based — ATW's live membership is
4 and Orbit's is 4, far below the family-parent threshold of more than ten. Promoting them
as ordinary family parents would be popularity-by-another-name and would misrepresent a
teaching primitive as a large lineage. The right treatment is a distinct pathway surface
that carries the teaching without the family-page roster model.

This also fits what already exists: Around the World is one of the six ruled Foundational
Physical Skills (the learner gateways). The Dex Pathway is the teaching cluster that gateway
anchors — the Skill entry is the single door; the pathway is the small, curated set of forms
taught around it. No new insight is created (Direction already threads ATW/Orbit); this is a
teaching-surface concept, so the insight registry is unaffected.

## Public label

Recommended design label: **Foundational Dex Pathway** ("pathway" captures the
learn-it-first, build-from-here progression better than "cluster," which only says
"group"). Because "dex" is in-house vocabulary, the public heading should gloss it on first
use per the copy standard — for example *"Around the World: a foundational dexterity
pathway"* with a one-line plain-language subtitle (*"the first circling dexterities, and
what they teach"*). Do **not** label it a "family" anywhere; the whole point is that it is
not one.

## Which tricks belong in the teaching cluster — and their status

Grounded in the live notation. The cluster's spine is the same-side dex that sets from the
toe, circles, and returns to a same-side toe stall.

**Strict members (ATW / Orbit terminal identity — same-side dex, same-side toe return):**
- `around_the_world` (2) — `TOE > SAME IN/OUT [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]` — the full orbit.
- `orbit` (2) — `TOE > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]` — ATW's direction mirror.
- `double_around_the_world` (3), `triple_around_the_world` (4) — the same orbit repeated.
- `double_orbit` (3), `triple_orbit` (4) — the orbit repeated.

**Related variants (same orbit structure, different surface — present as related, not
necessarily formal members):**
- `around_the_world_heel` (3), `double_around_the_world_heel` (4) — a heel ending instead of
  the toe (`SAME HEEL [UNS]`).
- `inside_around_the_world` (2) — `INSIDE > SAME IN [DEX] > SAME INSIDE [DEL]` — the orbit read
  from the inside surface.
- `outside_around_the_world` (2) — `OUTSIDE > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME OUTSIDE [DEL]` — read from
  the outside surface.

**Derived / marked (show as ATW-derived examples, clearly not formal family members):**
- `around_the_world_kick` (1), `orbit_kick` (1) — the dex without a landing stall (`[KICK]`);
  a dex-only form, derived.
- **DATW to sole** — see below; a derived surface-ending example, not a canonical row today.

**Comparison only (a teaching neighbor, never a member):**
- `pixie` — used to explain the shared-opening bridge (see below); it is a different trick on
  the side axis and never a cluster member.

The direction/reverse variants belong only **where the dictionary already settles them** —
ATW and Orbit are the settled direction mirror; do not manufacture reverse forms that the
data does not carry.

## Strict members vs related / derived — the boundary rule

- **Strict** = same terminal identity: a same-side dex (in or out) off the toe returning to a
  same-side toe stall, and its repeated forms (double/triple ATW and orbit). These share the
  orbit's structural signature.
- **Related** = the same orbit structure on a different surface (heel ending, inside/outside
  entry). Shown as related because the surface frame differs; do not fold them in as if the
  cluster were one flat family.
- **Derived / marked** = kicks (no stall) and surface-ending illustrations like DATW-to-sole.
  Present with an explicit "derived" or "related" tag.

This satisfies the rule not to imply every ATW-like trick belongs to one formal ATW family:
the cluster is a **teaching grouping with internal tiers**, not a single roster.

## How to describe DATW-to-sole

As a **derived, related example, not a formal member**: *"a Double Around the World that
lands on the sole instead of the toe — an ATW-derived surface-ending variant."* The current
dictionary does not carry a distinct DATW-to-sole canonical row, which is itself the reason
to present it as an illustrative derived form rather than a listed member. If it is ever
canonicalized, it would sit in the "related variant" tier (same orbit, different ending
surface), never as a strict member. Mark it "derived" wherever it appears.

## How to explain the pixie-like structure safely

The notation supports a precise, bounded statement:

- Around the World: `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]`
- Pixie: `TOE > SAME IN [DEX] > OP TOE [DEL]`

They share the **exact same opening move** — a same-side inward dex set off the toe — and
**diverge only at the landing**: ATW comes home to the *same* foot (closing the orbit), the
pixie crosses to the *other* foot. Safe teaching line: *"Around the World and the pixie start
the same way — a dex on your own side off the toe. They part at the finish: the Around the
World lands back on the same foot, the pixie crosses to the other. That is why the Around the
World is a natural first feel for the pixie's dex."*

Guard rails (all required):
- Ground it only in the shared `SAME IN [DEX]` opening and the divergent terminal side — both
  are in the notation.
- Do **not** call ATW and pixie the same family: same-side vs opposite-side landing is a real
  structural (side) axis, so they are different tricks.
- Do **not** generalize ("every ATW-like trick is a pixie", or the reverse). The bridge is a
  single, concrete adjacency, framed as a learning stepping-stone, not a taxonomic claim.

## Effect on the lesser-lineage histogram recommendation

It refines it, and strengthens the earlier finding. The histogram flagged `around_the_world`
(4) and `orbit` (4) as entry primitives wrongly surfaced by a raw count-ranked list. This
ruling gives them a **positive home** — the Foundational Dex Pathway — instead of only
"excluded from families." So:

- **Remove ATW and Orbit from any lesser-lineage reading entirely.** They are not lesser
  families; they are a distinct pathway category. A count histogram that lists them as
  small lineages actively mis-teaches.
- This is the clearest proof of the histogram review's core caveat: count-ranking buries a
  high-teaching-value cluster among low-count noise. The standing recommendation holds —
  defer any public lesser-lineage histogram, and route the genuinely teachable roots (ATW /
  Orbit here) to purpose-built teaching surfaces rather than a ranked list.
- Rake and Pendulum are **not** swept up by this: the pathway is earned by pedagogical
  primacy (first dex learned, teaches the core dex idea), which ATW/Orbit have and Rake and
  Pendulum do not. This ruling is not a backdoor to promote small roots by popularity.

## What this note does not do

No code, no production-data change, no family page, no roster. It records the category and
its boundaries. Whether the pathway is realized as a glossary cluster, the Around the World
Foundational-Skill page, or a future teaching page is a later, separate decision.
