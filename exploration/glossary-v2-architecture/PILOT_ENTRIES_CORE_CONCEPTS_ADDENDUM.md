## Cross-body
**Core Concept · connective**

**Line.** Cross-body describes what the working foot does with the body: instead of dexing
or catching the bag out in front of you, on your own open side, the foot reaches across and
works behind the opposite leg. The legs cross, and the bag is handled on the far side rather
than the near one.

**Relates.** Cross-body is the body relationship that defines the clipper: a clipper stall
is a cross-body catch, the foot crossed under the opposite leg (see Clipper Stall). It is
the shared signature of the atoms that finish there — whirl, swirl, butterfly, and osis all
land cross-body on the clipper — which is why they cluster as one branch of the sport, and
why their compounds do too: torque and blender are both an osis carried to a cross-body
clipper landing. It is a body *configuration*, not a travel direction: a whirl reaches
across to the opposite side and a swirl stays on its own side, yet both finish cross-body,
so cross-body is its own axis, separate from same-side versus opposite-side (see Side) and
from which surface catches the bag (that meaning lives at Surface Frame). In the notation it
is the `[XBD]` bracket, and because the bracket count is the score, the cross-body reach is a
scored part of the trick — it is exactly the extra point a whirl has over a mirage, whose
landing stays on the near-side toe (see ADD).

**Explore.** Clipper Stall (the cross-body catch it defines) · Whirl and Osis (atoms that
land cross-body) · Torque and Blender (their cross-body compounds) · ADD (why the cross-body
reach is a scored part).

*(No Reveal — connective by design. Cross-body is a prerequisite body relationship, not a
reframe. The reframe-level insight about a surface read inside its frame belongs to Surface
Frame, and the clipper's centrality is Clipper Stall's Reveal; a Reveal here would duplicate
one of those.)*

## Review

**Verdict: READY (pass).**

- **Physical first** — opens with the foot reaching across behind the opposite leg, a
  configuration a player can feel, before any notation or number.
- **Plain English** — no jargon ahead of its introduction; `[XBD]` appears only after
  cross-body is defined in body terms, and the one number claim (whirl scores one more than
  mirage) is concrete and checkable.
- **One useful relationship** — the coherent clipper cluster: the clipper stall it defines,
  the atoms that land there, their compounds, and the scored bracket. One thread, not a pile.
- **No earned insight** — correctly connective. It carries no Reveal, which is the right call
  and keeps the geometry/surface reveals where they belong.

**Doctrine risks: low, all grounded in the live notation.**

- The load-bearing claim that cross-body is a distinct axis from Side is verified: whirl
  finishes `OP CLIP [XBD]` (opposite side) and swirl finishes `SAME CLIP [XBD]` (same side),
  yet both are cross-body — so `[XBD]` is not the same as the opposite-side reading.
- The scoring claim is verified: mirage `SET > OP IN [DEX] > OP TOE [DEL]` is 2 and whirl
  `SET > OP IN [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` is 3, so the `[XBD]` reach is precisely the extra
  point. This is a concrete instance of the bracket-count-equals-ADD rule, not a new claim.
- The entry defers surface meaning to Surface Frame and the clipper's centrality to Clipper
  Stall, so it does not over-expand into either or duplicate their Reveals.

**Insight registry impact: none.** Cross-body is connective and introduces no new insight;
the count stays at eleven. This closes the last prerequisite gap the compounds walk found
without registry growth — the outcome the guidance asked for.

**Architecture impact: none.** No contradiction surfaced. The dependency graph already lists
Cross-body as its own Layer-1 concept beside Side, so a standalone Cross-body home is
consistent with the ratified structure rather than a change to it.

**Remaining inline concept:** Spin / body elements still have no standalone entry and stay
taught inline within Osis and Around the World, per the compounds walk. That remains a
separate, optional follow-up, not addressed here.
