# Boundary Analysis

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. It identifies the coordinates that resist assignment to a structural family and classifies why, without forcing any coordinate into a family the evidence does not support. Companion to `VOLUME_IV.md` and `taxonomy_catalog.csv`.

Of the 1920 geometric coordinates, 1216 assign cleanly to one of the eight primary families. The remaining 704 resist classification, and they resist for three distinct reasons, none of which is a true movement outlier.

## The three resistance classes

### Artifacts of Grammar v1: launch foreclosure (384 coordinates)

These are the blocked coordinates: a cross-body launch reaching for its own foreclosed first-dexterity face. They resist a structural family because they are not movements at all; the grammar refuses them. They are pure artifacts of the launch rule, and assigning them a leg-direction-receiver signature would be classifying a movement that cannot occur. Correct disposition: outside the taxonomy, as grammar artifacts.

### Artifacts of enumeration: midline collapse (192 coordinates)

These are the artifact coordinates: on a midline landing the receiver has no same-versus-opposite distinction, so the changed-receiver copy is a duplicate of the preserved one. They resist classification because their defining facet (receiver behavior) is degenerate on a central target: preserved and changed coincide, so the coordinate has no distinct receiver value. They are enumeration duplicates, not movements the taxonomy should separately hold. Correct disposition: outside the taxonomy, as enumeration artifacts, folded onto their preserved twin.

### Atlas boundaries: unresolved surface (128 coordinates)

These are the uncertain coordinates: every coordinate whose landing is the unresolved peak surface. They resist classification not because they are ill-formed but because the atlas itself has not settled the surface, so their receiver behavior cannot be read. They are unresolved because of an atlas boundary carried from Volume I, not because of anything about the movement. Correct disposition: held at the boundary until the surface is ruled, exactly as the earlier volumes held it.

## True outliers and bridges: none in the geometry, a few in the corpus

Within the pure geometry there are **no true structural outliers**: every valid coordinate has a clean leg-direction-receiver signature, because the space is a uniform hypercube with no irregular corners. Nothing sits between families or defies the facets; the facets are total.

The only movements that behave like bridges appear in the real corpus, not the geometry, and they bridge to the higher operator layer rather than between structural families:

- **Ambiguous-side movements** (the 40 corpus rows that write an either-side marker) cannot be placed on the leg facet, because their leg relation is deliberately left open. They are bridges in the precise sense that one notation spans both a same-leg and an alternating-leg reading. They resist not the taxonomy but a determinate leg value.
- **Body-mediated joins** (91 corpus rows) carry a body event between the two dexterities. Their dexterity pair still has a clean facet signature, so they assign, but the body event is an unmodeled higher-layer axis, so they sit on the bridge between the pure two-dex taxonomy and the operator layer. They are classified by their dexterity relation and flagged as carrying a higher-layer feature, not forced to pretend the body event is absent.

## Summary

| Resistance class | Count | Kind | Disposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| launch foreclosure | 384 | Grammar v1 artifact | outside the taxonomy (not a movement) |
| midline collapse | 192 | enumeration artifact | outside the taxonomy (a duplicate) |
| unresolved surface | 128 | atlas boundary | held until the surface is ruled |
| ambiguous side | 40 (corpus) | bridge (open leg reading) | flagged, not forced |
| body-mediated join | 91 (corpus) | bridge to the operator layer | classified by the dex pair, feature-flagged |

The headline is that the geometry produces no genuine outliers: everything that resists a family does so because it is an artifact, a boundary the atlas already marked, or a movement that bridges to a higher layer the two-dex facets do not cover. Nothing was forced into a family, and nothing needed to be: the resistant coordinates are all accounted for by the grammar, the enumeration, the atlas boundary, or the operator layer, never by a movement the taxonomy simply cannot place.
