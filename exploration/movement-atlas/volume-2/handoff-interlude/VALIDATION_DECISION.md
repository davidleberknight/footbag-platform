# Validation Decision

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. It chooses one next step from the four offered and justifies it from the validation evidence. It starts no work, repairs no grammar, and restores no count.

## The four options

1. The state model is sufficiently validated and can support a bounded correction of Volume II.
2. The state model needs one small extension and another validation pass.
3. Notation alone cannot resolve the handoff, and outside video or expert evidence is now required.
4. The proposed state-transition approach is refuted and should be replaced.

## The decision

**Option 2: the state model needs an extension and another validation pass, run against the notation, before anything else.** Option 3 is the correct eventual step for a specific, narrow part of the model, and the decision records exactly which part, but option 2 is the immediate move.

## Why option 2, from the evidence

The validation gave a two-sided result, and the two sides point to two different next actions, only one of which is immediate.

- **The model is incomplete, and the missing piece is notation-visible.** A majority of the corpus (59% of rows) forces a state variable the four-part model lacks, dominated by the intervening body-or-orientation event in 38.5% of rows, with a richer direction vocabulary and a per-dexterity paradox modifier close behind. All three of these are already written in the notation. So the extension is well-specified and, crucially, **testable in the same notation-only lane**: add the body-or-orientation join component, widen the direction axis, and add the paradox modifier slot, then re-run the reconstruction over the same 395 rows and measure whether the missing-variable class empties. That is an extension plus another validation pass, which is exactly option 2.

- **Two components are notation-invisible, and that is a different problem for later.** The reference-foot identity and the leg parity are absent from 100% of the notation. No extension and no re-validation in this lane can recover them, because there is nothing in the tokens to recover. Those two components are where option 3 applies: resolving them will require evidence from outside the notation. But that step is not next, because it is premature to seek outside evidence for two components while the notation-visible extension has not yet been made and re-validated. Option 3 is the eventual step for the reference-foot and leg-parity sub-question only, and the decision names it so it is not lost.

- **Option 1 is unavailable.** The model cannot support even a bounded correction of Volume II yet, because zero rows reconstruct from encoded state and a majority force a missing variable. A correction built now would inherit the incompleteness.

- **Option 4 is wrong.** The approach is not refuted. No row is inconsistent with the model, the diagonal resolved cleanly at the operational layer, and carriage and stance held. The failures are systematic and named, which is the signature of a model to extend, not one to discard.

## The sequence this implies

Extend the model with the three notation-visible additions (body-or-orientation join event, richer direction, paradox modifier) and re-validate against the same 395 rows (option 2). If the extended model empties the missing-variable class, the only remaining gap is the two notation-invisible components, and that gap is the precise trigger for the outside-evidence step (option 3), scoped to the reference foot and leg parity alone. A bounded correction of Volume II (option 1) becomes available only after the extended model reconstructs the notation-visible state cleanly and the two invisible components are either resolved or explicitly marked unresolved. The transport-rule proposal is the hypothesis the re-validation and the eventual outside-evidence pass should attack.

## Discipline

This decision makes no movement fit by assumption, counts no row as validated on a convenient reading, infers no impossibility, and repairs no grammar. It records that the state-transition approach survived, that the specific four-part model did not survive as complete or notation-recoverable, and that the next move is a notation-visible extension and re-validation, with the outside-evidence step held in reserve for the two components the notation provably cannot carry.
