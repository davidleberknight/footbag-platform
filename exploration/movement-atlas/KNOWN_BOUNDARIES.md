# Known Boundaries: where the Atlas stops

Status: exploratory research under `exploration/`. Not production data, not doctrine, not a change to the live dictionary. This file states, plainly, where the Movement Atlas (Volume I) intentionally ends. Everything beyond these edges is out of scope for the atlas as it stands; future work begins here. Naming a boundary is not a plan to cross it.

The atlas maps the movement universe generated by Grammar v1 within the frozen 0-and-1-dexterity bound. Its edges are deliberate, and they fall into two kinds: edges of scope (the map could extend here on the same grammar, but this volume does not) and edges of knowledge (the grammar itself has not settled what lies here).

## Edges of scope: the map could extend, this volume does not

- **Higher dexterity.** The atlas stops at one dexterity. Two-, three-, and higher-dex movements are the natural next volumes: the same fiber-and-cube coordinate system extends to them, but the cube becomes a product of cubes and the census grows fast, and this volume does not generate it. The grammar-closure work flagged that higher-dex generation should not begin until the primitive floor is settled (below), so this boundary is held on purpose.

- **Composition and transitions.** The atlas maps single movements, not sequences. Whether one movement can follow another, which is the combo-compatibility question, is a separate research branch with its own parked index and concept spec under `exploration/`. The atlas gives that branch its coordinate system (a transition is an edge between two atlas locations), but it does not draw those edges.

- **Naming and promotion.** The atlas records which coordinates are named and which are not; it never proposes a name or a promotion. Turning an unnamed location into a named one is a curator and doctrine act, outside the map. The map is the input to that decision, not the decision.

## Edges of knowledge: the grammar has not settled what lies here

- **Unresolved grammar primitives.** The bare-surface kicks and the pogo family score a point with no scored generator in the four-generator basis. The atlas marks these coordinates uncertain and stops; it does not rule whether a fifth primitive exists. That ruling is the gate the grammar-closure study named, and it is upstream of any higher-dex volume.

- **The uncertain surface.** The peak surface is unresolved as a landing versus a timing marker, so every peak fiber is marked uncertain rather than mapped as terrain. The atlas will not treat peak as a settled surface until that question is answered.

- **Parameter grades.** The frozen grammar leaves the grade of a rotational dexterity, a paradox, and a no-plant posture unpinned. The atlas maps the plain, planted, non-rotational, non-paradox core and does not enumerate those parameter variants, because it cannot score them without inventing a rule. They are terrain the map cannot yet grade.

## The hard edge: physical realizability

The atlas never claims a coordinate is or is not physically possible. Grammar-valid means well-formed under the movement language, nothing more. Whether a human can perform a given coordinate is a biomechanics question the atlas is deliberately silent on. This is the firmest boundary of all: the map describes the movement language, not the body. A future biomechanics layer would attach a realizability judgment to each atlas location, but that judgment is not in this map and must never be read into an "unnamed" or "valid" status.

## Summary of the edges

| Boundary | Kind | What lies beyond |
|---|---|---|
| Higher dexterity | scope | two-and-higher-dex volumes on the same coordinate system |
| Composition / transitions | scope | edges between atlas locations (combo compatibility) |
| Naming / promotion | scope | curator and doctrine acts on mapped locations |
| Unresolved primitives (pogo, bare kick) | knowledge | whether a fifth primitive exists |
| The peak surface | knowledge | whether peak is a landing or a timing marker |
| Parameter grades (rotation, paradox, posture) | knowledge | how to score the parameter variants |
| Physical realizability | hard edge | biomechanics; a body can or cannot perform a location |

The atlas is complete within its stated bound and silent beyond it. Every future branch of the Trick Universe project begins at one of these edges, and each can now begin by naming the atlas locations it concerns rather than reasoning from isolated trick names.
