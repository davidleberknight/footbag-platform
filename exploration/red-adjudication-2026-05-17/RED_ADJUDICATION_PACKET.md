## 1.2 — Barraging — single modifier or two-dex set?

**Affected tricks:** Barrage, Barraging Osis (Baroque), Furious tricks, anything Barraging-prefixed.

**Why it matters:** Two readings of "Barraging" both have defenders:

## 1.5 — Compression intent — intentional or retrospective?

**Affected:** pedagogy framing across the trick dictionary. No ADD values change.

**Why it matters:** The compression chain we teach: Osis → Miraging Osis (= Torque) → Spinning ss Torque (= Mobius). Your 2026-05-15 ruling adds: Mobius ≈ Gyro Torque.

Did the community **author** short names by compressing long ones (intentional shorthand), or do we **reconstruct** short names by reading long structural decompositions retrospectively?

- **A. Intentional shorthand.** Long forms came first; community compressed to short names. "Mobius decompresses to Spinning Miraging Osis" describes the language's actual authoring mechanism.
- **B. Retrospective interpretation.** Short names came first; long forms are structural readings applied after the fact. "Mobius can be read as Spinning Miraging Osis" is illumination, not authoring.
- **C. Mixed.** Some compressions are intentional (where long form predates short); others are retrospective. Per-token determination.

Affects glossary phrasing directly. Under A we'd write "compressed to"; under B "can be read as"; under C per-trick annotation. The Mobius / Gyro Torque case is the test: did "Gyro Torque" exist first and the community compress to "Mobius," or did "Mobius" exist first?
