# Question for Red: can Bedwetter begin directly from a clipper delay?

One question, self-contained. Nothing changes until you answer.

Our notation for Bedwetter starts it from a toe delay (toe set, then three dexterities,
ending back on the setting toe). But four independent historical Sick 3 performances,
spanning 2004 to 2019, enter Bedwetter directly from a trick that ends on a CLIPPER
delay (after Barraging Butterfly in 2008, after Blurriest in 2004, after Phoenix in
2019, and after Atomic Drifter in both 2008 and 2009).

The question: is Bedwetter performable directly out of a clipper delay, so that the
clipper-held bag is lifted straight into the Bedwetter movement without an intermediate
toe set? If yes, is that the same trick (a flexible set), or a distinct variant worth
its own entry?

Our recommended reading, unless you say otherwise: the four independent performances
suggest the entry is flexible in practice, and the toe-set spelling in our notation is
one canonical choice among historically valid sets, not a constraint. We would then
treat Bedwetter's entry as flexible for sequence analysis while leaving the notation
itself for you to confirm.
