# Curator decision: the two readings of "Whirlwind"

Self-contained. The two affected records stay unresolved until decided; the alias row
and both candidate tricks are frozen. Nothing in production has been changed.

## The two competing resolutions

**Modern, currently live (5 ADD).** The dictionary alias maps "whirlwind" to Sole
Survivor, the folk-named canonical for Spinning Symposium Whirl: spinning (+1) +
symposium (+1) + whirl (3) = 5. The alias arrived with the curator-ruled merge of the
spinning-symposium-whirl row into the folk-named canonical (the folk name survived, and
that row's aliases, whirlwind among them, transferred wholesale).

**Footbag.org-era (6 ADD).** The legacy footbag.org move list itself (move page 254, in
our committed snapshot) names "Whirlwind" as Spinning PARADOX Symposium Whirl: spinning
(+1) + paradox (+1) + symposium (+1) + whirl (3) = 6, one paradox harder. The
FootbagMoves observational corpus asserts the same identity with full notation, and its
ingestion note already flagged the collision with our live alias.

## Where it bites

Two historical Sick 3 records read "Blurry Whirl > Paradox Symposium Whirl > Whirlwind":
the 2002 Western Regionals (Ryan Mulroney, 2nd) and the 2003 SEAPA NZ championships.
Under the live alias each totals 15 ADD; under the footbag.org-era reading, 16. The
sequence context supports either: as a 6-ADD trick the closer escalates the middle trick
by adding spinning; as a 5-ADD trick it contrasts by swapping paradox for spinning.

## Recommendation

Adopt an era-scoped reading rather than a single winner: keep the live alias
(whirlwind resolves to Sole Survivor for search and navigation; it is already
curator-ruled), and record the footbag.org-era sense (Spinning Paradox Symposium Whirl,
6 ADD) in the divergence registry under the record-don't-adopt policy, so that
historical-record resolution can cite the era-appropriate sense. **Preserve the
2002-2003 records unresolved until the era-scoped reading is accepted.** If a single
reading must eventually be chosen for those two records, the footbag.org-era 6-ADD
sense is the better historical fit: both records predate the folk-name lineage, and
footbag.org was that era's naming authority.
