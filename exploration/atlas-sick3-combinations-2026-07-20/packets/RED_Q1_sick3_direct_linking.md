# Question for Red: did Sick 3's three tricks have to link directly?

One question, self-contained. Nothing changes until you answer.

In a Sick 3 contest, a player nominates and hits three tricks. Did those three tricks
have to link directly, meaning the delay that ends one trick is itself the set of the
next trick, with no other bag contact in between? Or was it acceptable to make an
unnamed setup contact between tricks (for example, catching trick one on a toe delay,
then making a plain clipper set to start trick two) with the run still counting as a
legitimate Sick 3?

Context you may recognize: period event pages describe the format as "best combination
of three tricks you can string together" and "whoever hits the phattest 3 trick comb
(out of ten tries)," but no page we can find states the intermediate-contact rule
explicitly. Out of 117 historical Sick 3 sequences we reconstructed from old results
postings, 110 chain directly under current notation; the handful that do not all funnel
through one or two specific tricks, which looks more like a trick-notation question than
a rules question.

Our recommended reading, unless you say otherwise: "combo" meant DIRECT linking, and the
few sequences that do not chain point at trick-entry questions (asked separately), not
at a bridging allowance.
