# Atlas A1 — Osis terminal anatomy

Research dossier. Lanes are marked **[Observed]**, **[Derived]**, **[Ruled]**
throughout; only Ruled may be cited as authority. No promotion or notation
change is proposed here. All formulas are the stored operational notation of
active canonical tricks, read on 2026-07-15.

---

## 1. Thesis and headline findings

Osis is the member of the clipper-terminal family whose pre-terminal engine is a
**body spin, not a dexterity**. Its base carries no dex at all. That makes it the
right test of whether a dexterity-centred theory of the vocabulary is complete:
it is not. Osis is best modelled as a **terminal architecture** — a body rotation
that resolves into a cross-body clipper catch — onto which entries and further
spins are layered.

Top findings (each detailed below, each Derived from the stored notation):

1. The **osis terminal signature is conserved** across the whole family:
   `… (back|front) SPIN [BOD] > <side> CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`. A body spin into a
   cross-body clipper delay. It is written two different ways (unbundled
   `SPIN > CLIP`, and a bundled single `OSIS [BOD]` token) for the same movement.
2. The **osis is a spin where whirl / butterfly / swirl are a dex.** Same
   terminal (cross-body clipper), different engine. This is the base identity.
3. **Torque, blender, reverse-torque, reverse-blender are the four cells of a
   dex-side × terminal-side matrix** — identical but for their two side markers —
   exactly analogous to the ratified whirl/reverse-whirl/swirl/reverse-swirl grid,
   but with an inward dex plus a spin instead of a bare dex.
4. The **names do not map cleanly onto those cells.** `quantum osis` (a separate
   row) is structurally the same cell as `blender`; `torque`'s aliases are
   *miraging osis* and *stepping opposite osis*, not *quantum osis*. Namesake and
   structural descendant have come apart here.
5. **Spin degree is not in the notation.** `spinning osis` and `gyro osis` are
   byte-identical but for terminal side, yet the names assert 360° vs 180°. The
   notation encodes spin *direction* (via the `(front)`/`(back)` pre-state) but
   not spin *degree*.
6. The **operator readings of the named bases live in aliases, not in
   modifier-links.** Torque, blender, and flux carry `base_trick = osis` and no
   registered modifier; their "X osis" decomposition is prose/alias-level only.

## 2. [Observed] The corpus

**The base.** `osis` — ADD 3:

```
SET > (back or front) SPIN [BOD] > SAME/OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]
```

Three scoring brackets, zero `[DEX]`: `[BOD]` (the spin) + `[XBD]` (cross-body) +
`[DEL]` (the delay). The entry is a flexible `SET`; the spin direction is
`(back or front)`; the terminal side is `SAME/OP` (unresolved, read from
configuration). This is the minimum osis.

**The conserved terminal.** Every active osis-family compound ends in the same
tail — a body spin resolving into a cross-body clipper delay — in one of two
written forms:

- **Unbundled** (the majority): `(back) SPIN [BOD] > <side> CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`.
- **Bundled** (the duck-family osis rows — `ducking_osis`, `weaving_osis`,
  `zulu_osis`): `<side> OSIS [BOD] [XBD] [DEL]`, i.e. a single `OSIS [BOD]` token
  standing in for `SPIN > CLIP`.

The same physical terminal is thus written two ways. That is a representation
choice, not two movements.

**Family size.** 57 active tricks resolve to `trick_family = osis` (base `osis`
directly, or the named bases torque / blender / flux / twirl / the duck-osis and
set-osis compounds). ADD spans 3 (the base) to 7 (`spinning miraging symposium
torque`).

## 3. [Derived] What defines osis identity

### 3.1 Osis is a spin where whirl / butterfly / swirl are a dex

Read the four single-engine clipper-terminal atoms side by side:

| atom | engine | formula |
|---|---|---|
| whirl | OP inward **dex** | `SET > OP IN [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| butterfly | OP outward **dex** | `SET > OP OUT [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| swirl | SAME outward **dex** | `SET > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| **osis** | body **spin** | `SET > (back\|front) SPIN [BOD] > SAME/OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |

All four share the terminal (a cross-body clipper delay). Whirl, butterfly, and
swirl reach it through a **dexterity**; osis reaches it through a **body spin**.
Osis is therefore not a fifth dex — it is the same terminal driven by the body
instead of the foot. **The minimum structure that makes an osis an osis is:
a body spin that resolves into a cross-body clipper catch.** No dex is required,
and the base has none.

This is the concrete refutation of a dexterity-only model: a whole productive
family sits on a zero-dex base.

### 3.2 What distinguishes osis from its neighbours

- **vs a "spinning clipper."** A bare spin into a cross-body clipper *is* the
  osis minimum; osis is the name for that slot. There is no separate
  spinning-clipper atom to distinguish — osis occupies it.
- **vs spinning butterfly.** `spinning_butterfly` =
  `CLIP > (back) SPIN [BOD] > OP OUT [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`: a spin
  **prepended to a butterfly that keeps its own OUT dex**. Osis has no base dex,
  so `spinning_osis` is `CLIP > SPIN > SPIN > OP CLIP` — **spin + spin**, because
  the osis's own engine is the second spin. The difference is exactly: butterfly
  contributes a dex, osis contributes a spin.
- **vs gyro / spinning clipper-rotations generally.** Same point: where a
  dex-atom keeps its dex under a body operator, osis stacks a second spin.
- **vs torque / blender.** These are osis compounds, not separate families:
  a single inward dex prepended to the osis engine (§3.3).

### 3.3 The dex-side × terminal-side matrix (torque / blender / reverse pair)

Four compounds are identical except for their two side markers:

```
SET > <side> IN [DEX] > (back) SPIN [BOD] > <side> CLIP [XBD] [DEL]     (all ADD 4)
```

| dex side ↓ \ terminal side → | **OP CLIP** | **SAME CLIP** |
| **OP IN dex** | `torque` | `blender` |
| **SAME IN dex** | `reverse_blender` | `reverse_torque` |

This is a clean two-axis lattice, structurally the same shape as the ratified
whirl/reverse-whirl/swirl/reverse-swirl clipper matrix — but the pre-terminal
element is `IN dex + spin` rather than a single dex. Torque and blender differ
**only in terminal clipper side** (OP vs SAME); the reverse pair flips the dex
side.

**The namesake collision.** The names do not sit on these cells the way the grid
suggests:

- `blender`'s alias is *whirling osis*; `torque`'s aliases are *miraging osis*
  and *stepping opposite osis* — **not** *quantum osis*.
- `quantum_osis` is a **separate active row**:
  `TOE > OP IN [DEX] > (back) SPIN [BOD] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` — an OP-IN dex
  into a SAME-CLIP terminal, which is **blender's cell**, differing only by the
  declared entry surface (`TOE` vs `SET`).

So the published equation *quantum osis = torque* (§5) and the stored structure
disagree: by side markers, `quantum_osis` lands on `blender`, not `torque`. This
is a **namesake-versus-descendant** question for a later ruling, not resolved
here — flagged, with the evidence, in the research lane.

### 3.4 The spin engine has internal variety the base hides

The pre-terminal engine is not always one spin. Reading the zero-dex and
body-modified rows:

| compound | engine (pre-terminal) | terminal | note |
|---|---|---|---|
| `osis` | one spin `(back\|front)` | SAME/OP CLIP | base |
| `spinning_osis` | **two** back-spins | OP CLIP | second spin added |
| `gyro_osis` | **two** back-spins | SAME CLIP | *identical to spinning_osis but for terminal side* |
| `inspinning_osis` | `(front)` spin then `(back)` spin | SAME CLIP | direction pair, not degree |
| `double_spinning_osis` | **three** back-spins | OP CLIP | repetition ladder |

**Finding.** `spinning_osis` and `gyro_osis` are the same formula but for terminal
side, yet their names assert different rotation *degrees* (spinning ≈ 360°, gyro
≈ 180°). The notation writes both as two `(back) SPIN [BOD]` tokens: **spin degree
is not encoded.** Spin *direction* is (the `(front)`/`(back)` pre-state
distinguishes `inspinning_osis`), but degree is carried only by the operator name.
This is the osis instance of "what is lost by SAME/OP + IN/OUT": here the loss is
in the body axis, not the dex axis.

### 3.5 The osis terminal graph

```
  ENTRY  ─ set surface: SET | TOE | CLIP
    │      (optional) entry dex or set operator:
    │        pixie / fairy / stepping / quantum  = one IN/OUT set-dex prepended
    ▼
  PRE-TERMINAL BODY ENGINE  ─ one of:
    │   • single spin        (back|front) SPIN [BOD]           ← the base engine
    │   • inspin              (front) SPIN > (back) SPIN        ← inspinning
    │   • double / triple spin  SPIN > SPIN [ > SPIN ]          ← spinning / gyro / double
    │   • duck-or-dive then spin  DUCK|DIVE [BOD] > SPIN        ← ducking / diving / weaving / zulu
    │   • path dex then spin   <side> FRONT SWIRL [DEX] > SPIN  ← twirl (see A3)
    ▼
  CROSS-BODY CLIPPER RECEIVING  ─ <side> CLIP [XBD]   (side = SAME | OP)
    │   written unbundled  … SPIN [BOD] > <side> CLIP [XBD] [DEL]
    │   or bundled          <side> OSIS [BOD] [XBD] [DEL]
    ▼
  TERMINAL DELAY  ─ [DEL]
```

Two independent axes govern the whole family: the **terminal side** (SAME vs OP
clipper) and the **pre-terminal engine** (which spin, or which entry-dex-plus-spin).
The entry surface is a third, weaker axis (SET/TOE/CLIP), often set by the leading
set operator rather than a free choice.

### 3.6 Placing the family on the graph (what each modifier adds)

| modifier class | example rows | what it adds to the osis engine |
|---|---|---|
| **set operators** (prepend one set-dex) | `pixie_osis` (TOE SAME IN), `fairy_osis` (TOE SAME OUT), `stepping_osis` (CLIP OP IN), `quantum_osis` (TOE OP IN) | a **dex entry** before the spin; terminal preserved |
| **named single-dex bases** | `torque`, `blender`, `flux`, `reverse_torque`, `reverse_blender`, `reverse_swirling_osis` | one inward/outward dex + spin; a **matrix cell** (§3.3), read by side markers |
| **body operators** (add rotation) | `spinning_osis`, `gyro_osis`, `inspinning_osis`, `double_spinning_osis` | a **second (or third) spin**; no dex |
| **duck operators** | `ducking_osis`, `weaving_osis`, `zulu_osis`, `diving_osis` | a **duck/dive body action** before the spin; often the **bundled** `OSIS [BOD]` terminal form |
| **suspension** | `symposium_torque`, `spinning_symposium_flux`, `surfing_osis` | a `(no plant while)` support-leg constraint on a dex, over an osis chassis |
| **path dex** | `twirl`, `reverse_whirling_twirl`, `fender` | a **rotational (FRONT/BACK SWIRL/WHIRL) dex** before the spin — the bridge to A3 (Twirl) |
| **stacked compounds** | `stepping_paradox_torque`, `spinning_miraging_symposium_torque`, `aeon_flux` | multiple of the above over a torque/flux chassis; ADD 5–7 |

Every one of these preserves the spin→cross-body-clipper terminal. The modifiers
transform the **entry and the engine**; none of them changes the **terminal
identity**. That is the concrete sense in which osis "is a terminal."

## 4. [Observed] The alias and registry layers

The operator decompositions of the named bases are recorded **unevenly**, which
is itself a finding:

- `torque`, `blender`, `flux`, `twirl` carry `base_trick = osis` and **no**
  `freestyle_trick_modifier_links` row. Their "X osis" reading exists only as an
  **alias** (`blender ← whirling osis`; `torque ← miraging osis / stepping
  opposite osis`; `flux ← atomic osis / illusioning osis`; `aeon_flux ← nuclear
  osis / nucleosis`; `osis ← frigidosis`; `barraging_osis ← baroque`).
- By contrast `quantum_osis`, `spinning_osis`, `gyro_osis`, `ducking_osis`, etc.
  **do** carry modifier-links (`quantum`, `spinning`, `gyro`, `ducking` …).

So two rows that a structural reading calls the same cell (`quantum_osis` and
`blender`, §3.3) are recorded on **different layers** — one as an operator stack,
one as a named base with an alias. Atlas should hold the operator decomposition as
one object across both layers; the public dictionary does not need to.

## 5. [Ruled] What is already published (do not re-derive as new)

- **The osis atom card** (Movement Basics): osis is the rotational cross-body
  atom — "SET + back-or-front spin + cross-body clipper catch," a cousin of
  whirl, swirl, and butterfly, and the base a single operator turns into a
  famous compound: **quantum osis is torque, whirling osis is blender**; other
  relationships (flux) are carried through the base identity and prose rather
  than a uniform modifier-link record.
- **Operator reference** is the authority for each operator's ADD/structure;
  these findings cite it, never restate its values.
- **Operator derivations** (doctrine): `super mario = spinning symposium torque`;
  "Double Down near Osis" (Motion) resolves through the osis tail; `Torquescrew`
  is torque's chassis with an added whirl. These are consistent with the terminal
  reading above.

Where §3.3 finds that the stored side markers put `quantum_osis` on blender's
cell rather than torque's, that is a **Derived tension with the Ruled equation**,
recorded for a future ruling — not a change to it.

## 6. Answers to the standard dossier questions

- **Identity.** A conserved terminal architecture: a body spin resolving into a
  cross-body clipper catch. Organised around a **terminal**, not an entry or a
  dex path.
- **Base vs operator vs compound vs alias vs historical.** Base engine: the spin.
  Named single-dex bases: torque, blender, flux (base_trick = osis). Operators:
  the set/body/duck/suspension modifiers that prepend entries and spins.
  Historical labels: frigidosis (osis), baroque (barraging osis), whirling osis
  (blender), miraging / stepping-opposite osis (torque), atomic / illusioning
  osis (flux), nuclear osis / nucleosis (aeon flux).
- **Independent axes.** (1) terminal side SAME/OP; (2) pre-terminal engine
  (which spin, or entry-dex + spin); (3) entry surface, weakly.
- **Real structural mirrors.** torque↔blender (terminal-side flip);
  torque↔reverse_blender and blender↔reverse_torque (dex-side flip);
  spinning_osis↔gyro_osis (terminal-side flip on a zero-dex double spin).
- **Formula collisions.** `quantum_osis` collides with `blender`'s cell (only the
  declared entry surface differs); `spinning_osis` and `gyro_osis` collide except
  for terminal side (spin degree not encoded).
- **What SAME/OP + IN/OUT loses.** Spin **degree** (gyro 180 vs spinning 360);
  the **bundled-vs-unbundled** terminal choice; and, at the margin, the exact
  front/back pass of the spin beyond the coarse pre-state.
- **Clean inheritors.** The set-operator and body-operator compounds inherit the
  terminal cleanly (they add entry or rotation and keep the tail).
- **Exceptions.** The duck-osis rows change the *notation form* of the terminal
  (bundled `OSIS [BOD]`); `twirl` inserts a rotational path dex (A3); the named
  bases carry their operator reading only in aliases (§4).
- **What Atlas should hold that the public view need not.** The two terminal
  encodings as one object; the dex-side × terminal-side lattice with the namesake
  collisions flagged; an explicit spin descriptor (degree + direction) since the
  notation under-encodes it; and the alias/registry layer split.

## 7. What this unlocks / handoffs

- **Zero-dex modelling.** A terminal-first representation that does not assume a
  dex, usable for any spin-terminated or receiving family.
- **Cleaner spinning / gyro / inspinning doctrine.** The degree-not-encoded
  finding is the concrete gap; A4 (operator order) and any future spin-descriptor
  work inherit it.
- **A general theory of terminal movements.** Osis is the first worked terminal;
  A5 generalises the base-cell/axes/engine/terminal representation.
- **Osis vs torque vs blender vs clipper-rotation clarity.** The matrix (§3.3) is
  the rigorous statement; the namesake collision is the open item.

**Open questions carried forward (research, not rulings):**

1. Does `quantum osis = torque` survive the side-marker evidence that puts
   `quantum_osis` on blender's cell, or are the two named differently for a real
   reason the notation drops? (For A4 / a future Red rider, not decided here.)
2. Should the osis terminal have one canonical notation form (unbundled
   `SPIN > CLIP` vs bundled `OSIS [BOD]`)? A representation question, not a
   scoring one — no ADD moves either way.
3. Is spin **degree** (gyro/spinning) worth an explicit descriptor, or is terminal
   side a faithful proxy for it in the osis family specifically?

These feed A2 (the swirl lattice, the same matrix shape on a dex base) and A4
(operator order and what a name's word order encodes).
