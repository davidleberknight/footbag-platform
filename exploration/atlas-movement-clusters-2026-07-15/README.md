# Atlas Phase — movement-cluster anatomy

Research, not promotion or doctrine change. Nothing in this directory alters the
published dictionary, the operator reference, or the doctrine papers. The
released v1.2 doctrine is the frozen publication baseline; Atlas studies the
model beneath it and is allowed to find flaws in that model without silently
changing anything downstream.

## Three lanes (kept explicitly separate in every dossier)

- **Observed** — what the names, descriptions, stored operational notation, and
  footage actually say. Cited from the live dictionary, not inferred.
- **Derived** — what follows from the current movement model when the observed
  formulas are read structurally. Clearly marked as derivation, with its proof.
- **Ruled** — what has actually been accepted for publication (the atom cards,
  the operator reference, the doctrine papers, the Red rulings). The only lane
  that may be cited as authority.

A finding that lives only in Observed or Derived is a research result, never a
ruling. Promotions and notation changes, if any ever follow, are separate,
scoped, and gated as usual.

## Dossiers (recommended order)

- **A1 — Osis terminal anatomy** (`A1_osis_terminal_anatomy.md`). A
  terminal-centred, largely zero-dex family; tests whether a dexterity-centred
  theory is complete. *Written.*
- **A2 — Swirl composition lattice** (`A2_swirl_composition_lattice.md`). Build
  outward from the ratified four-cell clipper-terminal base and verify every
  compound's inheritance. *Written.*
- **A3 — Twirl and the missing path axes.** Use notation collisions and legacy
  movement descriptions to show what side-plus-direction cannot express. *Next.*
- **A4 — Operator-order study.** What does name order mean structurally
  (Whirling Swirl vs Swirling Whirl, and the osis-order pairs)?
- **A5 — General cluster model.** Only after the case studies: a reusable Atlas
  representation (base cell, axes, entry state, movement sequence, terminal
  state, operator order, repetition, historical naming, formula equivalence,
  and unrepresented physical distinctions).

A1 and A2 together test both ends of the system — a terminal-centred family and
a dexterity-centred matrix. A3 then exposes what neither model captures.
