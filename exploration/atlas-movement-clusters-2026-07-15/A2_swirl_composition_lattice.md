# Atlas A2 — Swirl composition lattice

Research dossier. Lanes marked **[Observed]**, **[Derived]**, **[Ruled]**; only
Ruled may be cited as authority. No dictionary, doctrine, alias, family, or
promotion change is proposed. Movement events are described by **leg, direction,
order, entry state, terminal state** — never by legacy path tokens as the
primitive; legacy tokens (SWIRL/WHIRL/FRONT/BACK) appear only in the Observed
lane. The A1 quantum-osis/torque tension is out of A2's scope. Read 2026-07-15.

**Relational-coordinate caveat (governs the whole dossier).** `SAME` and `OP` are
**relational** labels — the dexing leg relative to a reference/support state at
that moment — not persistent names for the player's left or right leg. A2
compares **stored relational tokens**. It does not normalise each event into
persistent physical-leg identities across plants, support changes, or body
rotation. Any apparent `SAME`↔`OP` change after an earlier event must therefore
remain **unresolved** until a state-transition model is applied (an A3/A4 task).

---

## 1. Executive summary

- **The frozen four-cell base is clean on two axes:** dexing leg (SAME/OP) and dex
  direction (IN/OUT). In those four ruled rows the **dex-leg and terminal-side
  labels are perfectly correlated** (SAME-dex → SAME CLIP, OP-dex → OP CLIP), so
  the base corpus does not independently vary the two coordinates. A2 does **not**
  establish that one universally determines the other in compounds.
- **Repetition is repeating the same SAME-OUT cell**, a dex-count increase — but
  the two-circle case **forks in boundary encoding**: `swirling_swirl` (continuous
  `>`) and `ripstein` (= alias *double swirl*, major `>>`) carry the same ordered
  dex labels with a different connector strength. That is a represented sequencing
  distinction; its exact physical interpretation needs separate evidence.
- **The operators are not literal.** "Swirling" is usually an inserted SAME-OUT
  dex but is written OP-OUT in at least one row; "whirling" is written as an OP-IN
  dex in some rows and as a rotational FRONT/BACK path dex in others. The public
  "adds a swirl / adds a whirl" gloss does not hold across all active compounds.
- **Central result — Whirling Swirl and Swirling Whirl are distinct,
  order-sensitive stored compositions.** Their formulas are **not** related by a
  simple reversal of the written SAME/OP and IN/OUT tokens: the predicted
  second-dex leg label differs. This may be a genuine reference-frame transformation
  after the first dexterity, inconsistent authoring, or a limitation of treating
  locally relative coordinates as globally stable. **A2 cannot distinguish those
  explanations.** The last word reliably fixes the stored base and terminal.
- **Twirl is modeled in v1.2 as an osis-base compound** (`base_trick = osis`): a
  path-bearing dex into an osis-like spin-and-clipper terminal. That is strong
  evidence for osis-lineage treatment **in the current model**; it does not by
  itself settle Twirl's physical or historical identity. The path-token rows
  (twirl, reverse-whirling-twirl, toe-whirling-swirl, blink) are where leg ×
  direction stops carrying the corpus; they pass to A3.

## 3. [Ruled] The frozen four-cell base matrix

Do not reopen. Restated from the stored rows (all `SET` entry, one `[DEX]`):

| trick | leg | direction | terminal | formula |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| whirl | OP | IN | OP CLIP | `SET > OP IN [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| rev_whirl | OP | OUT | OP CLIP | `SET > OP OUT [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| swirl | SAME | OUT | SAME CLIP | `SET > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| rev_swirl | SAME | IN | SAME CLIP | `SET > SAME IN [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |

**[Derived] Which axes are genuinely independent.**

- **Dexing leg (SAME/OP)** and **dex direction (IN/OUT)** are two free axes — four
  cells, all present.
- **Terminal side vs leg — a four-row correlation, not an established law.** In
  these four ruled rows the leg and terminal-side labels are perfectly correlated
  (every SAME-dex cell terminates SAME CLIP; every OP-dex cell terminates OP CLIP).
  The base corpus therefore does not independently vary those two coordinates. A2
  does **not** establish that one universally determines the other in compounds;
  whether a compound can ever decouple them is an open lattice question (§7, §10).
- **Entry is abstracted to `SET`** in all four; it becomes a real (weak) axis only
  in compounds, usually fixed by the leading set operator (TOE/CLIP), not chosen
  freely.
- Held constant across the four: entry (`SET`), dex count (one), terminal mechanic
  (cross-body clipper delay). What defines each cell: the (leg, direction) pair.

## 4. [Derived] The composition lattice

Every swirl-lineage compound reads as: **entry → (operators/dexes in order) →
cross-body clipper terminal**, with the terminal side matching the last base word.
The lattice has four layers stacked on the base cell:

```
  ENTRY            SET | TOE | CLIP        (usually fixed by the leading set op)
    ▼
  LEADING DEX(ES)  inserted operator dexes, in written order
    ▼                each an event: <leg> <direction> [DEX]  (or a body action)
  BASE-CELL DEX    the terminating base circle (swirl = SAME OUT; rev_swirl = SAME IN)
    ▼
  TERMINAL         <side> CLIP [XBD] [DEL]   side matches the last base word
```

The rest of the dossier tests how faithfully each layer behaves.

## 5. [Observed → Derived] Repetition ladder

| name | circles | boundary | entry | ADD | formula |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| swirl | 1 × SAME OUT | — | SET | 3 | `SET > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| swirling_swirl | 2 × SAME OUT | `>` continuous | CLIP | 4 | `CLIP > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| ripstein (*double swirl*) | 2 × SAME OUT | `>>` **major** | CLIP | 4 | `CLIP >> SAME OUT [DEX] >> SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| triple_swirl (*cardinal swirl*) | 3 × SAME OUT | `>` continuous | CLIP | 5 | `CLIP > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]` |
| fairy_swirling_swirl | 3 × SAME OUT | `>` | TOE (fairy) | 5 | `TOE > SAME OUT [DEX] ×3 > SAME CLIP …` |

**[Derived] Finding.** Repetition **is** literal repetition of the SAME-OUT base
cell with a dex-count increase — the leg and direction do **not** alternate. But
the two-circle rung **forks in boundary encoding, not count**: the stored formulas
contain the same ordered dex labels but differ in connector strength —
`swirling_swirl` chains its circles with a continuous `>`, while `ripstein` (whose
alias is literally *double swirl*) writes **major boundaries `>>`** between them.
This establishes a **represented sequencing distinction**. Its exact physical
interpretation — timing, plant state, rhythm, or another transition property —
requires separate evidence and is not defined here. `ripstein` also stores **no
`base_trick`** and `trick_family = ripstein` — a named row, where `swirling_swirl`
is `base_trick = swirl`, `modifier = swirling`: the same movement pair recorded on
two representation layers (§10).

## 6. [Derived] Operator insertion — what "Swirling" and "Whirling" contribute

Reading every row where the operator leads, event by event:

**Swirling** (the inserted event, first dex unless noted):

| row | inserted event | leg | dir |
|---|---|---|---|
| swirling_swirl | SAME OUT | SAME | OUT |
| swirling_whirl | SAME OUT | SAME | OUT |
| swirling_butterfly | SAME OUT | SAME | OUT |
| swirling_reverse_swirl | **OP OUT** | **OP** | OUT |

**Whirling** (the inserted event):

| row | inserted event | leg | dir | form |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| whirling_swirl | OP IN | OP | IN | plain |
| toe_whirling_swirl | **OP FRONT WHIRL** | OP | (front) | **rotational path** |
| symposium_reverse_whirling_swirl | **OP BACK WHIRL** | OP | (back) | **rotational path** |

**[Derived] Finding.** Neither operator is literal across the corpus:

- "Swirling" adds a **SAME-OUT** dex in three of four leading rows, but an **OP-OUT**
  dex in `swirling_reverse_swirl`. Under the relational-coordinate caveat, this is
  either a leg-coordinate change relative to the following reverse-swirl base or an
  authoring difference — A2 does not decide. The public "swirling = adds a swirl"
  gloss holds only for the SAME-OUT majority as written.
- "Whirling" is written as a plain **OP-IN** dex in some rows and as a **rotational
  FRONT/BACK path dex** in others (toe_whirling_swirl, symposium_reverse_whirling_swirl).
  These are not the same event under leg × direction — the rotational form carries a
  front/back distinction the plain form drops (§11). So "whirling" spans two
  encodings.
- Terminal preservation: in every case the operator preserves the **base terminal**
  (the last base word's clip side). The operators transform the entry and the dex
  sequence, not the terminal identity — the same result A1 found for osis.

## 7. [Derived] Whirling Swirl versus Swirling Whirl (central case)

**Method note (read first).** This comparison is over **stored relational tokens**.
A2 does not normalise each event into persistent physical-leg identities across
plants, support changes, or body rotation. `SAME`/`OP` are relational to the state
at each event, not fixed left/right labels. Any apparent `SAME`↔`OP` mutation after
an earlier event is therefore **unresolved** here until a state-transition model is
applied.

Re-derived from the stored formulas, not the names:

- `whirling_swirl` = `CLIP > OP IN [DEX] > OP OUT [DEX] > SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`
  (base swirl, family swirl, modifier whirling)
- `swirling_whirl` = `SET > SAME OUT [DEX] > OP IN [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`
  (base whirl, family whirl, modifier swirling)

| property | Whirling Swirl | Swirling Whirl |
|---|---|---|
| entry | CLIP | SET |
| first dex leg | OP | SAME |
| first dex direction | IN | OUT |
| second dex leg | OP | OP |
| second dex direction | OUT | IN |
| plant / support transition | continuous `>` | continuous `>` |
| terminal | SAME CLIP [XBD] [DEL] | OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL] |
| ADD | 4 | 4 |
| stored family | swirl | whirl |
| base_trick | swirl | whirl |
| modifier links | whirling | swirling |

**Answers.**

- **Bytewise reversal under the stored coordinates?** *No.* Swirling Whirl's dexes
  are [SAME OUT, OP IN]; Whirling Swirl's are [OP IN, OP OUT]. A token-reversal of
  Swirling Whirl would be [OP IN, SAME OUT] — but the stored Whirling Swirl second
  dex is **OP** OUT, not **SAME** OUT. The first dexes match; the second dex's
  **leg label differs**.
- **Physical reversal after normalizing the changing support/reference frame?**
  *Unresolved.* Because `SAME`/`OP` are relational, the SAME-OUT-versus-OP-OUT
  difference could be a genuine reference-frame transformation after the first
  dexterity, inconsistent authoring, or an artifact of reading locally relative
  coordinates as globally stable. A2 cannot distinguish these.
- **Does either name misdescribe its formula?** Neither is a plain "swap the two
  base dexes." Both are honest about the **base/terminal** (last word), and both
  actually contain a leading operator dex, so the names are directionally right —
  but they do **not** encode a symmetric operator-order token swap.
- **True order-sensitive compositions?** *Yes.* Order changes the stored terminal
  side (OP vs SAME clip), the entry, and the second dex's leg label — order is
  load-bearing in the stored representation.
- **Would swapping operator order predict the other row?** *Not exactly.* Swapping
  predicts the correct base/terminal but the wrong second-dex leg label (SAME-OUT
  predicted, OP-OUT stored). The prediction fails on one coordinate.
- **Do additional rows occupy the predicted cells?** Yes — both spawn deep
  lineages: `whirling_swirl` has `gyro_/spinning_/stepping_/symposium_/pogo_/
  paradox_/blurry_/tapping_/swirling_whirling_swirl` and more; `swirling_whirl` has
  `swirling_symposium_whirl` and the paradox-symposium-whirl chain. Note the
  authoring split within the whirling_swirl lineage: `toe_whirling_swirl` writes the
  whirling event as the rotational `OP FRONT WHIRL`, while plain `whirling_swirl`
  writes `OP IN` — the same named operator, two encodings.

**Strongest conclusion.** Whirling Swirl and Swirling Whirl are **distinct,
order-sensitive stored compositions.** Their formulas are **not** related by a
simple reversal of the written SAME/OP and IN/OUT tokens: the predicted second-dex
leg label differs. This may represent a genuine reference-frame transformation
after the first dexterity, inconsistent authoring, or a limitation of treating
locally relative coordinates as globally stable — **A2 cannot distinguish those
explanations.** What is firm: the stored terminal is set by the last word, the
leading dex by the operator, and the two rows are not a clean token swap. Which of
the three explanations holds is an A3 (path/frame) and A4 (operator-order) task.

the swirl motion feeds an unusual-surface terminal (`DRAGON [UNS] [DEL]`) instead of
the clipper — the "swirl" there names the entry circle, not a swirl terminal.

## 10. [Observed] Formula collisions, near-collisions, and representation layers

- **Boundary-only near-collision:** `swirling_swirl` (`>`) vs `ripstein` / *double
  swirl* (`>>`) — identical dex label pair, different connector strength. Recorded,
  not merged; physical interpretation open (§5).
- **Same-cell namesakes:** `ripstein ← double swirl`; `triple_swirl ← cardinal
  swirl`; `rev_swirl ← reverse swirl`. The alias layer carries the descriptive
  name; the canonical row carries the formula.
- **Operator-order names, non-symmetric token formulas:** `whirling_swirl` vs
  `swirling_whirl` (§7) — names imply a swap, stored tokens are not a clean swap.
- **Within-name encoding split:** `whirling_swirl` (`OP IN`) vs `toe_whirling_swirl`
  (`OP FRONT WHIRL`) — the same named operator, two notations (plain vs rotational).
- **Representation-layer divergence (the A1 pattern):** the swirl lineage records
  operators **more uniformly** than osis — `swirling_swirl`, `whirling_swirl`, and
  `swirling_whirl` all carry proper `modifier_links` (swirling / whirling). The
  exceptions are `ripstein` (a **named** row, no `base_trick`, operator reading only
  in the *double swirl* alias) and `twirl` (stored `base_trick = osis`, §11) — so
  the "structure lives only in a name/alias, not in machine links" gap A1 found for
  torque/blender/flux recurs here specifically at **ripstein and twirl**.
- **Missing / invalid cells:** the base matrix is full. Above it, an apparent gap is
  a bare *reverse-whirling-swirl* single-word row — there is no such canonical; the
  reverse-whirling reading appears only inside compounds
  (`symposium_reverse_whirling_swirl`). Whether that cell is unnamed-but-valid or
  grammatically absent is an open lattice question, not resolved here.

## 11. [Observed → Derived] Twirl / path-vocabulary boundary (hand to A3)

Rows that carry a front/back **path token** the leg × direction axes cannot express:

| row | path-bearing event | stored base / family |
|---|---|---|
| twirl | `SAME FRONT SWIRL [DEX]` → `(back) SPIN [BOD]` | **osis** / osis |
| reverse_whirling_twirl | `OP FRONT SWIRL [DEX] > OP BACK WHIRL [DEX]` | twirl / osis |
| toe_whirling_swirl | `OP FRONT WHIRL [DEX]` | swirl / swirl |
| symposium_reverse_whirling_swirl | `OP BACK WHIRL [DEX]` | swirl / swirl |
| fender | `OP FRONT WHIRL [DEX]` | blender / osis |
| blink | `OP BACK SWIRL [DEX] > OP TOE [DEL]` | — (ADD 2, documented exception) |

**[Derived] Findings for A3.**

- **Twirl is modeled as an osis-base compound in v1.2.** It stores `base_trick =
  osis`: a rotational path dex followed by an osis-like **spin-and-clipper terminal**
  (`(back) SPIN [BOD] > OP CLIP`). That is strong evidence for osis-lineage treatment
  **in the current model**; it does not by itself settle Twirl's physical or
  historical identity. As A1 showed, `base_trick`, aliases, names, and structural
  readings can diverge, so one metadata relationship is not final movement doctrine.
- **What collapses under leg × direction.** A `FRONT SWIRL` / `BACK WHIRL` /
  `FRONT WHIRL` / `BACK SWIRL` event, reduced to bare `<leg> <IN/OUT>`, loses a
  **front/back path distinction** retained by the legacy rotational-dex vocabulary.
  A3 must determine precisely whether that distinction refers to anterior/posterior
  passage, rotational phase, support-leg topology, or another physical property.
  Under such a reduction, distinct rotational dexes would become byte-identical to
  each other and to plain IN/OUT dexes, which is why the Notation paper keeps the
  rotational dex in its own register [Ruled].
- **What information is lost.** The point A2 establishes is only that reducing the
  token to leg × IN/OUT **loses information**; the exact missing physical property
  remains open. For `blink`, the retained `BACK SWIRL` path is preserved by the
  documented exception precisely because IN/OUT cannot carry it.
- **Questions handed to A3.** (1) Is the front/back distinction an independent axis
  or a property of specific rotational dexes only, and what physical property is it?
  (2) Does `twirl`'s osis membership mean the "twirl" path belongs to the osis engine
  rather than the swirl base, or is the metadata diverging from the movement?
  (3) Which path-token rows are genuinely distinct movements and which are legacy
  spellings of an existing leg × direction cell?

## 12. Open questions and proposed A3 inputs

**Open (research, no ruling):**

1. Does a **reference-frame shift after the first dexterity** explain the
   SAME-OUT → OP-OUT divergence between Swirling Whirl and Whirling Swirl (and the
   swirling reverse-pair), or is it authoring drift, or a coordinate-stability
   artifact? A state-transition model (A4) is required before any operator-order
   doctrine.
2. Should the two-circle rung's **boundary-encoding fork** (swirling_swirl `>` vs
   ripstein `>>`) map to a represented physical axis, or is it a naming/sequencing
   distinction only? No ADD moves, and the physical reading is unestablished.
3. Are `ripstein` and `twirl` — the two swirl-lineage rows whose operator reading
   lives only in a name/alias — worth uniform modifier-links, or deliberately held
   as named bases? (Same governance choice A1 raised; do not action here.)

**Proposed A3 inputs (path vocabulary):** the six path-token rows in §11 as the
test set; the front/back path distinction as the candidate missing axis (its
physical meaning to be determined by A3); `twirl`'s osis membership as the sharpest
namesake case; and `blink` as the ruled boundary that already shows IN/OUT cannot
carry the rotational path.

**Proposed A4 inputs (operator order):** the Whirling Swirl / Swirling Whirl table
(§7), the swirling reverse-pair anomaly (§9), the within-name encoding split (plain
OP-IN vs rotational OP-FRONT-WHIRL for "whirling"), and — as the prerequisite — a
state-transition model that normalises the relational `SAME`/`OP` tokens into
persistent physical-leg identities so that "does the coordinate mutate after the
first dex" becomes a decidable question.
