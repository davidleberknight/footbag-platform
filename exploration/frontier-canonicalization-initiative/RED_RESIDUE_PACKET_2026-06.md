## Organizing principle: arithmetic validity ≠ structural certainty

Every item below has **valid arithmetic** — the ADD math closes, and where sources diverge on the number, the divergence resolves under the existing outlier rules. What is **uncertain is the structure**: which operators compose the trick, which base it lands on, whether a token is a real operator at all, and which of several readings is canonical.

This packet asks **only structural / governance questions**, never arithmetic ones. A trick can be a clean 7 ADD and still not be publishable, because the publication contract also requires a *reconciled, non-fabricated structure* and *honest incompleteness*. Holding such a trick is the framework working, not a gap. "Defer" remains a fine answer anywhere.

**Q1a — default disposition.** Confirm the heuristic: a down-family compound earns its own canonical row only if it has a competition record (Scorpion's Tail); the no-records members (Shooting Star, Pixie Double Over Down, Blurrier, Down Double-Down Swirl, …) live as **aliases on their structural decomposition**, not as standalone rows.

**Q1b — Shooting Star structural reconciliation (the real blocker).** The ADD is agreed at 7 (shooting +3 set, prefixing a 4-ADD base; PassBack's 4 is the lone outlier and is non-authoritative). The **structure is not** — the corpus gives four incompatible readings of the same name:

Until the base + the `[PDX]` placement are fixed, Shooting Star stays held (arithmetic-valid, structurally-uncertain).

## Q3 — Blurry redundant-paradox cases

**Background (settled):** pt14 deprecated `blurry` as a productive operator — it is the legacy way of writing a stepping-paradox trick, and "stepping paradox" is now the accurate form.

The open mechanistic case: a few names carry **both** "Blurry" **and** an explicit "Paradox" in the same name. Example:

- **blurry-chainsaw-massacre** = "Blurry Illusioning Paradox Symposium Eggbeater"

- **A. Redundant** — it *is* the stepping-paradox already named by the explicit Paradox; drop "blurry," read as stepping-paradox once (no double-count).
- **B. A separate layer** — blurry contributes its own stepping-paradox on top of the explicit Paradox, so the name carries two paradox-bearing elements.
- **C. Per-name** — depends on the specific construction.
