# Held-out positional foundations audit (2026-06-23)

Read-only analysis of 7 foundational bases. No authoring, no staging. Goal: resolve
the foundational patterns that propagate to the 75 held-out compounds.

Propagation leverage: **42 of the 75 held-out bases terminate in one of these 7 atoms**
(butterfly 10, mirage 9, whirl 8, pickup 6, legover 5, osis 3, clipper 1).

Two tiers emerge: four atoms are notation-resolved (OP); three are genuinely
unresolved in notation (butterfly / clipper / osis).

---

## Tier A — notation-resolved (single OP dex): mirage, whirl, legover, pickup

### mirage  `SET > OP IN [DEX] > OP TOE [DEL]`  (adds 2, toe-led)
1. **Default execution:** an inside dexterity reaching across the body to a far-side toe stall.
2. **SAME/OP:** the single dex is `OP`; terminal is `OP TOE`. Unambiguous.
3. **Qualifier target:** the one dex. A side word modifies that dex (Rule A, trivially).
4. **Evidence:** documented variants same 36 / far 16 — the same-side form is the dominant
   marked deviation, exactly what an OP default predicts (people name the non-default).
5. **Polarity sufficient?** YES for the bare base: OP ⇒ `far-mirage` = alias, `mirage-same-side` = distinct.
6. **Unresolved:** none at the atom. Compounds (`fairy-mirage`, `pixie-mirage`, `nuclear-mirage`)
   are held only because a SAME entry-operator dex precedes the mirage OP dex — see the
   compound-targeting doctrine below.

### whirl  `SET > OP IN [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`  (adds 3, clipper-caught)
1. **Default execution:** a spinning inside dex to a cross-body clipper catch.
2. **SAME/OP:** single dex `OP`; terminal `OP CLIP`. Unambiguous.
3. **Qualifier target:** the one dex.
4. **Evidence:** same 19 / far 17 (near-even at the name-token level, inflated by compounds);
   notation is decisive.
5. **Polarity sufficient?** YES for the bare base (OP).
6. **Unresolved:** none at the atom; same compound-targeting caveat (`fairy-whirl`, `nuclear-whirl`,
   `barraging-whirl`).

### legover  `SET > OP OUT [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]`  (adds 2)
1. **Default execution:** an outside dex (leg passes over) to a same-side toe stall.
2. **SAME/OP:** single dex `OP`; terminal `SAME TOE`. The DEX is OP (the catch is same — see point 3).
3. **Qualifier target:** the **dex** (the only dex), not the catch. The catch is already SAME and
   fixed; the relative-side word toggles the OP dex.
4. **Evidence:** same 20 / far 6 — strong same-side dominance ⇒ corroborates OP-dex default.
5. **Polarity sufficient?** YES for the bare base (OP dex).
6. **Unresolved:** legover is the cleanest case where the dex and the catch are on *different* sides
   (OP dex, SAME catch). Confirms the qualifier must target the DEX, not the catch — a useful
   precedent for the targeting rule. No atom-level open question.

### pickup  `SET > OP IN [DEX] > SAME TOE [DEL]`  (adds 2)
1. **Default execution:** an inside dex to a same-side toe stall.
2. **SAME/OP:** single dex `OP`; terminal `SAME TOE` (same dex/catch split as legover).
3. **Qualifier target:** the dex (OP), not the same-side catch.
4. **Evidence:** same 18 / far 6 — strong same-side dominance ⇒ OP-dex default.
5. **Polarity sufficient?** YES for the bare base.
6. **Unresolved:** none at the atom.

**Tier A finding:** all four are OP at the dex. `far-X` = alias, `X-same-side` = distinct. The
only thing keeping their ~28 descendants held out is the COMPOUND-TARGETING question, not the atom.

## Tier B — notation-unresolved: butterfly, clipper, osis

### butterfly  `SET > SAME/OP OUT [DEX] > OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`  (adds 3)
1. **Default execution:** an outside sweeping dex to a cross-body clipper catch — performed both
   same-side and opposite historically.
2. **SAME/OP:** the dex is explicitly `SAME/OP` (ambiguous IN NOTATION). The terminal is OP CLIP.
3. **Qualifier target:** the single dex — but its default side is undefined, so the word picks one
   of two genuinely-available executions rather than deviating from a default.
4. **Evidence:** same 30 / far 21 — BOTH heavily documented. The corpus treats both as real,
   matching the ambiguous notation. This is not under-documentation; it is genuine two-sidedness.
5. **Polarity sufficient?** NO. There is no single default; neither `far-butterfly` nor
   `butterfly-same-side` is reducible to the base by polarity.
6. **Unresolved doctrine:** does butterfly have a historical default side, or is it side-neutral
   (a true 3-way: base / same-side / far all distinct)? Highest-impact single ruling — clears ~10
   descendants (`quantum/atomic/stepping/barraging/fairy/pixie-butterfly`). NOTE: this is why the
   prior `far-butterfly` "alias" call must NOT stand — butterfly polarity is unsettled.

### clipper  `CLIP [XBD]`  (adds 1, body-category catch surface)
1. **Default execution:** the fundamental cross-body catch — the foot crosses the body line to
   trap the bag on the far side.
2. **SAME/OP:** NO scored dex at all. `[XBD]` (cross-body) is the only token; clipper is a catch
   surface, not a dexterity, so there is no SAME/OP dex marker to read.
3. **Qualifier target:** the **catch** (Rule B), not a dex. A side word describes which side the
   cross-body clip lands on, i.e., it modifies `[XBD]` itself.
4. **Evidence:** same 12 / far 20 — both documented, far slightly ahead. Because `[XBD]` already
   means cross-body, "far clipper" and "same-side clipper" describe the cross relative to the SET,
   not relative to a dex — a different axis than the Tier-A dex toggle.
5. **Polarity sufficient?** NO. Polarity is a dex concept; clipper has no dex. The alias/distinct
   call depends on whether `[XBD]` is inherently opposite-side.
6. **Unresolved doctrine:** does `[XBD]` cross-body encode OP by definition (making
   `same-side-clipper` the marked distinct and `far-clipper` redundant), or is the cross-body axis
   independent of the SAME/OP dex axis? Until ruled, every `*-clipper` whose side word targets the
   clip catch is unresolvable by polarity. (Tier-A `whirl`/`mirage` land ON clipper but via an OP
   dex, so they are unaffected — this is specifically about clipper AS the targeted element.)

### osis  `SET > (back or front) SPIN [BOD] > SAME/OP CLIP [XBD] [DEL]`  (adds 3)
1. **Default execution:** a body spin (back or front) resolving to a cross-body clipper catch.
2. **SAME/OP:** NO dex; the structural element is `SPIN [BOD]`, and the terminal catch is
   explicitly `SAME/OP CLIP` (ambiguous), with the spin able to go back OR front.
3. **Qualifier target:** the **terminal catch** (Rule B), whose side is itself undefined and
   interacts with the spin direction.
4. **Evidence:** same 11 / far 7 — both documented; no dominant deviation.
5. **Polarity sufficient?** NO. Two compounding ambiguities (spin direction + ambiguous catch)
   mean polarity cannot fix the side.
6. **Unresolved doctrine:** osis needs a ruling tying spin direction (back/front) to catch side
   before any `*-osis` side qualifier resolves. Clears ~3 descendants (`barraging/ducking-osis`).

## Synthesis — two doctrine levers that reduce the 75

**Lever 1 — Compound-targeting rule (highest leverage, ~28 descendants).**
For Tier-A atoms (mirage/whirl/legover/pickup) the atom dex is OP and settled; their compounds are
held out only because a SAME-side entry operator (fairy/pixie/nuclear/sailing) prepends a SAME dex,
so the notation has mixed markers. legover/pickup give the precedent: when the dex and catch sit on
different sides, the side qualifier targets the **structural base dex**, not the prepended
entry-operator dex and not the catch. Adopting "the qualifier modifies the base atom's defining dex"
resolves the SAME-entry + OP-base compounds in one ruling.

**Lever 2 — Three atom-level polarity rulings (~14 descendants).**
- **butterfly:** default side, or genuinely 3-way? (~10) — also reopens the earlier `far-butterfly` call.
- **osis:** spin-direction ↔ catch-side relationship. (~3)
- **clipper:** is `[XBD]` cross-body inherently OP? (~1 direct; conceptually underpins every clipper catch)

**Net:** the two levers address ~42 of the 75 held-out bases. The remaining ~33 hang off other
non-unanimous compounds and are out of scope here.
