A short map of where to look on the freestyle surfaces, and where
feedback would be especially useful. Not exhaustive; not prescriptive.
Skim what's interesting; skip what isn't.

## 2. Areas where feedback would be especially valuable

- **Readability of expanded structural readings.** Does
  _stepping ducking paradox illusion_ on avalanche feel informative,
  or like jargon overload?
- **Atom-level vs intermediary expansions.** blur expands to
  _stepping paradox mirage_ rather than _blurry mirage_. Does the
  deeper expansion help or feel forced?
- **Compression ladders.** Mobius walks _gyro torque → spinning
  ss torque → spinning ss miraging op osis_. Is the wow-moment
  present, or does the deepest reading feel inaccessible?
- **Alternate-derivations panel.** flurry and witchdoctor show two
  valid structural paths. Useful framing or noise?
- **Compressed-from line.** The small "Compressed from: pixie mirage"
  one-liner above the JOB on a handful of detail pages — does it
  reinforce the concept or add visual clutter?
- **Pure-alias rendering.** Quantum-mirage shows "Also known as: toe
  blur" simply. Does the alias treatment feel cleanly separated from
  the compositional content?
- **Set Encyclopedia.** Is the surface scannable? Do set names feel
  navigable to someone who doesn't already know the system?
- **Pedagogical flow on the glossary.** Particularly §composition →
  §run-architecture → §advanced. Are the conceptual jumps too steep?

## 3. Known unresolved / intentionally deferred

- Blurry / furious operator doctrine — partial; some compounds carry
  arithmetically clean readings, others remain under curator review.
- Nuclear / tapping family questions — light coverage; not all
  compounds promoted.
- Fairy / spyro modifier-class status — gated on a pending Red ruling.
- Recursive-expansion limits — when a structural reading expands "all
  the way down" vs stops at a useful depth.
- Pedagogical-exemplar selection — which famous compressions deserve
  multi-depth ladders vs single-depth readings.
- Several remaining alias-vs-compression edge cases (dimwalk,
  blurriest, food-processor lineage and similar folk-named compounds)
  — not yet through the same audit as the flagships above.

---

## 4. Things to sanity-check

- Whether any detail pages feel cluttered (too many sections, or
  redundant content in different sections).
- Whether the "Equivalent readings" and "Compressed from" surfaces
  feel duplicative when both render on the same page.
- Whether any structural readings feel unnatural or
  community-grammatically wrong (the kind of thing a player would
  notice that an audit wouldn't).
- Whether any aliases on detail pages look like they belong somewhere
  else (or vice versa).
- Whether the Tier-A ontology pages (mirage, whirl, paradox-mirage)
  feel proportionate to the lighter pages.
