# Emerging Vocabulary system audit (2026-07-14)

Read-only audit of the full Emerging Vocabulary surface (`/freestyle/observational`),
its data path, and every rendered row, against the rebuilt repository, the live
database (951 active canonicals, 374 aliases), and the ruling ledger. The proposed
replacement architecture is in `RESTRUCTURE_PLAN.md` beside this file. The full
row-by-row table (section 7) is generated from the machine join and is also
committed as `ROWS.csv` for filtering.

## 1. Executive finding

The page renders 826 entities (817 universe rows after the live-database filter,
plus 9 database-tracked external rows). Under a fresh row-by-row adjudication,
**489 of the 826 (59%) are not backlog at all**: 383 are resolved lookups (alias
archive plus live identities still rendered as blocked), 17 are operator/set or
notation-term objects mislabelled as trick candidates, 4 are malformed or
adjudicated rejects, 84 are observational history worth keeping but not workable,
and 1 follows a batch already counted elsewhere.

The honest active surface is:

- **25 rows are authorable or one James confirmation away** (the sailing and
  inspinning same-side batches, the registry-defined-operator compounds, the five
  ruled dragons, and POD).
- **28 rows are single-owner James decisions** (27 down-family cell labels and one
  alias-target call).
- **7 rows need evidence** (five mirror-ineligible names and two external folk rows).
- **277 rows are genuinely doctrine-gated, but by only ~14 named questions**, all
  already tracked in the Red queue or the drafted papers. No new question needs to
  be asked; every gated row maps to a question that already exists.

The current nine-heading ladder hides this: it presents 457 rows as seven flavours
of "awaiting", when the true shape is "a small decide-now pile, one evidence pile,
and a dozen doctrine questions with unlock counts".

on 2026-07-14 (the generator now joins the ledger with ledger-wins precedence),
and this audit confirms the fix reaches the whole surface. What remains defective
is listed in section 15: the ledger itself is stale against the operator registry;
the object-type guard is subordinate to ledger precedence; parenthetical folk
names are never resolved against the dictionary; duplicate identities render as
separate rows; and two section headings assert evidence states ("documented
execution", "awaiting doctrine") that the underlying rows contradict.

## 8. Cluster audit findings

### 8.1 Blurry (61 gated rows + 20 twin spellings)

Blurry is registry-defined as a +1 set modifier, and twenty blurry compounds are
already live canonicals (ripwalk, blur, blizzard, bedwetter, blurrage, fog,
blurrier, blurriest, blurry_whirl, blurry_torque, gauntlet, ripped_warrior,
avalanche, spike_hammer, locomotion, symposium_blur, paradox_blur, blurry_drifter,
blurry_symposium_whirl, blurry_whirling_swirl). The gate is therefore NOT "is
blurry defined" and NOT "are these movements unknown": thirteen gated rows carry a
parenthetical that IS a live canonical or alias (Ripwalk, Blur, Blizzard, Bed
Wetter, Blurrage, Locomotion, Ripped Warrior, Golden Shower, Avalanche, Spike
Hammer, Gauntlet, Riptide, plus Blurry Double Legover which resolves to fog), and
seven more are plain-spelling twins of those same rows. For all twenty, the
movement is published; the only open item is the name-form question the Scoring
paper carries: whether a "Blurry X" spelling is an alias of the live canonical or
names a distinct paradox-bearing variant. These rows must display as name
questions on published tricks, never as unresolved vocabulary.

The remaining 40 rows (Blurry Guay, Blurry Pickup, Blurry Clipper, the Blurry
Ducking and Blurry Symposium series without live counterparts, BS Blurriest, BS
Fury, BS Magellan, Alpine Blurry Whirl, Paradox Blurry Whirl, Dragon Blurry

with the answer. "Blurry (Stepping Paradox)" is the bare operator-definition row
and is not a trick. The predicate question is exactly one ask, already sent in
the Scoring paper; either answer mechanically resolves all 60 rows (40 + 20 name
forms).

## 15. Generator and source-of-truth defects

1. **The ledger is stale against the operator registry.** Eleven rows gate on
   "undefined" operators that `trick_modifiers.csv` defines with weights and
   worked examples. There is no reconciliation check between the registry and the
   ledger's blocker tags; the registry is the ruled single authority and must win.
2. **The object-type guard is subordinate to ledger precedence.** Bare
   operator/set/term labels that happen to have ledger rows (Diving, Whirling,
   Swirling, Miraging, Inspinning, Fairy Atomic, Fairy Spinning, Pixie
   Inspinning, Double Down, UNS set, the Dex rows) render as trick candidates.
   The guard fires only on disposition-F rows and only on single-token registry
   names.
3. **Parenthetical folk names are never resolved against the dictionary.** The
   suppression candidates strip parentheticals but never test their CONTENT, so
   sixteen live identities render as blocked rows (section 9).
4. **Duplicate identities render as separate unresolved rows.** Nine groups of
   plain/parenthetical twins (mostly FM + SG spellings of one identity) each
   display twice.
5. **A parser failure class is displayed as a lifecycle heading, with false
   evidence language.** "Documented execution, decomposition unresolved" contains
   zero rows with documented execution; `parse_confidence` and `failure_class`
   are frozen May/June diagnostics, not evidence states.
6. **`doctrine: stable` renders under "Awaiting Doctrine"** on 84 of 93 rows: the
   source CSV's `doctrine_confidence` measures the components' doctrine, while
   the section is driven by the ledger blocker; showing both without distinction
   reads as a contradiction.
7. **The frozen observational CSVs still shape sections and metrics** (section
   assignment, ecosystem, ADD grouping) even where the ledger has moved on;
   they should be provenance/evidence only.
8. **No question registry.** Blockers are free-text subtype strings; unlock
   counts, question wording, and paper routing are re-derived by hand in every
   audit. `RESTRUCTURE_PLAN.md` proposes the registry.
