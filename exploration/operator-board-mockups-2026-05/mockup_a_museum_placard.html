The operators of freestyle

A reference primer to the compositional vocabulary

A small set of body actions, combined according to a small set of rules, produces the entire vocabulary of named freestyle tricks. The fourteen operators below are the alphabet of that vocabulary — what a player has at their disposal when a bag goes into the air.

I. Set operators

What sends the bag into the air.

PIX

Pixie

A compressed uptime set in which the supporting leg drives the bag upward through a tight orbit.

PIX + BUTTERFLY → DIMWALK

AT

Atomic

A set wrapped with full-body rotation while the bag is in the air.

AT + OSIS → FLUX

Q

Quantum

A set carrying an added rotation through uptime.

Q + MIRAGE → TOE BLUR

BL

Blender

A set with a low-orbit pre-set sweep that arcs into the dex.

BL + BUTTERFLY → BLENDER BUTTERFLY

FAIRY

Fairy

A set carrying an extra revolution through uptime.

FAIRY + BLUR → DOUBLE FAIRY

STEP

Stepping

The foot relocates between the set and the catch.

STEP + BUTTERFLY → RIPWALK

II. Body operators

What the body does while the bag is up.

SPIN

Spinning

A full-body rotation around the vertical axis during the trick.

SPIN + TORQUE → MOBIUS

GY

Gyro

A body-rotation variant carried on a partial or inverted plane.

GY + BUTTERFLY → GYRO BUTTERFLY

DUCK

Ducking

The body drops under the bag mid-dex.

PIX + DUCK + BUTTERFLY → PHOENIX

PDX

Paradox

A hip pivot inserted between two dexes.

PDX + LEG-OVER → PARADOX LEG-OVER

SYMP

Symposium

An illusion combined with body rotation.

SYMP + ILLUSION → FLAIL

III. Structural concepts

Relationships across the trick.

XDEX

Cross-dex

The leg circles the bag on the opposite side of the body.

XDEX + INSIDE → CLIPPER

SAME

Same-foot

The set foot and the catch foot are the same.

SAME + BUTTERFLY → SAME-FOOT BUTTERFLY

OP

Opposite

The set foot and the catch foot are different. The conventional default.

OP + BUTTERFLY → BUTTERFLY
