## Why this layer exists

After Slices A–N stabilized the IFPA freestyle ontology and Slices O–R
produced curator-triage queues for branch families, chain alignment,
ADD divergences, and missing-move candidates, the **four-layer ontology
rule** remains the load-bearing forever-rule:

  1. Canonical nomenclature (slug + canonical_name)
  2. Symbolic decomposition (chain readings; operator notation)
  3. Glossary pedagogy (curator-authored explanations)
  4. **Embodied movement analogy** ← this layer

Layer 4 is the one that lives in a dancer's body, not in a database.
It is descriptive vocabulary for what the foot, leg, hip, and bag are
doing in real-world coordinates. It is the layer most likely to drift
into folk explanation, the layer most likely to mean different things
to different practitioners, and the layer least appropriate for
mechanical adjudication.

The user's Slice S directive named three seed analogies:

  - pixie ↔ ATW-like
  - fairy ↔ orbit-like
  - atomic ↔ pickup-like

The plan (`RECONCILIATION_AUDIT_PLAN.md §10`) extends this to the five
curator-confirmed Set/Uptime axis members from Slice L1:
**pixie, fairy, atomic, stepping, surging.**

This doc covers those five. Nothing else.

## Discipline

Every entry below contains:

- A one-line **movement-system gloss** quoted verbatim from
  `freestyleMovementSystems.ts` Slice M/N (curator-confirmed authority).
- A short **embodied analogy** paragraph describing the foot/leg/hip
  trajectory and which canonical trick the shape most closely resembles.
- An **explicit observational notice** distinguishing the analogy from
  structural identity.

The analogy is the smallest, most restrained statement that captures
the embodied resemblance. No essays, no tables of variations, no
"compounds list" — those live in the canonical layer.

## 1. `pixie` ↔ ATW-like

**Movement-system gloss (canonical)**:
> *"PIX + base — a compressed pre-base uptime set; tighter motion than
> stepping. Compounds: PIX + BUTTERFLY (dimwalk), PIX + MIRAGE (smear),
> PIX + DRIFTER (smoke)."*

**Embodied analogy (OBSERVATIONAL)**:
The leg traces a compressed orbital arc around the bag before the base
trick begins. The foot describes a tight, low-radius circle in the
sagittal-plus-lateral envelope — the shape an around-the-world (ATW)
makes, but executed pre-base rather than as a terminating dex. The bag
stays nearly stationary while the foot orbits underneath it. To a
practitioner watching from the side, pixie reads as "ATW compressed
into a set-modifier" — a recognizable shape with a different
structural role.

**Resemblance**: around-the-world (ATW); orbit.

**Why this is observational, not canonical**: pixie's canonical
identity is **a pre-base set modifier** (kind=operator per
`freestyleTrickKindOverrides.ts`). The Red-locked chain readings
(dimwalk = pixie butterfly; smoke = pixie drifter; smudge = pixie
illusion; pixie-illusion = pixie illusion) define pixie *structurally*
as a set primitive that combines with a base trick. The ATW resemblance
is a **shape**, not an identity. A pixie-butterfly is not "an ATW that
became a butterfly"; it is a butterfly base initiated by a pixie set.

## 2. `fairy` ↔ orbit-like

**Movement-system gloss**: *(no curator-authored gloss in Slice M/N
pilot set; fairy operator boundary is pending Red Wave 2.)*

**Embodied analogy (OBSERVATIONAL)**:
The leg traces a partial-orbit motion before contact — closer to a
horizontal sweep around the bag than to pixie's full compressed arc.
Where pixie completes an ATW-like loop pre-base, fairy describes a
shorter orbital travel: more of an "approach orbit" than a full
revolution. The hip's contribution is greater than in pixie; the foot
spends more time in the lateral envelope than in the sagittal one.

**Resemblance**: orbit (canonical trick — partial leg orbit before
contact).

**Why this is observational, not canonical**: fairy's canonical
identity is the load-bearing **Wave 2 dependency**. Red Wave 2 packet
(sent 2026-05-15) asks specifically about the operator-vs-trick
boundary for fairy. Slice P found Wave 2 references in 4 candidate
rows; Slice Q found 4 governing-rule-resolved rows tied to fairy
vocabulary; Slice R found 4 hidden-branch-family candidates using
fairy. Until Wave 2 lands, the embodied analogy here MUST stay
purely descriptive. **No future content-module addition for fairy
should be authored from this analogy alone** — wait on Red.

## 3. `atomic` ↔ pickup-like

**Movement-system gloss (canonical)**:
> *(from the pre-Slice-N Component view's body-mechanics map)*
> *"A cross-body uptime set with x-dex character."*

**Embodied analogy (OBSERVATIONAL)**:
The bag is initiated by a cross-body foot motion that arrives from one
side of the body and "picks up" the bag toward the opposite side. The
foot's trajectory resembles the canonical pickup primitive: the leg
sweeps under and across, with the bag rising along that crossed
path. The X-dex character (per Red 2026-05-15 ruling on Atom Smasher
preserving X-dex from a toe) is the structural correlate of this
embodied "pickup-shape" — the bag literally crosses the body's
midline during the set, the way pickup crosses it on the way up.

**Resemblance**: pickup (canonical 2-ADD set primitive); also visually
adjacent to the cross-body grasp in atom-smasher = atomic-mirage and
eggbeater = atomic-legover (both pt4-locked).

**Why this is observational, not canonical**: atomic's canonical
identity is a **+1 non-rotational / +2 rotational set modifier**. The
pickup resemblance describes the bag's trajectory through the cross-
body envelope, not atomic's structural role. The ADD math (atomic
adds +1 or +2 depending on the base's rotational class per pt4) is
the canonical authority; the pickup analogy is a pedagogical hook.
**If a future glossary section wants to use this analogy, it must
state the analogy IS the pedagogy and not the structure.**

## 4. `stepping` ↔ walking-step-like

**Movement-system gloss (canonical)**:
> *"STEP + base — a foot relocation during uptime that compresses or
> lengthens the set. Per pt11: blurry = stepping paradox; compounds:
> STEP + WHIRL, STEP + OSIS."*

**Embodied analogy (OBSERVATIONAL)**:
The setting foot takes a literal step — a partial relocation along
the ground — between the bag release and the base trick. The motion
is the same gait-cycle micro-step a walker makes when adjusting
foot placement: weight shifts, foot replants, then the base trick
launches from the new position. Where pixie compresses the foot's
trajectory in mid-air, stepping displaces the foot's *ground* position
before the trick begins. The motion is recognizable to anyone who
walks: a single half-step.

**Resemblance**: the walking-family progression (butterfly →
walks: dim-walk, rip-walk, big-walk, etc.). The "walk" naming of
this progression is, in part, an echo of stepping's embodied
analogy. Slice I's walking-family invariant (`leggy in dex >
ss clipper`) is the *structural* identity of that family; stepping
contributes the *temporal* "step before the base" feel.

**Why this is observational, not canonical**: stepping's canonical
identity is **a +1 set modifier on non-rotational bases** (pt11-locked
via blurry = stepping paradox). The walking-step analogy describes
the foot's relocation phase, not the modifier's structural role.

## 5. `surging` ↔ wave-like-forward-thrust

**Movement-system gloss**: *(no curator-authored gloss in Slice M/N
pilot set; surging is in `UNRESOLVED_COMPOUNDS` per Slice M.)*

**Embodied analogy (OBSERVATIONAL — high-uncertainty)**:
The motion has a forward-wave quality — body weight transfers through
the set as if propelling the bag along a horizontal current. Where
pixie compresses the foot's pre-base trajectory and stepping
relocates the foot pre-base, surging *adds momentum* through the set
phase. The bag's apparent travel is slightly more horizontal than in
pixie or stepping. This is the least visually distinctive of the five
set primitives; the embodied analogy is correspondingly the most
uncertain.

**Resemblance**: not strongly resemblant to any canonical primitive.
Loosely analogous to the "surge" intuition (forward-flow), which is
why the row name persists.

**Why this is observational, not canonical**: surging is in the
`UNRESOLVED_COMPOUNDS` allow-list (Slice M). Its structural role is
explicitly curator-deferred. Slice P found `surreal = surging paradox
whirl` divergent with PB (`Surging far Whirl`). Slice Q flags surging
as a pt12-related operator-definition deferral. **Until Red Wave 2
rules on the operator-vs-trick boundary for surging, this analogy is
the least load-bearing entry in this document and the most likely to
shift on curator review.**
